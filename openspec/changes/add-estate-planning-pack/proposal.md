# add-estate-planning-pack

## Why

Estate instruments are the most rule-shaped documents in law — formalities, recitals, and arithmetic — and the highest-stakes place to be mechanically wrong: a missing attestation recital or a residuary clause that doesn't dispose of everything sends property through intestacy. Vaulytica already has the scaffolding: the N-trust-estate sub-domain, `last-will-and-testament` and `revocable-living-trust` playbooks, and 60 EST-### rules gated to `EST_PLAYBOOK_IDS` (`src/engine/rules/v4/trust-estate/`). What's missing is depth on wills specifically: execution-formality recital checks with *accurate* state overlays, and share arithmetic. The overlay data must encode the verified reality, which defeats folk wisdom: **no state requires more than two attesting witnesses** — but Pennsylvania requires none for an ordinary signed will (20 Pa. C.S. § 2502), Louisiana's notarial testament needs two witnesses *plus* a notary with per-page signatures (La. Civ. Code arts. 1576–1577), UPC § 2-502(a) accepts two witnesses signing "within a reasonable time" *or* notarized acknowledgment, and only Colorado and North Dakota adopted that notarization alternative (C.R.S. § 15-11-502(1)(c)(II); N.D.C.C. § 30.1-08-02). Roughly 27 states recognize holographic wills; electronic-will regimes are a separate, fast-moving track (8 UEWA-uniform jurisdictions; ~16 states + DC in total after New York's December 2025 act). A tool that gets this right per state — and says loudly that it checks *recitals*, never *valid execution* — is genuinely useful; one that hardcodes "two witnesses everywhere" is malpractice bait.

## What Changes

- **Execution-formality recital rules** (new EST-1xx range, gated to the will/codicil playbooks): attestation clause present and reciting the witness count; self-proving affidavit present (UPC § 2-504 pattern); notary block present; testator signature block; witness signature blocks matching the attestation recital's count. Findings are worded as presence-of-recital observations.
- **State formalities overlay** (DKB data, one node per state with citations + `retrieved_at`): expected attestation pattern per state — seeded with the verified corrections above (PA zero-witness path, LA notarial testament, CO/ND notarization alternative, UPC "reasonable time" jurisdictions), holographic-recognition flag, e-will-regime flag (flag only; e-will validation is out of scope). With no state asserted (`--state` / tab picker), only jurisdiction-neutral recital checks run.
- **Share arithmetic** (EST-2xx): percentage/fraction extraction (extending `src/extract/amounts.ts` with percent and word-fraction forms — "one-half (1/2)"); residuary shares that sum to less or more than 100% → warning citing UPC § 2-604(b) and § 2-101(a) (unallocated residue passes by intestacy); per-beneficiary lists with the computed total shown.
- **Fiduciary and survivorship presence checks** (EST-3xx): executor/trustee named; successor fiduciary named; guardian nomination present when the document references minor children; survivorship/simultaneous-death provision present; anti-lapse awareness note when a gift names a beneficiary with no alternate.
- **Scope-of-review**: the pack checks recitals, arithmetic, and internal consistency; it does NOT determine that a will is validly executed, that witnesses were competent or present, or that the instrument is effective in any jurisdiction.

## Impact

- Affected specs: `estate-planning` (new capability spec)
- Affected code: `src/engine/rules/v4/trust-estate/` (new gated rules), `src/extract/amounts.ts` (percent/fraction forms — additive fields only), DKB state-formalities nodes + build, `--state` flag reuse, tests; playbook additions (`codicil`)
- Risk: amounts-extractor extension touches a hashed path for all documents — new fields must be additive and absent-when-unmatched so existing goldens stay byte-identical (pinned by regression test); everything else is gated.
