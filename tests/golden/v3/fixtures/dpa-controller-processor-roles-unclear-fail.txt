Data Processing Agreement (Customer to Vendor)

This Data Processing Agreement ("DPA") is entered into as of February 1, 2026, pursuant to Article 28 of the General Data Protection Regulation (EU) 2016/679 ("GDPR"), between Globex Inc., a German GmbH ("Customer"), and Wayne Enterprises LLC, a Delaware limited liability company ("Vendor"). The parties acknowledge that the roles of "Customer" and "Vendor" are intended as commercial labels reflecting the operational relationship between the parties, without incorporation by reference of the GDPR Article 4(7)/(8) defined-term framework or any controlled vocabulary of regulator-defined party designations used elsewhere in the regulation. This DPA supplements and is incorporated into the Master Services Agreement between the parties dated January 1, 2026 (the "MSA").

1. Subject-Matter, Duration, Nature, and Purpose of Processing. The subject-matter of the processing is the provision of cloud-based analytics services as described in the MSA. The duration of the processing corresponds to the term of the MSA. The nature and purpose of the processing is to analyze Customer's customer engagement data for reporting and optimization as instructed by Customer. Processing shall cease upon termination of the MSA.

2. Categories of Personal Data and Data Subjects. The Vendor shall handle the following types of personal data: names, email addresses, IP addresses, device identifiers, and behavioral analytics data. The categories of data subjects are Customer's end-users and prospective customers. A complete list is set forth in Annex I to this DPA.

3. Rights and Obligations of the Customer. Customer is responsible for ensuring the lawfulness of the processing, obtaining necessary consents, and responding to data subject requests. Customer shall provide Vendor with documented instructions regarding the handling of personal data.

4. Documented Instructions Only. Vendor shall handle personal data only on documented instructions from Customer, including with regard to transfers of personal data to a third country or an international organization, unless required to do so by applicable law; in such a case, Vendor shall inform Customer of that legal requirement before processing, unless that law prohibits such information on important grounds of public interest. Vendor shall immediately notify Customer if, in Vendor's reasonable opinion, any instruction from Customer infringes the GDPR or other applicable data protection law.

5. Confidentiality of Personnel. Vendor shall ensure that persons authorized to handle personal data on behalf of Customer have committed themselves to confidentiality or are under an appropriate statutory obligation of confidentiality.

6. Security Measures (Article 32 GDPR). Vendor shall implement and maintain appropriate technical and organizational measures to ensure a level of security appropriate to the risk, including as appropriate: (a) encryption of personal data in transit and at rest; (b) pseudonymization of personal data where possible; (c) ongoing confidentiality, integrity, availability, and resilience of handling systems and services; (d) the ability to restore availability and access to personal data in a timely manner in the event of a physical or technical incident; (e) a process for regularly testing, assessing, and evaluating the effectiveness of technical and organizational measures for ensuring the security of the handling.

7. Subcontractors. Vendor shall not engage any subcontractor that handles personal data without prior specific or general written authorization of Customer. Where Vendor relies on general written authorization, Vendor shall inform Customer of any intended changes concerning the addition or replacement of subcontractors at least thirty (30) days prior to such change, thereby giving Customer the opportunity to object to such changes. Vendor shall impose equivalent data protection obligations on any subcontractor by way of a written contract, and shall remain fully liable to Customer for the performance of such subcontractor's obligations.

8. Assistance with Data Subject Rights. Vendor shall assist Customer by appropriate technical and organizational measures, insofar as this is possible, for the fulfilment of Customer's obligation to respond to requests for exercising data subject rights under Chapter III of the GDPR, including rights of access, rectification, erasure, restriction, portability, and objection.

9. Assistance with Articles 32 to 36 Obligations. Vendor shall assist Customer in ensuring compliance with the obligations under Articles 32 to 36 of the GDPR, taking into account the nature of the handling and the information available to Vendor, including obligations relating to security, breach notification, data protection impact assessments, and prior consultation with supervisory authorities.

10. Breach Notification (Article 33 GDPR). In the event of a personal data breach, Vendor shall notify Customer without undue delay after becoming aware of the breach, and in any event within 48 hours, providing Customer with sufficient information to allow it to meet its obligations to notify the competent supervisory authority within 72 hours and, where required, affected data subjects.

11. Audit and Inspection Rights. Vendor shall make available to Customer all information necessary to demonstrate compliance with the obligations set out in this DPA, and shall allow for and contribute to audits, including inspections, conducted by Customer or an auditor mandated by Customer, subject to reasonable prior written notice and agreed confidentiality obligations.

12. Deletion or Return at End of Services. Upon termination of the MSA for any reason, Vendor shall, at the choice of Customer, delete or return all personal data to Customer and delete existing copies, unless applicable law requires storage of the personal data. Vendor shall certify such deletion in writing within thirty (30) days of termination.

13. Cross-Border Transfers and EU SCCs. Any transfer of personal data to a third country outside the European Economic Area shall be governed by the Standard Contractual Clauses adopted by the European Commission pursuant to Commission Implementing Decision (EU) 2021/914, Module Two, which are hereby incorporated by reference and form part of this DPA.

Annex I — Categories of Personal Data
The following categories of personal data are subject to this DPA: (A) identification data: name, username, email address; (B) technical data: IP address, browser type, device identifiers, session tokens; (C) behavioral data: click-stream events, feature usage logs, session duration metrics.
