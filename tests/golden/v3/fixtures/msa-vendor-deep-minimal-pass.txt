Master Services Agreement

This Master Services Agreement ("MSA" or "Agreement") is entered into as of March 1, 2026, between Acme Corp, a Delaware corporation ("Vendor"), and Wayne Enterprises LLC, a Delaware limited liability company ("Customer"). This Agreement governs all services Vendor provides to Customer pursuant to one or more Statements of Work.

1. Order of Precedence. In the event of any conflict or inconsistency between this MSA and any Statement of Work ("SOW"), the terms of this MSA shall control and govern, except to the extent that a SOW explicitly states that it supersedes a specific provision of this MSA by reference to the section number.

2. Services. Vendor shall provide the services described in each SOW executed by the parties. Each SOW is incorporated into and made a part of this Agreement. Vendor shall perform services in a professional and workmanlike manner consistent with industry standards.

3. Fees and Payment. Customer shall pay Vendor the fees set forth in each applicable SOW. Payment is due within thirty (30) days of Customer's receipt of Vendor's invoice (Net 30). Overdue amounts shall accrue interest at the lesser of 1.5% per month or the maximum rate permitted by law.

4. Intellectual Property Ownership. Vendor retains all right, title, and interest in and to its pre-existing intellectual property, including all software, tools, methodologies, and know-how ("Vendor IP"), whether or not incorporated into any deliverable. Subject to Customer's timely payment of all fees, Vendor hereby grants Customer a non-exclusive, non-transferable, worldwide license to use the deliverables described in each SOW solely for Customer's internal business purposes. Customer retains all right, title, and interest in and to Customer's pre-existing data and materials.

5. Confidentiality. Each party (as "Receiving Party") shall hold in strict confidence all non-public information of the other party (as "Disclosing Party") designated as confidential or that reasonably should be understood to be confidential given the nature of the information and the circumstances of disclosure. Each party shall use the Disclosing Party's confidential information solely to perform its obligations or exercise its rights under this Agreement, and shall disclose such information only to employees and contractors who need to know it and are bound by written confidentiality obligations no less protective than this Agreement. These mutual confidentiality obligations shall survive termination of this Agreement for three (3) years.

6. Limited Warranty. Vendor warrants that the services will conform substantially with the specifications set forth in the applicable SOW for a period of ninety (90) days following delivery ("Warranty Period"). Customer's sole remedy for breach of this warranty is re-performance of the nonconforming services at no additional charge. EXCEPT AS EXPRESSLY SET FORTH IN THIS SECTION, THE SERVICES AND DELIVERABLES ARE PROVIDED "AS IS" AND VENDOR MAKES NO OTHER WARRANTIES, EXPRESS OR IMPLIED, INCLUDING ANY IMPLIED WARRANTIES OF MERCHANTABILITY OR FITNESS FOR A PARTICULAR PURPOSE.

7. Mutual Indemnification. (a) Vendor Indemnification. Vendor shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless Customer and its officers, directors, employees, and agents from and against any third-party claim, action, or proceeding arising out of or relating to any allegation that the deliverables or services, as provided by Vendor and used in accordance with this Agreement, infringe any patent, copyright, trademark, or trade secret of a third party. (b) Customer Indemnification. Customer shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless Vendor and its officers, directors, employees, and agents from and against any third-party claim, action, or proceeding arising out of or relating to Customer's use of the deliverables in violation of this Agreement or applicable law. (c) Indemnification Procedure. The indemnified party shall: (i) promptly notify the indemnifying party in writing of any claim for which indemnification is sought; (ii) give the indemnifying party sole control over the defense and settlement of the claim; and (iii) cooperate fully with the indemnifying party, at the indemnifying party's expense, in the defense and settlement of the claim. The indemnifying party shall not settle any claim in a manner that imposes liability on the indemnified party without the indemnified party's prior written consent.

8. Limitation of Liability. (a) Aggregate Liability Cap. EACH PARTY'S AGGREGATE LIABILITY TO THE OTHER PARTY FOR ALL CLAIMS ARISING OUT OF OR RELATED TO THIS AGREEMENT SHALL NOT EXCEED THE TOTAL FEES PAID OR PAYABLE BY CUSTOMER TO VENDOR IN THE TWELVE (12) MONTHS IMMEDIATELY PRECEDING THE CLAIM. (b) Carveouts. The aggregate liability cap in Section 8(a) shall not apply to: (i) either party's breach of its confidentiality obligations under Section 5; (ii) either party's indemnification obligations under Section 7; or (iii) liability arising from either party's gross negligence or willful misconduct. (c) Exclusion of Consequential Damages. IN NO EVENT SHALL EITHER PARTY BE LIABLE TO THE OTHER PARTY FOR ANY INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL, SPECIAL, EXEMPLARY, OR CONSEQUENTIAL DAMAGES, INCLUDING LOSS OF PROFITS, LOSS OF REVENUE, LOSS OF DATA, OR BUSINESS INTERRUPTION, ARISING OUT OF OR RELATED TO THIS AGREEMENT, REGARDLESS OF THE THEORY OF LIABILITY AND EVEN IF SUCH PARTY HAS BEEN ADVISED OF THE POSSIBILITY OF SUCH DAMAGES. THIS MUTUAL EXCLUSION OF CONSEQUENTIAL DAMAGES SHALL NOT APPLY TO BREACHES OF CONFIDENTIALITY OR INDEMNIFICATION OBLIGATIONS.

9. Insurance. During the term of this Agreement, Vendor shall maintain at its own expense: (a) commercial general liability insurance with limits of at least One Million Dollars ($1,000,000) per occurrence and Two Million Dollars ($2,000,000) in the aggregate; (b) professional liability / errors and omissions insurance with limits of at least One Million Dollars ($1,000,000) per occurrence. Upon request, Vendor shall provide Customer with certificates of insurance evidencing such coverage.

10. Termination. (a) Termination for Cause. Either party may terminate this Agreement upon written notice if the other party materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receiving written notice specifying the breach in reasonable detail. (b) Termination for Convenience. Either party may terminate this Agreement without cause upon sixty (60) days prior written notice to the other party.

11. Governing Law; Venue. This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of Delaware, without regard to its conflict-of-laws principles. Any dispute arising under this Agreement shall be resolved exclusively in the state or federal courts located in Wilmington, Delaware, and the parties hereby consent to the personal jurisdiction of such courts.

12. Entire Agreement. This Agreement, together with all SOWs executed hereunder, constitutes the entire agreement between the parties with respect to the subject matter hereof and supersedes all prior negotiations, representations, or agreements.
