Master Services Agreement (Customer Form)

This Master Services Agreement ("Agreement" or "MSA") is entered into as of March 1, 2026, between Acme Corp, a Delaware corporation ("Customer"), and Globex Solutions Inc., a California corporation ("Vendor"). This Agreement governs all services Vendor provides to Customer pursuant to one or more Statements of Work.

1. Order of Precedence. In the event of any conflict or inconsistency between this MSA, any Statement of Work ("SOW"), or any Data Processing Agreement ("DPA") executed by the parties, the terms of this MSA shall control and govern in all respects, except to the extent that a SOW or DPA explicitly states that it supersedes a specific provision of this MSA by reference to the applicable section number. No pre-printed or standard terms appearing in any Vendor invoice, purchase order, or similar document shall modify or supplement this Agreement.

2. Services. Vendor shall provide the services described in each SOW executed by the parties. Each SOW is incorporated into and made a part of this Agreement. Vendor shall perform services in a professional and workmanlike manner consistent with industry standards applicable to the relevant services.

3. Vendor Warranty and Service-Level Credits. Vendor warrants that the services will conform substantially with the specifications set forth in the applicable SOW for the duration of the engagement. Vendor further represents that its services will be provided by personnel with the requisite skill and expertise. In addition, Vendor commits to the service levels described in each SOW. For each calendar month in which Vendor fails to meet the agreed service levels, Customer shall receive a service-level credit equal to five percent (5%) of the monthly fees for that SOW, up to a maximum of twenty-five percent (25%) of the monthly fees. Credits shall be applied to Customer's next invoice.

4. Fees and Payment. Customer shall pay Vendor the fees set forth in each applicable SOW. Payment is due within thirty (30) days of Customer's receipt of Vendor's invoice (Net 30). Customer may withhold payment for any disputed invoice pending good-faith resolution of the dispute.

5. Intellectual Property Ownership. Customer retains all right, title, and interest in and to Customer's pre-existing data, materials, and intellectual property. All deliverables specifically created for Customer under a SOW shall be considered works made for hire to the maximum extent permitted by law; to the extent any deliverable does not qualify as a work made for hire, Vendor hereby assigns all right, title, and interest in such deliverable to Customer. Vendor retains all right, title, and interest in its pre-existing intellectual property, tools, and methodologies ("Vendor Background IP"), and grants Customer a non-exclusive, perpetual, royalty-free license to use Vendor Background IP incorporated into deliverables solely for Customer's internal business purposes.

6. Confidentiality. Each party (as "Receiving Party") shall hold in strict confidence all non-public information of the other party (as "Disclosing Party") designated as confidential or that reasonably should be understood to be confidential. Each party shall use such information solely to perform its obligations or exercise its rights under this Agreement, and shall disclose it only to employees and contractors who need to know and are bound by written confidentiality obligations no less protective than this Agreement. These obligations survive termination of this Agreement for three (3) years; obligations relating to trade secrets shall survive indefinitely.

7. Vendor Indemnification. Vendor shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless Customer and its officers, directors, employees, and agents ("Customer Indemnitees") from and against any third-party claim, suit, or proceeding arising out of or relating to: (a) any allegation that the deliverables or services, as provided by Vendor and used by Customer in accordance with this Agreement, infringe any patent, copyright, trademark, trade secret, or other intellectual property right of a third party; (b) Vendor's breach of this Agreement, including any breach of Vendor's representations, warranties, or data-protection obligations; or (c) the gross negligence or willful misconduct of Vendor or its personnel. Customer shall: (i) promptly notify Vendor in writing of any claim for which indemnification is sought; (ii) give Vendor sole control over the defense and settlement, provided no settlement imposes liability or obligations on Customer without Customer's prior written consent; and (iii) cooperate fully with Vendor, at Vendor's expense. Vendor's indemnification obligations are not subject to the aggregate liability cap in Section 8(a).

8. Limitation of Liability. (a) Aggregate Liability Cap. Each party's aggregate liability to the other party for all claims arising out of or related to this Agreement shall not exceed the greater of: (i) One Million Dollars ($1,000,000); or (ii) the total fees paid or payable by Customer to Vendor in the twelve (12) months immediately preceding the claim giving rise to liability. (b) Carveouts from Cap. The aggregate liability cap in Section 8(a) shall not apply to: (i) Vendor's indemnification obligations under Section 7; (ii) either party's breach of its confidentiality obligations under Section 6; or (iii) liability arising from either party's gross negligence or willful misconduct. (c) Mutual Consequential-Damages Waiver. In no event shall either party be liable to the other party for any indirect, incidental, special, exemplary, or consequential damages, including loss of profits, loss of revenue, loss of data, or business interruption, arising out of or related to this Agreement, regardless of the theory of liability and even if such party has been advised of the possibility of such damages. This mutual exclusion of consequential damages shall not apply to breaches of confidentiality obligations or indemnification obligations.

9. Vendor Insurance. During the term of this Agreement and for two (2) years thereafter, Vendor shall maintain, at its own expense: (a) commercial general liability insurance with limits of at least Two Million Dollars ($2,000,000) per occurrence and Five Million Dollars ($5,000,000) in the aggregate; (b) professional liability / errors and omissions insurance with limits of at least Two Million Dollars ($2,000,000) per occurrence; (c) workers' compensation insurance as required by applicable law. Upon request, Vendor shall provide Customer with certificates of insurance evidencing such coverage, naming Customer as an additional insured on the commercial general liability policy.

10. Termination. Customer may terminate this Agreement for convenience upon thirty (30) days prior written notice to Vendor. In the event of termination for convenience, Customer shall pay Vendor for all services satisfactorily performed through the effective date of termination. Either party may also terminate this Agreement upon written notice if the other party becomes insolvent, makes an assignment for the benefit of creditors, or has a receiver appointed for its business.

11. Data Portability and Return on Termination. Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement for any reason, Vendor shall: (a) promptly provide Customer with a complete, machine-readable export of all Customer Data in a standard, non-proprietary format reasonably selected by Customer; (b) delete or destroy all copies of Customer Data from Vendor's systems within thirty (30) days of the later of the termination date or completion of the data export; and (c) certify such deletion in writing. Vendor's obligation to return Customer Data is not subject to any lien or right of retention.

12. Audit Right. Customer (or its designated auditor) may audit Vendor's compliance with this Agreement, including Vendor's data-protection and security practices, upon thirty (30) days prior written notice, no more than once per calendar year unless a material breach is reasonably suspected. Audits shall be conducted during Vendor's normal business hours and in a manner that does not unreasonably disrupt Vendor's operations. Customer shall bear the cost of the audit unless the audit reveals a material breach, in which case Vendor shall bear all reasonable audit costs.

13. Governing Law; Venue. This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of Delaware, without regard to its conflict-of-laws principles. Any dispute arising under this Agreement shall be resolved exclusively in the state or federal courts located in Wilmington, Delaware, and the parties hereby consent to the exclusive personal jurisdiction of such courts.

14. Entire Agreement. This Agreement, together with all SOWs and DPAs executed hereunder, constitutes the entire agreement between the parties with respect to the subject matter hereof and supersedes all prior negotiations, representations, or agreements.
