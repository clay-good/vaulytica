# Part 0 â€” Intent

## Â§1. Why we're doing this

v36 gave the posture archive its **direction-resolved** concession order: across the deal, when fronts A and B both *fall* below the floor, does one reliably give ground **first**? It is the leading edge of a coming concession â€” the warning a deal lead watches. But the floor is crossed two ways, and v36 reads only the fall. v35 split into nothing; v36 took the *fall* half of the directional precedence. The mirror is unread:

- **Who recovers first.** Cap climbs back above the floor in rounds 2 and 4; Term climbs back in rounds 3 and 5 â€” every comparison, Cap was restored first. Cap leads the recovery: the counterparty repairs Cap ahead of Term.
- **Who recovers _last_.** The same relation, read from the other end: Term recovers last, every time. Term is the front the counterparty leaves below the floor **longest** â€” the standing exposure a recovery-blind read (and even v36's concession order) cannot name. A front that concedes first *and* recovers last is the persistent weak point; a front that concedes first but recovers first is merely volatile.

To v36 these are invisible (it orders falls only). The difference is **directional** â€” the missing recovery half of the same precedence relation, the way v33 completes v32 and v30 completes v28. It matters because the gate-worthy signal here is *asymmetric in the opposite way* from v36: recovering **first** is good news (a front the counterparty restores quickly), so it is not the warning; recovering **last** is the warning (a front left exposed longest), and a consistent last-recoverer is exactly the partner of a consistent first-recoverer. v37 reports both ends of the pairing and gates on the laggard.

This is not a gap in v36 â€” a concession order is supposed to order falls. It is the missing **recovery** dimension of the same ordering relation. v37 supplies it. Read the same per-front recovery-step lists v33 overlaps, on the ordering: for each unordered pair, how many comparisons saw A recover first, how many saw B first, how many were same-step ties, the first-recoverer, its laggard partner, the resulting affinity, the deal's clearest such recovery order, and whether any pair has a front that recovered first for a strict majority of the comparisons (and so a consistent laggard). The gate follows: `--fail-on-lagging-recovery` fires when a pair has a front that _consistently_ recovers last â€” the one ordering boundary whose meaning ("one front is reliably restored after the other, left exposed longest") needs no tuning. All of this is a reduction over the same `floors[]` recoveries v29 counts per step and v33 overlaps per pair.

## Â§2. What v37 is and is not

**It is:**

- A **document-free recovery-order (recovery-precedence) command.** `coherence-recovery-order <r1.coherence.json> â€¦ <rN.coherence.json>` reads N â‰¥ 2 saved coherence artifacts in round order, verifies each, runs the cross-ladder guard across all of them, and prints, per **pair** of fronts with any ordered recovery comparison, how many comparisons saw A recover first (`a_recovers_first`), B first (`b_recovers_first`), the two together (`co_recoveries`), out of all `comparisons`; the pair's `first_recoverer`, its `last_recoverer`, its `affinity`, and its `class` â€” plus the deal's **clearest recovery order** (`max_affinity` / `most_ordered_pair` / `first_recovering_front` / `last_recovering_front`, `null` when no front ever consistently recovered first), the `total_ordered_comparisons` and `total_co_recoveries` (`= Î£_t C(recovering_t, 2)`). Markdown (default) or `--format json`. No documents are read; the engine is never run.
- A **single deal-level ordering gate, on the laggard.** `--fail-on-lagging-recovery` exits 2 when at least one pair of fronts has a front that climbed back at-or-above the floor first for a strict **majority** of the comparisons (`max(a_recovers_first, b_recovers_first) Ã— 2 > comparisons`) â€” which means its *partner* recovers **last** for that same majority. This is the *direction-resolved* mirror of v36's concession gate: where v36 fires on a front that *gives ground* first, this fires on a front left *exposed* longest. The first-recoverer is good news (restored quickly); the warning is its laggard. A pair that recovered first once but recovered together twice, or split its recoveries evenly, is `interleaved` and clears it; a consumer wanting a different bar reads `affinity` and `max_affinity` from the JSON. It needs no tuning â€” a strict majority of the comparisons (ties and reverse-order working against it) *is* "this front is consistently restored last."
- A **whole-sequence-verified input, sharing the v17â€“v36 front end.** Every artifact's `coherence_hash` is re-derived and checked on load (a tampered/corrupt round is a hard error, errors prefixed `round N:`). The v15/v16 cross-ladder guard runs across the sequence via the same `verifyCoherenceSequence` loader the twenty trend/exposure/â€¦/concession commands use, with no change to it.
- A **derived report carrying its own fingerprint.** The report carries a `recovery_order_hash` (a stable SHA-256 over the canonical per-pair set â€” the integer `a_recovers_first`/`b_recovers_first`/`co_recoveries`, the `first_recoverer`, and the class; the derived `last_recoverer`, the float `affinity`, and the derived integer `comparisons` are omitted, so the fingerprint is integer-exact over the inputs), namespaced apart from every prior hash (`coherence_hash`, â€¦, `precedence_hash`, `concession_hash`), so the same N artifacts in the same order always reproduce the same report.

**It is not:**

- **Not a new diff, classifier, predicate, or artifact format.** It reuses the `weakest_tier` binding floor v12 derives, the `below-acceptable` rung v10 defines, and the _recovery_ events v33 defines; v37 is a subcommand plus one pure module (`computeCoherenceRecoveryOrder`) whose only new logic is a per-pair ordered comparison of the recovery-step lists and a strict-majority test. `parsePostureCoherenceJson`, the coherence schema, and every trajectory/exposure/â€¦/concession function are unchanged.
- **Not the concession order command (v36).** v36 orders *falls* only, isolating which front *concedes* first. v37 orders *recoveries* only, isolating which front *recovers* first (and so which recovers last). A pair `leading` on v36 can be `interleaved` on v37 and vice versa; the fall-order and recovery-order are independent reads of the same `floors[]` matrix. A consumer asking "which front gives ground first?" runs v36; one asking "which front is restored last?" runs v37.
- **Not the precedence command (v35).** v35 orders *every* crossing (a fall or a recovery), direction-blind. v36 + v37 are its two direction-resolved halves; a pair `leading` on v35 may be `leading` on one half and `interleaved` on the other, depending on which direction carried the lead.
- **Not the co-recovery affinity command (v33).** v33 reads, per pair, which two fronts recover *together* in one step (the same-step coincidence, a Jaccard overlap of the recovery-step *sets*). v37 reads, per pair, which front recovers *first* across steps (the ordering of the recovery-step *lists*). A pair `coupled` on co-recoveries (v33) can lead, follow, or interleave on recovery order (v37), and a pair with no co-recovery at all can be the *clearest* recovery order â€” the same-step and across-step relations are independent. The link is exact (each pair's `co_recoveries` equals v33's `co_recoveries`).
- **Not the concurrency command (v29).** v29 counts, per step, how many fronts recovered together â€” a per-step scalar. v37 reads, per pair, the *order* of recoveries across the deal â€” a per-pair relation. Their link is the totals: summed over all pairs, v37's `total_co_recoveries` equals `Î£_t C(recovering_t, 2)`, the same per-step recovery count v29 reports.
- **Not a same-step ordering (who recovered earlier *within* one transition).** A transition is atomic â€” two fronts recovering in the same step have no order (a `co_recoveries` tie, which counts *against* a lead). v37's precedence is strictly *between* transitions.
- **Not a configurable "recovers first at least K times" gate.** v37 does not take a `--min-leads K` knob; the gate fires on a strict majority of the comparisons, the one ordering condition whose meaning needs no tuning. (v20â€“v36 made the same call.)
- **Not a minimum-comparison gate.** A pair with one ordered comparison and no ties reads `a_recovers_first` 1 / `comparisons` 1 / `affinity` 1.0 / `leading` â€” honestly, the one comparison the two made, one recovered first.
- **Not a per-front reduction.** Like v33/v36, v37's primary object is an unordered **pair**, not a front. The `first_recoverer`/`last_recoverer` name *which* fronts of the pair recovered first/last more often; they are properties of the pairing, not a per-front rank.
- **Not a recovery-order artifact.** v37 introduces no new on-disk format; the report is _derived_, recomputed on demand from the N auditable coherence inputs.
- **Not a sequence the command discovers.** It takes the artifacts **in round order** on the argv, exactly as the twenty trend/exposure/â€¦ commands do.
- **Not a browser surface.** An N-round pairwise recovery order of uploaded artifacts is a CI/dashboard concern (mirroring v16â€“v36).

## Â§3. The posture filter (unchanged)

1. **Deterministic** â€” `computeCoherenceRecoveryOrder` over N `PostureCoherence` objects is pure: it matches fronts by dimension, pins fronts by `localeCompare(_, "en")` (the same order the trajectory/exposure/affinity/concession functions use), enumerates unordered pairs in that pinned order (`dimension_a < dimension_b`), counts each pair's recover-first/follow/tie comparisons by ordering one front's recovery-step list against the other's, and picks the clearest-order affinity by **integer cross-multiplication** (`leads Ã— comparisons` of the two pairs), never a float comparison. Identical artifacts in identical order â†’ identical `recovery_order_hash` on any machine.
2. **Honest about unstated data** â€” a front no document states in a round does not _recover_ into or out of that round: silence is neither a fall nor a recovery (the same Â§3 contract that keeps `below â†’ unstated â†’ below` zero recoveries in v29/v33). A recovery revealed after a silent gap is attributed to the transition into the round that reveals the new standing â€” never an invented recovery on the silent step. A pair the two never both recovered in a *different* step (no ordered comparison â€” one never recovered, or they always recovered together) has no recovery-order edge and is omitted from `pairs[]`, never ranked.
3. **Advisory** â€” the command names which front the counterparty restored first across the team's own floor, and which it left exposed longest, and how reliably. It asserts no legal conclusion.
4. **No server** â€” N local files in, one report out. No socket, no engine run.
5. **Additive** â€” a brand-new subcommand and one new pure module. Every existing command (`analyze`, `diff`, `compare`, `compare-coherence`, `coherence-trend`, â€¦, `coherence-concession`, `verify`) is byte-for-byte unchanged in _output and goldens_; `coherence-sequence.ts` is imported and reused **without modification** (v37 needs nothing exported that was not already public â€” like v19â€“v36, it touches no existing source file's behavior at all).

# Part I â€” The report (pure)

`src/report/coherence-recovery-order.ts` (a new sibling to `coherence-concession.ts`):

- **`RecoveryOrderClass`** â€” a pair's recovery-order coupling class: `leading` (one front climbed back at-or-above the floor *before* the other for a strict majority of the comparisons â€” `max(a_recovers_first, b_recovers_first) Ã— 2 > comparisons` â€” a consistent first-recoverer, and so a consistent laggard, the gate-worthy class), `interleaved` (the two recovered in mixed order with no strict-majority first-recoverer, including an exact split or a pair whose comparisons are dominated by same-step ties). Only pairs with â‰¥ 1 *ordered* comparison (`a_recovers_first + b_recovers_first â‰¥ 1`) are classified; a pair the two never both recovered in a different step is omitted.
- **`CoherenceRecoveryOrderPair`** â€” one unordered pair's whole-sequence recovery-order relation: the `dimension_a`/`dimension_b` (pinned, `a < b`), the `a_recovers_first`/`b_recovers_first` (comparisons A/B recovered first), the `co_recoveries` (same-step ties), the `comparisons`, the `first_recoverer` (the front that recovered first more often, `null` on an exact split), the `last_recoverer` (the front recovered first *less* often â€” the laggard left exposed longest, `null` on a split), the `affinity` (`max(a_recovers_first, b_recovers_first) / comparisons`), and its `class`.
- **`CoherenceRecoveryOrder`** â€” the whole-sequence reduction: `rounds` (count), `pairs[]` (the recovery-order pairs above, pinned), `class_counts` (the per-pair tally by class), `total_ordered_comparisons` (every pair's ordered comparisons summed), `total_co_recoveries` (every pair's same-step recovery ties summed â€” equal to `Î£_t C(recovering_t, 2)`), `max_affinity` (the clearest recovery order, `null` when no pair has a first-recoverer), `most_ordered_pair` (the `[dimension_a, dimension_b]` owning `max_affinity`, earliest on a tie, `null` when none), `first_recovering_front` (the first-recoverer of that pair, `null` when none), `last_recovering_front` (the laggard of that pair, `null` when none), `lags` (any pair a strict-majority recovery order â€” so a consistent laggard â€” the gate verdict), and `recovery_order_hash`.
- **`computeCoherenceRecoveryOrder(rounds: PostureCoherence[])`** â€” the pure, IO-free core. For each front, derives its recovery-step list (the v33 events, silence-skipping, Â§3); for each unordered pair, orders A's recovery steps against B's into `a_recovers_first`/`b_recovers_first`/`co_recoveries`, computes the comparisons, the affinity, and the strict-majority class; selects the clearest-order affinity by integer cross-multiplication, returning the reduction with a `recovery_order_hash`. Requires â‰¥ 2 rounds.
- **`exposureLags(recoveryOrder)`** â€” the CI gate predicate: `recoveryOrder.lags`. The direction-resolved laggard verdict no other command exposes (v36 gates on the concession order; v35 on a direction-blind ordering; v33 on the same-step recovery coupling; v25/v29 on per-step counts).
- **`buildCoherenceRecoveryOrderJson(recoveryOrder)`** / **`renderCoherenceRecoveryOrderSummary(recoveryOrder)`** â€” the JSON (`schema: vaulytica.posture-recovery-order.v1`) and human-readable renderers. The summary prints the clearest recovery order (the first-recoverer, the laggard it leaves exposed, and its lead share, or none-ordered when no front ever consistently recovered first, plus the class tally), then one line per recovery-order pair (`leading` pairs first), then the hash.

v37 imports only the already-public `PostureCoherence` type, the `NegotiationTier` type, and the shared hashing helpers; the `below-acceptable` literal is a plain string; no existing source file changes.
