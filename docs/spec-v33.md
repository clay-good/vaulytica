# Vaulytica v33 — Document-Free Exposure Co-Recovery Affinity (Pairwise Recovery Coupling)

> **Status:** **Shipped (9.30.0).** v32 took the posture family's first **pairwise** read: for each unordered pair of fronts, how reliably the two _fell_ below floor **together** across the deal (`co_falls` / `union_falls`, a Jaccard `affinity`, a strict-majority `coupled` verdict) — the linked concession the counterparty trades as a block. But a fall is only half the floor crossing. v29 resolved each step into _falls_ **and** _recoveries_; v28/v30 paired the two into episodes; v32 read the pairwise overlap of the **fall** direction only. The mirror direction is unread: across the deal, **which two fronts does the counterparty keep _restoring_ as a block?** A recovery on front A that only ever lands alongside a recovery on front B is a _linked recovery_ — A is hostage to B, repaired as a bundle, never alone. That is strategic information the co-_fall_ view (v32) structurally cannot pose: two deals with byte-identical v32 co-fall affinity (the same fronts conceded together) can have opposite recovery coupling — one where the same pair is _restored_ together every time, another where each recovery is independent. v33 supplies the exact mirror of v32 on the recovery direction (as v30 mirrors v28, v27 mirrors v26): for each unordered pair of fronts, how many round-transitions saw **both** _recover_ in the **same step** (`co_recoveries`), out of the steps in which **either** recovered (`union_recoveries`), the resulting `affinity` (`co_recoveries / union_recoveries` — the Jaccard overlap of the two fronts' recovery-step sets), the deal's **tightest pairing** (`max_affinity` / `tightest_pair`), and — the one tuning-free verdict — whether any pair recovered together for a strict **majority** of the steps either recovered (`coupled`). It continues the global step numbering after v32's Step 212, beginning at **Step 213**. One new subcommand, one pure module, **zero** change to any existing source file.
> **Scope:** one idea — the recovery-direction transpose of v32's co-fall affinity. v32 answers _which two fronts fall below the floor together, and how reliably_; v33 answers _which two fronts recover together, and how reliably_. It reuses v29's exact **recovery** event (below → at-or-above, silence-skipping per §3) — so a co-recovery is two fronts both registering a v29 recovery in the same transition — but where v29 keeps a per-step recovery count (`recovering`) and v32 keeps the per-pair _fall_ overlap, v33 keeps the per-pair _recovery_ overlap. By construction `total_co_recoveries` equals `Σ_t C(recovering_t, 2)` (the sum over v29's per-step recovery counts of "two-from-k"), and `total_recoveries` equals v29's `total_recoveries` (and the recovery half of v24's per-front crossing sum). The use case is the same archive of per-round coherence artifacts: v32 says "across the deal, **Cap and Term** _fell_ together in 3 of the 4 rounds either fell — the counterparty concedes them as a block"; v33 says "and they _recovered_ together in 2 of the 2 rounds either recovered — the counterparty restores them as a block too: you cannot get Cap back without Term."
> **Posture (unchanged, non-negotiable):** deterministic (the affinity is a pure per-pair overlap count over the same `below-acceptable` rung v10 defines, taken over the binding floors v12 already derived and v24/v25/v29/v32 already read; the tightest-pair pick is decided by integer cross-multiplication, never a float compare, so the same artifacts → identical `recovery_affinity_hash`, on any machine, forever), no AI / no probabilistic path, no server (N local files in, one report out; no socket), citable (the artifacts carry the same per-front binding floors v12 derived from each document's own clause and the team's own playbook — v33 adds no new claim), lints / references / positions — but never drafts, and never renders a legal conclusion. The command is **advisory and verifiable**: every artifact is hash-verified (a tampered round is a hard error, side-labeled by round index), and the ladders are checked for a match across the _whole_ sequence (any cross-ladder pair is refused) via the same `verifyCoherenceSequence` loader the sixteen trend/exposure/persistence/breadth/recurrence/volatility/synchrony/settling/onset/latency/concurrency/relapse/tenure/affinity commands use, unchanged. Every step passes the §3 filter or it does not ship.

# Part 0 — Intent

## §1. Why we're doing this

v32 gave the posture archive its first pairwise read: for each unordered pair of fronts, how reliably the two _fell_ below floor **together** across the whole sequence — `co_falls` out of `union_falls`, the Jaccard `affinity`, and the strict-majority `coupled` verdict (a stable concession pairing). Its gate (`--fail-on-coupled-fronts`) fires when two fronts fell together more often than apart — the linkage to exploit on the **concession** side.

But the floor is crossed in two directions, and v32 reads only one. v29 resolved each step into _falls_ and _recoveries_; v28 and v30 paired fall→recovery and recovery→fall into episodes; v32 took the pairwise overlap of the **fall** direction. The mirror is unread: across the sequence, **is it the same two fronts that keep _recovering_ together?** v32 knows nothing about it — a pair can fall together every time (v32 `coupled`) and recover entirely independently, or fall apart yet recover as a block. The recovery coupling is a separate relation:

- **The linked recovery.** Cap and Term recover together in rounds 4 and 6 — every time either climbed back above floor, the other climbed with it. The counterparty restores them as a **block**: you cannot get Cap back without Term. A recovery linkage.
- **The independent recovery.** Round 4 Cap recovered alone, round 5 Term recovered alone, round 6 Fee recovered alone — three recoveries, no pair repeating. The same v29 per-step recovery counts, but **no recovery coupling**: each front was restored on its own.

To v32 these are indistinguishable (v32 reads only falls). The difference is **pairwise on the recovery direction**, the exact mirror of v32 on the fall direction — as v30 mirrors v28's latency on the relapse direction, and v27 mirrors v26's settling on the onset direction. It matters because a stable recovery pairing is _strategic information_: it tells a deal lead which fronts the counterparty treats as a bundle when _conceding back_ — the linkage to break (to free a hostage front) or to lean on (to recover two at the price of one). A one-off independent recovery tells them nothing.

This is not a gap in v32 — a _co-fall_ affinity is supposed to read falls. It is the missing **mirror direction** of the same pairwise reduction. v33 supplies it. Read the same artifacts per-pair on the recovery direction: for each unordered pair of fronts, how many transitions saw _both_ recover (the numerator), how many saw _either_ recover (the denominator), the resulting affinity, the deal's tightest such pairing, and whether any pair recovered together for a strict majority of the steps either recovered. The gate follows: `--fail-on-coupled-recoveries` fires when a pair recovered together _more often than apart_ — the one coupling boundary whose meaning ("they recover together more often than separately") needs no tuning. All of this is a reduction over the same `floors[]` crossings v24 counts, v25 buckets per step, v29 splits by direction, and v32 pairs on the fall direction.

## §2. What v33 is and is not

**It is:**

- A **document-free recovery-affinity command.** `coherence-recovery-affinity <r1.coherence.json> … <rN.coherence.json>` reads N ≥ 2 saved coherence artifacts in round order, verifies each, runs the cross-ladder guard across all of them, and prints, per **pair** of fronts that ever recovered together, how many transitions saw both recover (`co_recoveries`), each front's own recovery count (`a_recoveries` / `b_recoveries`), the count of transitions where either recovered (`union_recoveries = a_recoveries + b_recoveries − co_recoveries`), the resulting `affinity` (`co_recoveries / union_recoveries`), and its affinity `class` — plus the deal's **tightest pairing** (`max_affinity` / `tightest_pair`, `null` when no two fronts ever recovered together), the `total_co_recoveries` (`= Σ_t C(recovering_t, 2)`, v29's per-step recovery counts choose-two'd) and `total_recoveries` (`=` v29's `total_recoveries`). Markdown (default) or `--format json`. No documents are read; the engine is never run.
- A **single deal-level coupling gate.** `--fail-on-coupled-recoveries` exits 2 when at least one pair of fronts recovered together for a strict **majority** of the transitions in which either recovered (`co_recoveries × 2 > union_recoveries`). This is the _recovery-direction_ counterpart to v32's _fall-direction_ gate: where v32 fires on a stable _concession_ pairing, this fires on a stable _restoration_ pairing — two fronts that recovered together more often than they recovered apart, across the deal. A pair that recovered together once but apart twice is `incidental` and clears it; a consumer wanting a different bar reads `affinity` and `max_affinity` from the JSON. It needs no tuning — a strict majority of the union _is_ "they recover together more often than not."
- A **whole-sequence-verified input, sharing the v17–v32 front end.** Every artifact's `coherence_hash` is re-derived and checked on load (a tampered/corrupt round is a hard error, errors prefixed `round N:`). The v15/v16 cross-ladder guard runs across the sequence via the same `verifyCoherenceSequence` loader the sixteen trend/exposure/persistence/breadth/recurrence/volatility/synchrony/settling/onset/latency/concurrency/relapse/tenure/affinity commands use, with no change to it.
- A **derived report carrying its own fingerprint.** The affinity carries a `recovery_affinity_hash` (a stable SHA-256 over the canonical per-pair set — the integer `a_recoveries`/`b_recoveries`/`co_recoveries` and the class; the derived float `affinity` and the derived integer `union_recoveries` are omitted, so the fingerprint is integer-exact over the inputs), namespaced apart from every `coherence_hash`, `movement_hash`, `trajectory_hash`, `shift_trajectory_hash`, `arc_hash`, `exposure_hash`, `persistence_hash`, `breadth_hash`, `recurrence_hash`, `volatility_hash`, `synchrony_hash`, `settling_hash`, `onset_hash`, `latency_hash`, `concurrency_hash`, `relapse_hash`, `tenure_hash`, and `affinity_hash`, so the same N artifacts in the same order always reproduce the same report.

**It is not:**

- **Not a new diff, classifier, predicate, or artifact format.** It reuses the `weakest_tier` binding floor v12 derives, the `below-acceptable` rung v10 defines, and the _recovery_ event v29 defines (below → at-or-above, silence-skipping); v33 is a subcommand plus one pure module (`computeCoherenceRecoveryAffinity`) whose only new logic is a per-pair overlap of the recovery-step sets and a strict-majority test. `parsePostureCoherenceJson`, the coherence schema, and every trajectory/exposure/…/affinity function are unchanged.
- **Not the co-fall affinity command (v32).** v32 reads, per pair, how reliably two fronts _fall_ below floor together — the concession linkage. v33 reads, per pair, how reliably two fronts _recover_ together — the restoration linkage. A pair `coupled` on falls (v32) can be `incidental` on recoveries (v33) and vice versa: the two directions are independent relations over the same `floors[]` matrix, the same way a v28 latency and a v30 relapse interval are independent episodes. A consumer wanting "which fronts concede as a block?" runs v32; one wanting "which fronts are restored as a block?" runs v33; one wanting both runs both and joins on the shared `floors[]` matrix.
- **Not the concurrency command (v29).** v29 counts, per step, how many fronts recovered — a per-step scalar (`recovering`), and it gates on the _fall_ direction (`concerted-fall`). v33 reads, per pair, how reliably two fronts recover _together_ across the deal — a per-pair relation, gated on a pair that recovered more often together than apart. Every co-recovery step _is_ a v29 step with `recovering ≥ 2`, so `coupled ⟹ a concerted-recovery step`; but the reverse fails the way that matters: three different pairs each recovering together once each give v29 three `recovering ≥ 2` steps yet leave every pair `incidental` to v33.
- **Not the synchrony command (v25).** v25 counts, per step, how many fronts _crossed_ the floor together — direction-blind (a fall and a recovery both count). v33 reads only _recoveries_ (the v29 direction), and only the _pairwise_ overlap. A step where one front fell and one recovered is a synchrony of two to v25 and is **not** a co-recovery to v33 (only one front recovered).
- **Not the volatility command (v24).** v24 counts a front's floor _crossings_ (falls and recoveries). v33 counts only _recoveries_, and only their pairwise coincidence.
- **Not a per-front reduction.** Like v32, v33's primary object is an unordered **pair**, not a front. The per-front recovery counts (`a_recoveries`, `b_recoveries`) appear only as the context for the pair's overlap.
- **Not co-restoration of standing (both above floor in the same round, however they got there).** v33's co-recovery is a coincidence of _recovery events_ (the v29 transition), not of standing: two fronts both already above floor through rounds 2–4 without _crossing back_ in the same step do not co-recover (no shared recovery transition). This keeps the lineage to v29's recovery and the join invariant to `Σ C(recovering_t, 2)` exact.
- **Not a configurable "co-recovered at least K times" gate.** v33 does not take a `--min-co-recoveries K` knob; the gate fires on a strict majority of the union (`co_recoveries × 2 > union_recoveries`), the one coupling condition whose meaning needs no tuning. (v20–v32 made the same call.)
- **Not a minimum-union gate.** A pair where both fronts recovered exactly once, in the same step, reads `co_recoveries` 1 / `union_recoveries` 1 / `affinity` 1.0 / `coupled` — every recovery either took was a joint recovery. (Mirrors v32's "both-fell-once → 100%/coupled" call.)
- **Not a recovery-affinity artifact.** v33 introduces no new on-disk format; the affinity is _derived_, recomputed on demand from the N auditable coherence inputs.
- **Not a sequence the command discovers.** It takes the artifacts **in round order** on the argv, exactly as the sixteen trend/exposure/persistence/… commands do.
- **Not a browser surface.** An N-round pairwise affinity of uploaded artifacts is a CI/dashboard concern (mirroring v16–v32).

## §3. The posture filter (unchanged)

1. **Deterministic** — `computeCoherenceRecoveryAffinity` over N `PostureCoherence` objects is pure: it matches fronts by dimension, pins fronts by `localeCompare(_, "en")` (the same order the trajectory/exposure/concurrency/affinity functions use), enumerates unordered pairs in that pinned order (`dimension_a < dimension_b`), counts each pair's co-recoveries and union by intersecting the fronts' recovery-step sets, and picks the tightest affinity by **integer cross-multiplication** (`co × union` of the two pairs), never a float comparison. Identical artifacts in identical order → identical `recovery_affinity_hash` on any machine.
2. **Honest about unstated data** — a front no document states in a round does not _recover_ into or out of that round: silence is neither a fall nor a recovery (the same §3 contract that keeps `below → unstated → below` zero crossings in v24 and zero recoveries in v29). A recovery revealed after a silent gap is attributed to the transition into the round that reveals the new standing — never an invented recovery on the silent step. A pair that never both-recovered is omitted from `pairs[]` (no affinity edge), never ranked.
3. **Advisory** — the command names which fronts climbed back above the team's own floor together, and how reliably. It asserts no legal conclusion.
4. **No server** — N local files in, one report out. No socket, no engine run.
5. **Additive** — a brand-new subcommand and one new pure module. Every existing command (`analyze`, `diff`, `compare`, `compare-coherence`, `coherence-trend`, `coherence-shift-trend`, `coherence-arc`, `coherence-exposure`, `coherence-persistence`, `coherence-breadth`, `coherence-recurrence`, `coherence-volatility`, `coherence-synchrony`, `coherence-settling`, `coherence-onset`, `coherence-latency`, `coherence-concurrency`, `coherence-relapse`, `coherence-tenure`, `coherence-affinity`, `verify`) is byte-for-byte unchanged in _output and goldens_; `coherence-sequence.ts` is imported and reused **without modification** (v33 needs nothing exported that was not already public — like v19–v32, it touches no existing source file's behavior at all).

# Part I — The affinity (pure)

`src/report/coherence-recovery-affinity.ts` (a new sibling to `coherence-affinity.ts`):

- **`RecoveryAffinityClass`** — a pair's co-recovery coupling class: `coupled` (the two fronts recovered together for a strict majority of the steps either recovered — `co_recoveries × 2 > union_recoveries`), `incidental` (they recovered together in ≥ 1 step but not a strict majority of the union, including an exact split). Only pairs with `co_recoveries ≥ 1` are classified; a pair that never both-recovered has no affinity edge and is omitted.
- **`CoherenceRecoveryAffinityPair`** — one unordered pair's whole-sequence co-recovery relation: the `dimension_a`/`dimension_b` (pinned, `a < b`), the `a_recoveries`/`b_recoveries` (each front's total recoveries), the `co_recoveries` (transitions both recovered), the `union_recoveries` (transitions either recovered, `a_recoveries + b_recoveries − co_recoveries`), the `affinity` (`co_recoveries / union_recoveries`), and its `class`.
- **`CoherenceRecoveryAffinity`** — the whole-sequence reduction: `rounds` (count), `pairs[]` (the co-recovering pairs above, pinned by `(dimension_a, dimension_b)`), `class_counts` (the per-pair tally by class), `total_co_recoveries` (every pair's co-recoveries summed — equal to `Σ_t C(recovering_t, 2)`), `total_recoveries` (every front's recoveries summed — equal to v29's `total_recoveries`), `max_affinity` (the tightest coupling across the deal, `null` when no two fronts ever recovered together), `tightest_pair` (the `[dimension_a, dimension_b]` owning `max_affinity`, earliest on a tie, `null` when none), `coupled` (any pair a strict-majority coupling — the gate verdict), and `recovery_affinity_hash`.
- **`computeCoherenceRecoveryAffinity(rounds: PostureCoherence[])`** — the pure, IO-free core. For each front, derives its recovery-step set (the v29 recovery event, silence-skipping, §3); for each unordered pair, intersects the two sets for `co_recoveries`, computes the union, the affinity, and the strict-majority class; selects the tightest affinity by integer cross-multiplication, returning the reduction with a `recovery_affinity_hash`. Requires ≥ 2 rounds.
- **`exposureRecoveryCoupled(affinity)`** — the CI gate predicate: `affinity.coupled`. The recovery-direction pairwise verdict no other command exposes (v32 gates on the fall-direction pairing; v29 gates on a single per-step fall count).
- **`buildCoherenceRecoveryAffinityJson(affinity)`** / **`renderCoherenceRecoveryAffinitySummary(affinity)`** — the JSON (`schema: vaulytica.posture-recovery-affinity.v1`) and human-readable renderers. The summary prints the tightest-pairing verdict (the pair and its affinity, or none-paired when no two fronts ever recovered together, plus the class tally), then one line per co-recovering pair (`coupled` pairs first), then the hash.

v33 imports only the already-public `PostureCoherence` type, the `NegotiationTier` type, and the shared hashing helpers; the `below-acceptable` literal is a plain string; no existing source file changes.

# Part II — The command (headless)

`tools/cli/coherence-recovery-affinity.ts` (a new sibling to `coherence-affinity.ts`):

- **`computeCoherenceRecoveryAffinityArtifacts(texts, format?)`** — the pure CLI core. Verifies all N artifacts and runs the cross-ladder guard via the shared `verifyCoherenceSequence` loader (unchanged from v18–v32); on any malformed/tampered round returns `{ ok: false, errors }` (each prefixed `round N:`); on a cross-ladder pair returns `{ ok: false }` naming the two rounds; an unpinned artifact yields `{ ok: true }` with a `ladderNote`. Then `computeCoherenceRecoveryAffinity(rounds)` and renders (markdown summary or JSON), returning `{ ok: true, output, coupled, ladderNote }`.
- **`runCoherenceRecoveryAffinity(argv)`** — the CLI handler: reads the N files, calls the core, writes the `ladderNote` (if any) to stderr, prints the affinity to stdout, and — under `--fail-on-coupled-recoveries` — exits 2 when any pair recovered together for a strict majority of the steps either recovered. A malformed/tampered/cross-ladder input is a hard exit-1 error. Requires ≥ 2 positionals.

`tools/cli/run.ts` (the dispatcher) gains a `coherence-recovery-affinity` case and a `USAGE` entry.

```
# Each round, archive its coherence (kilobytes, no clause text):
vaulytica analyze 'round1/*.docx' --playbook-file team.playbook.json --posture --emit-coherence round1.coherence.json
vaulytica analyze 'round2/*.docx' --playbook-file team.playbook.json --posture --emit-coherence round2.coherence.json
vaulytica analyze 'round3/*.docx' --playbook-file team.playbook.json --posture --emit-coherence round3.coherence.json

# Later — read/gate which two fronts the counterparty restores as a block, from the archive alone:
vaulytica coherence-recovery-affinity round1.coherence.json round2.coherence.json round3.coherence.json \
  --fail-on-coupled-recoveries
```

# Part XVI — Principled deferrals

- **A `coherence-affinity --recoveries` flag.** ⬜ Not built — and deliberately not, mirroring v18–v32 Part XVI. v33 is a separate command, not a flag on the co-fall command, so each command keeps exactly one gate and one hash.
- **A configurable "co-recovered at least K times" gate (`--min-co-recoveries K`).** ⬜ Not built. The gate fires on a strict majority of the union (`co_recoveries × 2 > union_recoveries`), the one coupling condition whose meaning needs no tuning. `affinity` and `max_affinity` are in the JSON for a consumer that wants a different bar.
- **A minimum-union floor for the gate (`--min-union N`).** ⬜ Not built. A pair that both-recovered once reads 100% / coupled — honestly, every recovery either took was joint. `union_recoveries` is in the JSON for a consumer wanting to discount thin evidence.
- **A combined co-fall/co-recovery view (the full directional affinity matrix).** ⬜ Not built. v32 reads the fall direction, v33 the recovery direction; a consumer wanting both joins the two reports on `(dimension_a, dimension_b)`. A single command emitting both would carry two gates and two hashes — against the one-command-one-gate discipline v18–v32 hold.
- **A co-_occupancy_ affinity (both above floor in the same round, however they arrived).** ⬜ Not built. v33's co-recovery is a coincidence of recovery _events_ (the v29 transition), which keeps the join invariant to `Σ C(recovering_t, 2)` exact. Co-occupancy (both above floor in the same round, no shared recovery required) is a different relation over each front's `floors[]`; a consumer wanting it intersects the above-floor rounds directly.
- **A standalone recovery-affinity artifact (`--emit-recovery-affinity`).** ⬜ Deferred, for the same reason v14–v32 keep the derived thing derived: the affinity is cheaply recomputable from the N coherence artifacts on demand.
- **A full pairwise affinity matrix (every pair, including zero-overlap pairs).** ⬜ Not built. `pairs[]` lists only pairs with `co_recoveries ≥ 1` (the affinity edges); a zero-overlap pair has affinity 0 and carries no coupling signal.
- **A directory/glob walker that infers round order.** ⬜ Not built. The command takes artifacts in round order on the argv (the caller's contract, mirroring the sixteen trend/exposure/persistence/… commands).
- **A browser surface for an N-round affinity.** ⬜ Deferred (v16–v32 Part XVI). The browser does an in-session two-round comparison; an N-artifact pairwise affinity is a CI/dashboard concern.
