# Vaulytica v43 — Document-Free Recovery Chain (Who Is Restored Last Across the Whole Cascade, Not Just One Pair — the Transitive Closure of v37's Recovery-Order Relation)

> **Status:** **Shipped (9.40.0).** v37 gave the posture archive its recovery-direction precedence: per _pair_ of fronts, when both _recover_ (climb back at-or-above the acceptable floor), does one consistently recover _first_ — and so the other _last_ (the laggard, left exposed longest)? But a deal lead reading the close does not watch one pair; they want a single **restoration order**: the front restored _after everything else_, the deal's persistent weak point. v37 cannot supply it — it reads each pair in isolation and never composes them. v43 is the **transitive closure** of that relation — the exact **recovery mirror of v42** (the way v37 mirrors v36, v33 mirrors v32, v30 mirrors v28). It builds the directed graph whose edges are exactly v37's strict-majority `first_recoverer → last_recoverer` pairs, computes reachability, and reads the structure no pairwise scan can see — the _chains_ (Cap recovers before Term recovers before Indemnity, so Indemnity is restored after Cap even with no direct edge), the deal's **tailwater** (the greatest-`recovered_before_by` sink, the front restored last of all), and the **cycle** (Cap recovers before Term recovers before Indemnity recovers before Cap — three clean pairwise orders that cannot be globally ranked, a Condorcet cycle no pairwise read can detect). It is gated on the one tuning-free condition: does the relation contain a directed cycle (`--fail-on-recovery-cycle`)? It continues the global step numbering after v42's Step 222, beginning at **Step 223**. One new subcommand, one pure module, **zero** change to any existing source file.
> **Scope:** one idea — the _transitive_ read of the same recovery-order edges v37 derives pairwise. It is not a new crossing, ordering, magnitude, or pairwise read; it reuses `computeCoherenceRecoveryOrder` **unchanged** (the join pattern v38/v40/v41/v42 use) and composes its strict-majority `leading` pairs into a directed graph. The use case is the restoration order: v37 says "Cap recovers before Term, and Term recovers before Indemnity"; v43 says "so **Indemnity** is the tailwater — the front the counterparty restores last across the whole cascade, exposed below the floor longest" — or "your fronts form a **cycle**, so there is _no_ single front restored last." Where v37 names a _pair's_ laggard, v43 names the deal's _global_ restoration order — and whether one exists at all. Where v42 (lead chain) names the _headwater_ (the front to watch _first_), v43 names the _tailwater_ (the front exposed _longest_): the two transitive reads the two directions of a floor crossing admit.
> **Posture (unchanged, non-negotiable):** deterministic (the chain verdict is a pure reduction over the edges v37 derives — the reachability a plain integer Floyd–Warshall fixpoint, the tailwater pick decided by greatest `recovered_before_by` with the earliest label on a tie, the gate a pure boolean over the integer-derived edges; no float threshold enters the verdict, so the same artifacts → identical `recovery_chain_hash`, on any machine, forever), no AI / no probabilistic path, no server (N local files in, one report out; no socket), citable (the artifacts carry the same per-front binding floors v12 derived from each document's own clause and the team's own playbook — v43 adds no new claim), lints / references / positions — but never drafts, and never renders a legal conclusion. The command is **advisory and verifiable**: every artifact is hash-verified (a tampered round is a hard error, side-labeled by round index), and the ladders are checked for a match across the _whole_ sequence (any cross-ladder pair is refused) via the same `verifyCoherenceSequence` loader the twenty-six trend/exposure/…/chain commands use, unchanged. Every step passes the §3 filter or it does not ship.

# Part 0 — Intent

## §1. Why we're doing this

v42's Open Question #1 left exactly one transitive read of the pairwise family unbuilt: the **recovery mirror**. v42 composes v35's _crossing_ (lead-lag, direction-blind) edges into a watch-order and names the headwater — the front to watch _first_. The recovery direction has its own precedence (v37), and it had no transitive read. v43 is that read.

v37 already pairs each two fronts and asks which _recovers_ first — and so which is left exposed _last_. It reads each pair in isolation, and that isolation hides two facts a deal lead reviewing the close actually wants:

- **The chain.** Cap recovers before Term, Term recovers before Indemnity. v37 reports two pairs and _never_ the composed fact: Indemnity is restored after Cap — _through_ Term — even though Cap and Indemnity may never have recovered in different steps (no direct edge). The deal lead who wants to know "which front is left exposed longest of all?" gets two disconnected pair verdicts; v37 cannot name that Indemnity is the _tailwater_, because it never composes the edges.
- **The cycle.** Cap recovers before Term, Term recovers before Indemnity, Indemnity recovers before Cap — three pairwise recovery orders, each a perfectly consistent strict majority, that **cannot be globally ranked**. There is no last-recoverer; the recovery-order relation is _intransitive_ (a Condorcet cycle, the same intransitive-tournament phenomenon non-transitive dice exhibit). Every pair looks clean to v37; only composing all three reveals the paradox. When this happens, the per-pair laggard signal v37 sells _does not compose_ into a restoration order — there is no single front restored last, because the order loops.

To v37 read alone both are invisible — it sees pairs, never the graph they form. v43 supplies the closure and the one verdict whose meaning needs no tuning: per front, the `recovers_before_directly` (its direct out-neighbours), the transitive `reach` (how many fronts it recovers before through _any_ chain) and `recovered_before_by` (how many recover before it), whether it sits on a cycle (`in_cycle`), and a `class` (`source` / `relay` / `sink` / `cyclic` / `isolated`); plus the deal's `tailwater` (the greatest-`recovered_before_by` sink), `max_lag`, `edges` (= v37's `leading` tally), `acyclic`, and `cyclic` (the gate). All of it is a reachability fixpoint over the same recovery-order edges v37 already derives — no new crossing, no new ordering, no new on-disk format.

## §2. What v43 is and is not

**It is:**

- A **document-free exposure recovery-chain command.** `coherence-recovery-chain <r1.coherence.json> … <rN.coherence.json>` reads N ≥ 2 saved coherence artifacts in round order, verifies each, runs the cross-ladder guard across all of them, and prints, per **front**, its direct out-neighbours (`recovers_before_directly`), its transitive `reach` and `recovered_before_by`, whether it sits on a cycle (`in_cycle`), and its `class` — plus the deal's `tailwater` (the greatest-`recovered_before_by` sink — the front restored last of all), `max_lag`, the `edges` count, `acyclic`, and `cyclic` (the gate verdict). Markdown (default) or `--format json`. No documents are read; the engine is never run.
- A **single deal-level gate, on the global ordering's coherence.** `--fail-on-recovery-cycle` exits 2 when the recovery-order relation contains a directed cycle — three or more fronts each recovering above the floor first over the next in a loop, so no single restoration order ranks every front. A directed cycle either exists or it does not; the gate is a pure boolean over the integer-derived edges and inherits no knob. It is the _transitive_ verdict v37 structurally cannot pose: every pair on the loop is individually `leading` to v37.
- A **whole-sequence-verified input, sharing the v17–v42 front end.** Every artifact's `coherence_hash` is re-derived and checked on load (a tampered/corrupt round is a hard error, errors prefixed `round N:`). The v15/v16 cross-ladder guard runs across the sequence via the same `verifyCoherenceSequence` loader the twenty-six trend/exposure/…/chain commands use, with no change to it.
- A **derived report carrying its own fingerprint.** The report carries a `recovery_chain_hash` (a stable SHA-256 over the canonical per-front set — the `dimension`, its sorted `recovers_before_directly`, and its `class`; the derived transitive `reach`/`recovered_before_by` integers, fully determined by the edge set, and the deal-level scalars are omitted, so the fingerprint is integer-/string-exact over the inputs), namespaced apart from every prior hash (`coherence_hash`, …, `chain_hash`), so the same N artifacts in the same order always reproduce the same report.

**It is not:**

- **Not a new diff, classifier, crossing, or artifact format.** It reuses the recovery-order edges `computeCoherenceRecoveryOrder` already derives — no new fall, recovery, crossing, or ordering math. v43 is a subcommand plus one pure module (`computeCoherenceRecoveryChain`) whose only new logic is the reachability fixpoint over those edges. `parsePostureCoherenceJson`, the coherence schema, `coherence-recovery-order.ts`, and every other function are unchanged.
- **Not the recovery-order command (v37).** v37 reads each **pair** in isolation and gates on the _presence_ of a stable laggard (`--fail-on-lagging-recovery`). v43 **composes** those pairs into a directed graph and gates on the _incoherence_ of the composed ordering (`--fail-on-recovery-cycle`). A deal whose every pair is `leading` (v37 fires) can be acyclic (v43 clears — a clean restoration order) or cyclic (v43 fires — an intransitive loop); the two verdicts are independent, one over a pair, one over the whole graph.
- **Not the lead chain (v42).** v42 composes v35's _crossing_ edges (a fall _or_ a recovery) and names the _headwater_ (the front that moves first, the early-warning origin). v43 composes v37's _recovery-only_ edges and names the _tailwater_ (the front restored last, exposed longest). A pair `leading` on the lead chain can be `interleaved` on the recovery chain and vice versa — the two compose independent edge sets. The headwater is good news to chase; the tailwater is the warning.
- **Not a read over plurality edges.** The graph uses only v37's strict-majority `leading` pairs (the edges v37's own gate fires on), not every non-null `first_recoverer`. A razor-thin plurality first-recoverer is not a stable order, so it carries no edge; the chain is built from the same stable couplings the family gates on everywhere else.
- **Not a longest-path or simple-path read.** v43 reads transitive _reach_ / _recovered_before_by_ (how many fronts a front recovers before / is recovered before by through any chain) and cycle _existence_, all polynomial and integer-exact. The longest _simple_ path is NP-hard on a cyclic graph and would need a float-free tie-break the binary floor does not naturally supply; the in-reach count is the cascade-breadth read that is exact.
- **Not a depth-weighted score.** The floor is binary (`below-acceptable` is the only sub-floor rung), so a _depth_ weighting is not viable; v43 weights nothing — it counts reachable fronts and detects cycles, both pure structure.
- **Not a chain artifact.** v43 introduces no new on-disk format; the report is _derived_, recomputed on demand from the N auditable coherence inputs.
- **Not a sequence the command discovers.** It takes the artifacts **in round order** on the argv, exactly as the twenty-six trend/exposure/… commands do.
- **Not a browser surface.** An N-round transitive recovery-order synthesis of uploaded artifacts is a CI/dashboard concern (mirroring v16–v42).

## §3. The posture filter (unchanged)

1. **Deterministic** — `computeCoherenceRecoveryChain` over N `PostureCoherence` objects is pure: it delegates the per-pair recovery-order derivation to `computeCoherenceRecoveryOrder` (the `localeCompare`-pinned front order, the silence-skipping recovery scan), keeps only its strict-majority `leading` pairs as directed `first_recoverer → last_recoverer` edges, computes a plain integer reachability fixpoint (Floyd–Warshall, paths of length ≥ 1), and picks the tailwater by greatest `recovered_before_by` with the earliest label on a tie. The gate is the boolean "any front reaches itself." No float enters the verdict. Identical artifacts in identical order → identical `recovery_chain_hash` on any machine.
2. **Honest about unstated data** — a front no document states never recovers (the §3 contract v37 already enforces): it has no recovery-order edge, so it is `isolated` (reach 0, led-by 0), counted but carrying no chain. A pair with no consistent first-recoverer (v37 `interleaved`) is no edge — it joins no chain. Silence is never an edge.
3. **Advisory** — the command names which front the counterparty restores _last_ across the team's own floor _through a chain_, and whether that ordering is coherent. It asserts no legal conclusion.
4. **No server** — N local files in, one report out. No socket, no engine run.
5. **Additive** — a brand-new subcommand and one new pure module. Every existing command (`analyze`, `diff`, `compare`, `compare-coherence`, `coherence-trend`, …, `coherence-chain`, `verify`) is byte-for-byte unchanged in _output and goldens_; `coherence-recovery-order.ts` and `coherence-sequence.ts` are imported and reused **without modification** (v43 needs nothing exported that was not already public — like v19–v42, it touches no existing source file's behavior at all).

# Part I — The report (pure)

`src/report/coherence-recovery-chain.ts` (a new sibling to `coherence-recovery-order.ts` and `coherence-chain.ts`):

- **`RecoveryChainRole`** — a front's role in the transitive recovery-order graph: `cyclic` (it transitively recovers before itself — on a directed cycle, the gate-worthy class), `source` (not on a cycle, recovers before ≥ 1 front and `recovered_before_by` 0 — restored first of all), `sink` (not on a cycle, recovered-before by ≥ 1 and recovers before none — the **tailwater** candidate, restored last), `relay` (not on a cycle, both recovers before some and is recovered-before by others), `isolated` (no edge touches it — reach 0 and led-by 0, incl. an unstated front).
- **`CoherenceRecoveryChainFront`** — one front's place in the graph: the `dimension`, the sorted `recovers_before_directly` (its direct out-neighbours), `reach` (transitive out-reach, excluding self), `recovered_before_by` (transitive in-reach, excluding self), `in_cycle` (does it transitively recover before itself), and its `class`.
- **`CoherenceRecoveryChain`** — the whole-sequence reduction: `rounds` (count), `fronts[]` (pinned by `dimension`), `class_counts` (the per-front tally by role), `edges` (the directed recovery-order edge count = v37's `leading` pair count), `max_lag` (the greatest `recovered_before_by` among sink fronts), `tailwater` (the sink owning it, earliest label on a tie; `null` when no sink), `acyclic`, `cyclic` (`≥ 1` front on a cycle — the gate verdict), and `recovery_chain_hash`.
- **`computeCoherenceRecoveryChain(rounds: PostureCoherence[])`** — the pure, IO-free core. Delegates the per-pair recovery-order derivation to `computeCoherenceRecoveryOrder`, builds the directed graph from its `leading` edges, computes the reachability fixpoint, classifies each front, selects the tailwater by greatest in-reach, and returns the reduction with a `recovery_chain_hash`. Requires ≥ 2 rounds (v37 enforces this — a recovery is a between-round event).
- **`exposureRecoveryCyclic(chain)`** — the CI gate predicate: `chain.cyclic`. The recovery mirror of v42's `exposureCyclic` (v42 composes the lead-lag/crossing edges; v43 the recovery-order edges).
- **`buildCoherenceRecoveryChainJson(chain)`** / **`renderCoherenceRecoveryChainSummary(chain)`** — the JSON (`schema: vaulytica.posture-recovery-chain.v1`) and human-readable renderers. The summary prints the tailwater (the sink and its in-reach, or none when no clean sink exists), the ordering verdict (acyclic / intransitive), the role tally, then one line per front that touches the graph (sinks first, then relays, cyclic fronts, sources), then the hash. An `isolated` front is counted but never listed (§3 honesty — it joins no chain).

v43 imports `computeCoherenceRecoveryOrder` and the shared hashing helpers; no existing source file changes.

# Part II — The command (headless)

`tools/cli/coherence-recovery-chain.ts` (a new sibling to `coherence-chain.ts`):

- **`computeCoherenceRecoveryChainArtifacts(texts, format?)`** — the pure CLI core. Verifies all N artifacts and runs the cross-ladder guard via the shared `verifyCoherenceSequence` loader (unchanged from v18–v42); on any malformed/tampered round returns `{ ok: false, errors }` (each prefixed `round N:`); on a cross-ladder pair returns `{ ok: false }` naming the two rounds; an unpinned artifact yields `{ ok: true }` with a `ladderNote`. Then `computeCoherenceRecoveryChain(rounds)` and renders (markdown summary or JSON), returning `{ ok: true, output, cyclic, ladderNote }`.
- **`runCoherenceRecoveryChain(argv)`** — the CLI handler: reads the N files, calls the core, writes the `ladderNote` (if any) to stderr, prints the report to stdout, and — under `--fail-on-recovery-cycle` — exits 2 when the recovery-order relation contains a directed cycle. A malformed/tampered/cross-ladder input is a hard exit-1 error. Requires ≥ 2 positionals.

`tools/cli/run.ts` (the dispatcher) gains a `coherence-recovery-chain` case and a `USAGE` entry.

```
# Each round, archive its coherence (kilobytes, no clause text):
vaulytica analyze 'round1/*.docx' --playbook-file team.playbook.json --posture --emit-coherence round1.coherence.json
vaulytica analyze 'round2/*.docx' --playbook-file team.playbook.json --posture --emit-coherence round2.coherence.json
vaulytica analyze 'round3/*.docx' --playbook-file team.playbook.json --posture --emit-coherence round3.coherence.json

# Later — name the deal's tailwater (the front restored last of all), or catch an intransitive recovery-order cycle, from the archive alone:
vaulytica coherence-recovery-chain round1.coherence.json round2.coherence.json round3.coherence.json \
  --fail-on-recovery-cycle
```

# Part XVI — Principled deferrals

- **A longest-chain (cascade depth) read.** ⬜ Not built. The longest _simple_ path is NP-hard on a cyclic graph and needs a float-free tie-break the binary floor does not naturally supply; v43 reads transitive `recovered_before_by` (cascade _breadth_), the polynomial, integer-exact magnitude. The full edge set is in the JSON for a consumer that wants to walk paths.
- **Plurality edges (every non-null first-recoverer, not just strict majority).** ⬜ Not built. The graph uses only v37's `leading` edges — the same stable couplings the family gates on everywhere. A razor-thin plurality first-recoverer is not a stable order; admitting it would make the cycle gate fire on noise.
- **A configurable cascade-breadth gate (`--min-lag K`).** ⬜ Not built. The gate fires on a directed _cycle_ — a structural pathology, not a threshold — already tuning-free. The per-front `recovered_before_by` is in the JSON for a consumer wanting a depth/breadth bar.
- **A 2-D per-front × per-step read.** ⬜ Deferred (v41/v42 Part XVI). The per-front, pairwise, and now _both_ transitive reads (lead v42, recovery v43) are complete on the structure the binary floor admits; the next genuinely-new direction is a _matrix_ read (per-front × per-step), not another reduction of the same edges.
- **A standalone chain artifact (`--emit-recovery-chain`).** ⬜ Deferred, for the same reason v14–v42 keep the derived thing derived: the report is cheaply recomputable from the N coherence artifacts on demand.
- **A browser surface for an N-round recovery-chain synthesis.** ⬜ Deferred (v16–v42 Part XVI). The browser does an in-session two-round comparison; an N-artifact transitive recovery-order read is a CI/dashboard concern.

# Part XVII — Open questions for the maintainer

1. **Is the transitive family now closed, both directions included?** v42 composed the lead-lag (crossing) edges into the headwater/watch-order read; v43 composes the recovery-order edges into the tailwater/restoration-order read. Recommendation: **treat the transitive pairwise family as complete.** The remaining genuinely-new read is the _2-D_ one (per-front × per-step matrix) — a different shape of output, not another reduction of the pairwise edges.
2. **Should the gate read the cycle or the cascade?** Today `--fail-on-recovery-cycle` reads the structural pathology (an intransitive loop); a consumer wanting to flag a deep restoration cascade reads `recovered_before_by`/`max_lag` from the JSON. Recommendation: **keep the gate on the cycle** — it is the uniquely-transitive condition (impossible to detect pairwise) and tuning-free, where any cascade-depth bar would need a knob.

# Part XVIII — What this gives the user

- **The deal's tailwater, named from the archive alone.** Archive each round's kilobyte coherence artifact as you go; `coherence-recovery-chain` composes v37's pairwise recovery orders into a directed graph and names the front restored _after everything else_ — the deal's persistent weak point, left exposed below the floor longest, even with no clause text checked out and no re-analysis run. v37 can name a pair's laggard; only the closure can name the _deal's_ last-restored front.

- **The recovery mirror of v42, completing the transitive family.** v42 names the headwater (watch first); v43 names the tailwater (exposed longest) — the two transitive reads the two directions of a floor crossing admit. Deterministic, no AI, no server, citable, never drafts — every line of v43 passes the §3 gate.
