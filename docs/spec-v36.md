# Vaulytica v36 — Document-Free Exposure Concession Order (Fall-Precedence / Who Concedes First)

> **Status:** **Shipped (9.33.0).** v35 gave the posture family its **first directional** pairwise read: per unordered pair of fronts, when the two both *cross* the acceptable floor (in any direction), does one consistently cross **first**? But v35 is **direction-blind** — it orders v24's *crossings*, a fall **or** a recovery, so a pair can lead there because one front reliably *recovers* first, not because it *concedes* first. The two questions diverge: "which front *moves* first?" (v35) and "which front gives **ground** first?" (the deal lead's real question) are different reads of the same matrix, and v35 conflates them. v36 supplies the **direction-resolved** read: restrict the ordering to *falls* only. For each unordered pair of fronts, how many round-transition comparisons saw A fall below the floor *before* B (`a_concedes_first`), how many saw B before A (`b_concedes_first`), how many saw the two fall in the *same* step (`co_falls` — a timing tie), out of all `comparisons` (`a_concedes_first + b_concedes_first + co_falls`); the pair's `first_conceder` (the front that fell first more often), the resulting `affinity` (the first-conceder's share of all comparisons), the deal's **most-conceding pairing** (`max_affinity` / `most_conceding_pair` / `first_conceding_front`), and — the one tuning-free verdict — whether any pair has a front that fell first for a strict **majority** of the comparisons (`concedes`). It continues the global step numbering after v35's Step 215, beginning at **Step 216**. One new subcommand, one pure module, **zero** change to any existing source file.
> **Scope:** one idea — the family's **first direction-resolved precedence**. v35 answers *which front crosses first* over any-direction crossings; v36 answers *which front **concedes** first* — the fall-only restriction of the same ordering. It reuses v32's exact **fall** events (an at-or-above → below flip; silence-skipping per §3) — so a comparison pairs one A-fall step against one B-fall step, and A *concedes first* when its step is the earlier one. By construction each pair's `co_falls` (the same-step fall ties) equals v32's `co_falls` for that pair, and summed over all pairs `total_co_falls` equals `Σ_t C(falling_t, 2)` (v29's per-step fall count choose-two'd) — the same total v32 reports. The use case is the same archive of per-round coherence artifacts: v35 says "across the deal, **Cap always crosses the floor before Term**"; v36 says "and the reason is **Cap concedes first** — it falls below the floor ahead of Term, deal after deal; watch Cap to anticipate Term giving ground."
> **Posture (unchanged, non-negotiable):** deterministic (the concession order is a pure per-pair ordered comparison over the same `below-acceptable` rung v10 defines, taken over the binding floors v12 already derived and v24/v29/v32 already read; the most-conceding pick is decided by integer cross-multiplication, never a float compare, so the same artifacts → identical `concession_hash`, on any machine, forever), no AI / no probabilistic path, no server (N local files in, one report out; no socket), citable (the artifacts carry the same per-front binding floors v12 derived from each document's own clause and the team's own playbook — v36 adds no new claim), lints / references / positions — but never drafts, and never renders a legal conclusion. The command is **advisory and verifiable**: every artifact is hash-verified (a tampered round is a hard error, side-labeled by round index), and the ladders are checked for a match across the _whole_ sequence (any cross-ladder pair is refused) via the same `verifyCoherenceSequence` loader the nineteen trend/exposure/persistence/breadth/recurrence/volatility/synchrony/settling/onset/latency/concurrency/relapse/tenure/affinity/recovery-affinity/opposition/precedence commands use, unchanged. Every step passes the §3 filter or it does not ship.

# Part 0 — Intent

## §1. Why we're doing this

v35 gave the posture archive its first **directional** pairwise read: across the deal, when fronts A and B both cross the acceptable floor, does one reliably move **first**? It is the lead-lag ordering a same-step read (v32/v33/v34) structurally cannot pose. But it orders v24's **crossings** — a *fall* **or** a *recovery* — so it is **direction-blind**, and that conflates two distinct relations:

- **Who concedes first.** Cap falls below the floor in rounds 1 and 3; Term falls in rounds 2 and 4 — every comparison, Cap *gave ground* first. This is the early-warning signal a deal lead wants: movement of Cap *below the floor* reliably precedes Term doing the same.
- **Who recovers first.** A pair can be a clear v35 leader for the *opposite* reason — one front reliably *recovers* (climbs back above the floor) first, while their *falls* interleave. v35 reports a leader; the concession order is mixed.

To v35 these are indistinguishable (it orders crossings of either direction). The difference is **directional** — the missing concession-vs-recovery split of the same precedence relation, the way v29 split v25's direction-blind synchrony into *falling* vs *recovering*. It matters because the gate-worthy signal is asymmetric: a deal lead watches for the front the counterparty **concedes first** (the leading edge of a coming concession), not merely the front that moves first in any direction. A lead that comes from recovering first is *good* news; a lead that comes from conceding first is the warning.

This is not a gap in v35 — a *direction-blind* precedence is supposed to order every crossing. It is the missing **directional** dimension of the same ordering relation. v36 supplies it. Read the same per-front fall-step lists v32 overlaps, on the ordering: for each unordered pair, how many comparisons saw A fall first, how many saw B first, how many were same-step ties, the first-conceder, the resulting affinity, the deal's most-conceding such pairing, and whether any pair has a front that fell first for a strict majority of the comparisons. The gate follows: `--fail-on-leading-concession` fires when a pair has a front that _consistently_ concedes first — the one ordering boundary whose meaning ("one front reliably gives ground before the other") needs no tuning. All of this is a reduction over the same `floors[]` falls v29 counts per step and v32 overlaps per pair.

## §2. What v36 is and is not

**It is:**

- A **document-free concession-order (fall-precedence) command.** `coherence-concession <r1.coherence.json> … <rN.coherence.json>` reads N ≥ 2 saved coherence artifacts in round order, verifies each, runs the cross-ladder guard across all of them, and prints, per **pair** of fronts with any ordered fall comparison, how many comparisons saw A fall first (`a_concedes_first`), B first (`b_concedes_first`), the two together (`co_falls`), out of all `comparisons`; the pair's `first_conceder` and `affinity`, and its `class` — plus the deal's **most-conceding pairing** (`max_affinity` / `most_conceding_pair` / `first_conceding_front`, `null` when no front ever consistently fell first), the `total_ordered_comparisons` and `total_co_falls` (`= Σ_t C(falling_t, 2)`). Markdown (default) or `--format json`. No documents are read; the engine is never run.
- A **single deal-level ordering gate.** `--fail-on-leading-concession` exits 2 when at least one pair of fronts has a front that fell below the floor first for a strict **majority** of the comparisons (`max(a_concedes_first, b_concedes_first) × 2 > comparisons`). This is the *direction-resolved* counterpart to v35's direction-blind gate: where v35 fires on any-direction ordering, this fires on the *concession* ordering — a front the counterparty reliably gives ground on *before* its partner. A pair that conceded first once but fell together twice, or split its concessions evenly, is `interleaved` and clears it; a consumer wanting a different bar reads `affinity` and `max_affinity` from the JSON. It needs no tuning — a strict majority of the comparisons (ties and reverse-order working against it) *is* "this front consistently concedes first."
- A **whole-sequence-verified input, sharing the v17–v35 front end.** Every artifact's `coherence_hash` is re-derived and checked on load (a tampered/corrupt round is a hard error, errors prefixed `round N:`). The v15/v16 cross-ladder guard runs across the sequence via the same `verifyCoherenceSequence` loader the nineteen trend/exposure/…/precedence commands use, with no change to it.
- A **derived report carrying its own fingerprint.** The report carries a `concession_hash` (a stable SHA-256 over the canonical per-pair set — the integer `a_concedes_first`/`b_concedes_first`/`co_falls`, the `first_conceder`, and the class; the derived float `affinity` and the derived integer `comparisons` are omitted, so the fingerprint is integer-exact over the inputs), namespaced apart from every prior hash (`coherence_hash`, …, `opposition_hash`, `precedence_hash`), so the same N artifacts in the same order always reproduce the same report.

**It is not:**

- **Not a new diff, classifier, predicate, or artifact format.** It reuses the `weakest_tier` binding floor v12 derives, the `below-acceptable` rung v10 defines, and the _fall_ events v32 defines; v36 is a subcommand plus one pure module (`computeCoherenceConcession`) whose only new logic is a per-pair ordered comparison of the fall-step lists and a strict-majority test. `parsePostureCoherenceJson`, the coherence schema, and every trajectory/exposure/…/precedence function are unchanged.
- **Not the precedence command (v35).** v35 orders *every* crossing (a fall or a recovery), so a pair can lead there because one front *recovers* first. v36 orders *falls* only, isolating which front *concedes* first. A pair `leading` on v35 can be `interleaved` on v36 (it led on recoveries) and vice versa; the direction-blind and direction-resolved orderings are independent reads of the same `floors[]` matrix. A consumer wanting "which front moves first?" runs v35; one wanting "which front gives ground first?" runs v36.
- **Not the co-fall affinity command (v32).** v32 reads, per pair, which two fronts fall *together* in one step (the same-step coincidence, a Jaccard overlap of the fall-step *sets*). v36 reads, per pair, which front falls *first* across steps (the ordering of the fall-step *lists*). A pair `coupled` on co-falls (v32) can lead, follow, or interleave on concession order (v36), and a pair with no co-fall at all (every fall on a different step) can be the *strongest* first-conceder — the same-step and across-step relations are independent. The link is exact (each pair's `co_falls` equals v32's `co_falls`).
- **Not the concurrency command (v29).** v29 counts, per step, how many fronts fell together — a per-step scalar, gated on a single concerted-fall step. v36 reads, per pair, the *order* of falls across the deal — a per-pair relation, gated on a pair with a consistent first-conceder. Their link is the totals: summed over all pairs, v36's `total_co_falls` equals `Σ_t C(falling_t, 2)`, the same per-step fall count v29 reports.
- **Not a recovery-order command.** v36 orders *falls* only; the mirror read — which front *recovers* (climbs back above the floor) first — is a further read over the recovery-step lists (the v33 events), deferred until a consumer asks, exactly as v35 deferred its directional split. (v36 *is* that deferred split, taken on the fall side first because the concession order is the warning a deal lead watches.)
- **Not a same-step ordering (who fell earlier *within* one transition).** A transition is atomic — two fronts falling in the same step have no order (a `co_falls` tie, which counts *against* a lead). v36's precedence is strictly *between* transitions.
- **Not a configurable "concedes at least K times" gate.** v36 does not take a `--min-leads K` knob; the gate fires on a strict majority of the comparisons, the one ordering condition whose meaning needs no tuning. (v20–v35 made the same call.)
- **Not a minimum-comparison gate.** A pair with one ordered comparison and no ties reads `a_concedes_first` 1 / `comparisons` 1 / `affinity` 1.0 / `leading` — honestly, the one comparison the two made, one fell first.
- **Not a per-front reduction.** Like v32/v35, v36's primary object is an unordered **pair**, not a front. The `first_conceder` names *which* front of the pair fell first more often; it is a property of the pairing, not a per-front rank.
- **Not a concession-order artifact.** v36 introduces no new on-disk format; the report is _derived_, recomputed on demand from the N auditable coherence inputs.
- **Not a sequence the command discovers.** It takes the artifacts **in round order** on the argv, exactly as the nineteen trend/exposure/… commands do.
- **Not a browser surface.** An N-round pairwise concession order of uploaded artifacts is a CI/dashboard concern (mirroring v16–v35).

## §3. The posture filter (unchanged)

1. **Deterministic** — `computeCoherenceConcession` over N `PostureCoherence` objects is pure: it matches fronts by dimension, pins fronts by `localeCompare(_, "en")` (the same order the trajectory/exposure/affinity/precedence functions use), enumerates unordered pairs in that pinned order (`dimension_a < dimension_b`), counts each pair's concede-first/follow/tie comparisons by ordering one front's fall-step list against the other's, and picks the most-conceding affinity by **integer cross-multiplication** (`leads × comparisons` of the two pairs), never a float comparison. Identical artifacts in identical order → identical `concession_hash` on any machine.
2. **Honest about unstated data** — a front no document states in a round does not _fall_ into or out of that round: silence is neither a fall nor a recovery (the same §3 contract that keeps `below → unstated → below` zero falls in v29/v32). A fall revealed after a silent gap is attributed to the transition into the round that reveals the new standing — never an invented fall on the silent step. A pair the two never both fell in a *different* step (no ordered comparison — one never fell, or they always fell together) has no concession-order edge and is omitted from `pairs[]`, never ranked.
3. **Advisory** — the command names which front the counterparty gave ground on first across the team's own floor, and how reliably. It asserts no legal conclusion.
4. **No server** — N local files in, one report out. No socket, no engine run.
5. **Additive** — a brand-new subcommand and one new pure module. Every existing command (`analyze`, `diff`, `compare`, `compare-coherence`, `coherence-trend`, …, `coherence-precedence`, `verify`) is byte-for-byte unchanged in _output and goldens_; `coherence-sequence.ts` is imported and reused **without modification** (v36 needs nothing exported that was not already public — like v19–v35, it touches no existing source file's behavior at all).

# Part I — The report (pure)

`src/report/coherence-concession.ts` (a new sibling to `coherence-precedence.ts`):

- **`ConcessionClass`** — a pair's concession-order coupling class: `leading` (one front fell below the floor *before* the other for a strict majority of the comparisons — `max(a_concedes_first, b_concedes_first) × 2 > comparisons` — a consistent first-conceder, the gate-worthy class), `interleaved` (the two fell in mixed order with no strict-majority first-conceder, including an exact split or a pair whose comparisons are dominated by same-step ties). Only pairs with ≥ 1 *ordered* comparison (`a_concedes_first + b_concedes_first ≥ 1`) are classified; a pair the two never both fell in a different step is omitted.
- **`CoherenceConcessionPair`** — one unordered pair's whole-sequence concession-order relation: the `dimension_a`/`dimension_b` (pinned, `a < b`), the `a_concedes_first`/`b_concedes_first` (comparisons A/B fell first), the `co_falls` (same-step ties), the `comparisons`, the `first_conceder` (the front that fell first more often, `null` on an exact split), the `affinity` (`max(a_concedes_first, b_concedes_first) / comparisons`), and its `class`.
- **`CoherenceConcession`** — the whole-sequence reduction: `rounds` (count), `pairs[]` (the concession-order pairs above, pinned), `class_counts` (the per-pair tally by class), `total_ordered_comparisons` (every pair's ordered comparisons summed), `total_co_falls` (every pair's same-step fall ties summed — equal to `Σ_t C(falling_t, 2)`), `max_affinity` (the most-conceding coupling, `null` when no pair has a first-conceder), `most_conceding_pair` (the `[dimension_a, dimension_b]` owning `max_affinity`, earliest on a tie, `null` when none), `first_conceding_front` (the first-conceder of that pair, `null` when none), `concedes` (any pair a strict-majority concession order — the gate verdict), and `concession_hash`.
- **`computeCoherenceConcession(rounds: PostureCoherence[])`** — the pure, IO-free core. For each front, derives its fall-step list (the v32 events, silence-skipping, §3); for each unordered pair, orders A's fall steps against B's into `a_concedes_first`/`b_concedes_first`/`co_falls`, computes the comparisons, the affinity, and the strict-majority class; selects the most-conceding affinity by integer cross-multiplication, returning the reduction with a `concession_hash`. Requires ≥ 2 rounds.
- **`exposureConcedes(concession)`** — the CI gate predicate: `concession.concedes`. The direction-resolved precedence verdict no other command exposes (v35 gates on a direction-blind ordering; v32/v33/v34 on same-step couplings; v25/v29 on per-step counts).
- **`buildCoherenceConcessionJson(concession)`** / **`renderCoherenceConcessionSummary(concession)`** — the JSON (`schema: vaulytica.posture-concession.v1`) and human-readable renderers. The summary prints the most-conceding verdict (the first-conceder, its follower, and its lead share, or none-conceding when no front ever consistently fell first, plus the class tally), then one line per concession-order pair (`leading` pairs first), then the hash.

v36 imports only the already-public `PostureCoherence` type, the `NegotiationTier` type, and the shared hashing helpers; the `below-acceptable` literal is a plain string; no existing source file changes.

# Part II — The command (headless)

`tools/cli/coherence-concession.ts` (a new sibling to `coherence-precedence.ts`):

- **`computeCoherenceConcessionArtifacts(texts, format?)`** — the pure CLI core. Verifies all N artifacts and runs the cross-ladder guard via the shared `verifyCoherenceSequence` loader (unchanged from v18–v35); on any malformed/tampered round returns `{ ok: false, errors }` (each prefixed `round N:`); on a cross-ladder pair returns `{ ok: false }` naming the two rounds; an unpinned artifact yields `{ ok: true }` with a `ladderNote`. Then `computeCoherenceConcession(rounds)` and renders (markdown summary or JSON), returning `{ ok: true, output, concedes, ladderNote }`.
- **`runCoherenceConcession(argv)`** — the CLI handler: reads the N files, calls the core, writes the `ladderNote` (if any) to stderr, prints the report to stdout, and — under `--fail-on-leading-concession` — exits 2 when any pair has a front that fell first for a strict majority of the comparisons. A malformed/tampered/cross-ladder input is a hard exit-1 error. Requires ≥ 2 positionals.

`tools/cli/run.ts` (the dispatcher) gains a `coherence-concession` case and a `USAGE` entry.

```
# Each round, archive its coherence (kilobytes, no clause text):
vaulytica analyze 'round1/*.docx' --playbook-file team.playbook.json --posture --emit-coherence round1.coherence.json
vaulytica analyze 'round2/*.docx' --playbook-file team.playbook.json --posture --emit-coherence round2.coherence.json
vaulytica analyze 'round3/*.docx' --playbook-file team.playbook.json --posture --emit-coherence round3.coherence.json

# Later — read/gate which front the counterparty concedes first within a pair, from the archive alone:
vaulytica coherence-concession round1.coherence.json round2.coherence.json round3.coherence.json \
  --fail-on-leading-concession
```

# Part XVI — Principled deferrals

- **A `coherence-precedence --falls-only` flag.** ⬜ Not built — and deliberately not, mirroring v18–v35 Part XVI. v36 is a separate command, not a flag on the precedence command, so each command keeps exactly one gate and one hash.
- **A recovery-order command (which front *recovers* first).** ⬜ Not built. v36 orders *falls* only; the mirror — which front climbs back above the floor first (the v33 recovery events ordered the same way) — is a further read over the recovery-step lists, deferred until a consumer asks (it mirrors v36 the way v33 mirrors v32). The concession side ships first because the *first-conceder* is the early-warning signal a deal lead watches.
- **A configurable "concedes at least K times" gate (`--min-leads K`).** ⬜ Not built. The gate fires on a strict majority of the comparisons, the one ordering condition whose meaning needs no tuning. `affinity` and `max_affinity` are in the JSON for a consumer that wants a different bar.
- **A minimum-comparison floor for the gate (`--min-comparisons N`).** ⬜ Not built. A pair with one ordered comparison reads 100% / leading — honestly, the one comparison the two made, one fell first. `comparisons` is in the JSON for a consumer wanting to discount thin evidence.
- **A mean-lead-time concession (how *long* the first-conceder leads, in rounds).** ⬜ Not built. v36 counts *whether* A's fall index is lower, not *by how much*. A mean lead-time is a magnitude read over the same step indices; deferred (it adds a float aggregate the integer-exact hash would have to omit), exactly as v35 deferred its mean-latency variant.
- **A transitive concession chain (A concedes before B before C ⇒ A before C).** ⬜ Not built. v36 reports each unordered pair independently; composing the pairwise first-conceders into a deal-wide concession ordering is a graph reduction over `pairs[]`; a consumer wanting it joins the pairs themselves.
- **A standalone concession artifact (`--emit-concession`).** ⬜ Deferred, for the same reason v14–v35 keep the derived thing derived: the report is cheaply recomputable from the N coherence artifacts on demand.
- **A full pairwise matrix (every pair, including no-ordered-comparison pairs).** ⬜ Not built. `pairs[]` lists only pairs with `a_concedes_first + b_concedes_first ≥ 1` (the concession-order edges); a pair the two never both fell in a different step carries no ordering signal.
- **A directory/glob walker that infers round order.** ⬜ Not built. The command takes artifacts in round order on the argv (the caller's contract, mirroring the nineteen trend/exposure/… commands).
- **A browser surface for an N-round concession order.** ⬜ Deferred (v16–v35 Part XVI). The browser does an in-session two-round comparison; an N-artifact pairwise concession order is a CI/dashboard concern.

Recommendation: **defer until a consumer asks** — the concession order is the gate-worthy signal; a recovery-order read is informational (recovering first is *good* news, not a warning), so it has no obvious tuning-free gate of its own beyond "who restores first," which the headline already surfaces on the fall side.

# Part XVIII — What this gives the user

- **The concession structure of the deal, from the archive alone.** Archive each round's kilobyte coherence artifact as you go; `coherence-concession` shows not just _which front the counterparty moves first_ (v35, direction-blind) but _which front it **concedes** first_ — the direction-resolved read, distinguishing a first-conceder (the same front falling below the floor ahead of its partner, deal after deal — the leading edge of a coming concession) from an interleaved pair (mixed order, no first-conceder), and from a v35 leader that leads only because it *recovers* first, with no clause text checked out and no re-analysis run. The "which front does the counterparty give ground on first?" question a deal lead asks, answered deterministically from N small, verifiable files.
- **A gate on a stable concession order, not a single early fall.** `--fail-on-leading-concession` fires when a pair has a front that fell first for a strict majority of the comparisons — a relation that holds _across_ the deal.
