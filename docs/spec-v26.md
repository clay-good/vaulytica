# Vaulytica v26 — Document-Free Exposure Settling (Latest Floor Crossing / Settling Round)

> **Status:** **Shipped (9.23.0).** v24 gave the posture archive a *per-front crossing-count* axis: per front, how many times its standing crossed the acceptable floor across the whole deal (`crossings`). v25 re-bucketed those same crossings *per step*: per round-transition, how many fronts crossed at once (`crossing_fronts`). Both reduce the crossings to a **count** — and a count throws away *when the last crossing happened*. The one axis a deal lead asks reconciling a close neither can pose: per deal, **at what round did the package last cross the floor — and was it still in motion at the final round?** A deal whose only crossing was a front falling in round 1→2 and then five rounds of stability *settled early*; a deal whose only crossing was a front falling in the **final** round is identical to v24 (one monotone front) and v25 (one isolated step) yet is **unsettled** — the floor was still being crossed as the deal closed. That is a missing **axis**: the *time-of-last-movement* dimension of the same crossing data. v26 supplies it: read the same floor crossings v24/v25 count for the **index** of the last one — the deal's `settling_round`, the length of the quiet tail of steady rounds after it (`quiet_tail`), and whether the *final* transition itself crossed (`unsettled`). It gates, with no tuning, on a deal that never settled before the close. It continues the global step numbering after v25's Step 205, beginning at **Step 206**. One new subcommand, one pure module, **zero** change to any existing source file.
> **Scope:** one idea — the *time-of-last-movement* reduction the per-front crossing count (v24) and the per-step crossing count (v25) both leave out. v24 answers *how many times did a front cross the floor across the deal* (a per-front sum); v25 answers *how many fronts crossed the floor together in each step* (a per-transition count); v26 answers *which round did the package last cross the floor, and had it settled by the close* (a time index). `total_crossings` equals v24's grand total and v25's per-step sum by construction — v26 reads the same crossings for *where the last one falls*, not *how many there are*. The use case is the same archive of per-round coherence artifacts: v24 says "the Cap front crossed the floor twice over the deal"; v25 says "and round 1→2 is the step where Cap and Term crossed at once"; v26 says "and the package's last floor crossing was round 4→5, the final round — it never settled."
> **Posture (unchanged, non-negotiable):** deterministic (the settling index is a pure reduction over the same `below-acceptable` rung v10 defines, taken over the binding floors v12 already derived and v20/v22/v24/v25 already read; same artifacts → identical `settling_hash`, on any machine, forever), no AI / no probabilistic path, no server (N local files in, one report out; no socket), citable (the artifacts carry the same per-front binding floors v12 derived from each document's own clause and the team's own playbook — v26 adds no new claim), lints / references / positions — but never drafts, and never renders a legal conclusion. The command is **advisory and verifiable**: every artifact is hash-verified (a tampered round is a hard error, side-labeled by round index), and the ladders are checked for a match across the *whole* sequence (any cross-ladder pair is refused) via the same `verifyCoherenceSequence` loader the nine trend/exposure/persistence/breadth/recurrence/volatility/synchrony commands use, unchanged. Every step passes the §3 filter or it does not ship.

# Part 0 — Intent

## §1. Why we're doing this

v24 gave the posture archive a per-front crossing-count axis — per front, how many times its standing crossed the acceptable floor (`crossings`), falls and recoveries alike. v25 re-bucketed those same crossings by step — per round-transition, how many fronts crossed at once (`crossing_fronts`). Both are the right shape for "how much did the package move across the floor?" But both reduce the crossings to a **count**, and a count is silent on *time*: it cannot say *when* the package last crossed the floor. A deal lead reconciling a close asks a question neither the per-front sum nor the per-step count can answer: **had the package stopped crossing the floor before signing, or was it still in motion at the final round?**

Neither v24 nor v25 can answer it, because both sum or count crossings without keeping their position in the sequence. Two deals they hold as identical can be opposites on this axis:

- **The early cross.** A front falls below floor in round 1→2, then holds steady (below or recovered) for five more rounds. The package *settled early*: it quieted long before the close. One crossing, one isolated step.
- **The late cross.** A front falls below floor only in the **final** round. To v24 this is the same as the early cross (one monotone front, `crossings = 1`); to v25 it is the same (one isolated step, not synchronized — the gate clears). Yet it is **unsettled**: the floor was still being crossed as the deal closed — exactly the event a deal lead wants flagged.

To v24 and v25 these are indistinguishable (one crossing each, the same total). To v21 the *duration* may even agree (a front down for one round in each). None of the existing commands reports the one fact that separates a settled close from an unsettled one: the **round the package last crossed the floor**, and whether that round was the close.

This is not a bug in v24 or v25 — a crossing *count* is supposed to sum or bucket, not index. It is a missing **axis**: the *time-of-last-movement* dimension of the same crossing data. Where v24 sums crossings down the front axis and v25 sums them down the step axis, v26 reads the **index** of the last step any front crossed.

v26 supplies it. Read the same floor crossings v24/v25 count for the *position* of the last one — and report the deal's **settling round** (the latest round-transition any front crossed the floor), the **quiet tail** (the run of steady rounds after it), and whether the **final** transition itself crossed (`unsettled`). The gate fires on an unsettled close — the one time condition whose meaning ("the package was still moving across the floor at the final round") needs no tuning. All of this is a reduction of the crossings derivable from the `floors[]` array v22/v24/v25 already carry — the sum of every transition's crossings still equals v24's grand crossing total.

## §2. What v26 is and is not

**It is:**
- A **document-free settling command.** `coherence-settling <r1.coherence.json> … <rN.coherence.json>` reads N ≥ 2 saved coherence artifacts in round order, verifies each, runs the cross-ladder guard across all of them, and prints the deal's **settling round** (`settling_round` — the `to_round` of the latest transition any front crossed the floor, `null` when none ever did), the **quiet tail** (`quiet_tail` — the run of steady steps after the last crossing), the **active-step count** (`active_count` — steps where any front crossed), the **grand crossing total** (`total_crossings`, equal to v24's per-front sum and v25's per-step sum), and a per-transition series marking each step `active` (a front crossed) or `still` (none did). Markdown (default) or `--format json`. No documents are read; the engine is never run.
- A **single deal-level settling gate.** `--fail-on-unsettled-exposure` exits 2 when the **final** transition crossed the floor (`unsettled`) — the package was still moving across the floor at the close. This is the *time-of-last-movement* counterpart to v24's *per-front instability* gate and v25's *per-step co-movement* gate: where `--fail-on-volatile-exposure` fires on a front whose standing crossed ≥ 2 times *across the deal* and `--fail-on-synchronized-exposure` fires on a single *step* where ≥ 2 fronts crossed *together*, `--fail-on-unsettled-exposure` fires on the single fact that the *last* crossing fell on the *final* step. A deal that crossed early and then held steady clears it (even if a step was volatile or synchronized); a deal that crossed only in the final round trips it (even though no front is volatile and no step synchronized). It needs no tuning — a crossing in the final round *is* an unsettled close.
- A **whole-sequence-verified input, sharing the v17–v25 front end.** Every artifact's `coherence_hash` is re-derived and checked on load (a tampered/corrupt round is a hard error, errors prefixed `round N:`). The v15/v16 cross-ladder guard runs across the sequence via the same `verifyCoherenceSequence` loader the nine trend/exposure/persistence/breadth/recurrence/volatility/synchrony commands use, with no change to it.
- A **derived report carrying its own fingerprint.** The settling carries a `settling_hash` (a stable SHA-256 over the canonical per-transition set), namespaced apart from every `coherence_hash`, `movement_hash`, `trajectory_hash`, `shift_trajectory_hash`, `arc_hash`, `exposure_hash`, `persistence_hash`, `breadth_hash`, `recurrence_hash`, `volatility_hash`, and `synchrony_hash`, so the same N artifacts in the same order always reproduce the same report.

**It is not:**
- **Not a new diff, classifier, predicate, or artifact format.** It reuses the `weakest_tier` binding floor v12 derives and the `below-acceptable` rung v10 defines; v26 is a subcommand plus one pure module (`computeCoherenceSettling`) whose only new logic is locating the latest floor crossing over the same `floors[]` array v24/v25 scan. `parsePostureCoherenceJson`, the coherence schema, and every trajectory/exposure/persistence/breadth/recurrence/volatility/synchrony function are unchanged.
- **Not a crossing-count command.** v24 counts a front's crossings over the deal; v25 counts the fronts crossing in each step. v26 does not headline a count — it headlines an **index** (the round of the last crossing). A consumer wanting the per-front count runs v24; one wanting the per-step count runs v25; one wanting the time of the last crossing runs v26. Their totals agree (`total_crossings`).
- **Not a duration command.** v21 sums how many rounds each front sat *below* the floor (a length); v26 locates the round of the last *crossing* (a position). A front stuck below floor for all N rounds has a large v21 `rounds_below` but **zero** crossings — it never moved — so it is `settled` to v26 (its last crossing is `null`, the quiet tail is the whole sequence). v26 reports *when movement stopped*, not *how long exposure lasted*; a consumer wanting the duration runs v21, and one wanting both runs v21 and v26.
- **Not silence-makes-a-close-unsettled.** A round no document states does **not** count as a crossing and does **not** reset the standing: per §3 (the v21/v24/v25 contract), silence after a fall keeps the last known standing — it is neither a recovery nor a fall. A crossing across a silent gap is attributed to the transition *into the round that reveals the new standing*, never to the silent step. So a final round left entirely unstated reveals no crossing and leaves the deal **settled** (silence at the close is stability, not movement), and `below → unstated → acceptable` is one crossing on the step ending at the third round (an unsettled close *if* that third round is the final one). This keeps v26 from inventing — or mislocating — a crossing at the close.
- **Not a configurable "settled by round N" gate.** v26 does not take a `--settled-by N` knob; the gate fires on a crossing in the final transition, the one time condition whose meaning ("still moving at the close") needs no tuning. A team wanting to gate on a *longer* required quiet tail reads `quiet_tail` and `settling_round` from the JSON. (v20/v21/v22/v23/v24/v25 made the same call against a configurable floor/duration/breadth/episode/crossing count/simultaneity.)
- **Not a trajectory-whipsaw command.** v17's `whipsaw` fires on rung jitter anywhere on the ladder, including movement entirely *above* the floor. v26's settling is specific to the **floor boundary**: an above-floor whipsaw (`acceptable → ideal → acceptable`) crosses the floor zero times, so it has no settling round (`null`) and is `settled`. v26 isolates the floor-crossing motion that matters for exposure from rung jitter that never risks it.
- **Not a settling artifact.** v26 introduces no new on-disk format; the settling is *derived*, recomputed on demand from the N auditable coherence inputs — the same "keep the derived thing derived" discipline v14–v25 hold.
- **Not a sequence the command discovers.** It does not glob a directory or sort filenames; the caller passes the artifacts **in round order** on the argv, exactly as the nine trend/exposure/persistence/breadth/recurrence/volatility/synchrony commands do.
- **Not a browser surface.** An N-round settling of uploaded artifacts is a CI/dashboard concern, which is what this command serves (mirroring v16–v25).

## §3. The posture filter (unchanged)

1. **Deterministic** — `computeCoherenceSettling` over N `PostureCoherence` objects is pure: it matches fronts by dimension, pins each step's crossed-dimension list by `localeCompare(_, "en")` (the same order the trajectory/exposure/volatility/synchrony functions use), and locates the latest floor crossing over the caller's round order. Identical artifacts in identical order → identical `settling_hash` on any machine.
2. **Honest about unstated data** — a front no document states in a round does not cross into or out of that round. Within a front's path, an unstated round is skipped: it neither counts as a crossing nor resets the standing (silence is not a fall or a recovery — the §3 contract that keeps `unstated` never-flagged in v20 and `below → unstated → below` zero crossings in v24/v25). A crossing across a silent gap is attributed to the transition into the round that *reveals* the new standing; a final round left unstated reveals no crossing, so the close is `settled`.
3. **Advisory** — the command names the round the team's own floor was last crossed. It asserts no legal conclusion.
4. **No server** — N local files in, one report out. No socket, no engine run.
5. **Additive** — a brand-new subcommand and one new pure module. Every existing command (`analyze`, `diff`, `compare`, `compare-coherence`, `coherence-trend`, `coherence-shift-trend`, `coherence-arc`, `coherence-exposure`, `coherence-persistence`, `coherence-breadth`, `coherence-recurrence`, `coherence-volatility`, `coherence-synchrony`, `verify`) is byte-for-byte unchanged in *output and goldens*; `coherence-sequence.ts` is imported and reused **without modification** (v26 needs nothing exported that was not already public — like v19–v25, it touches no existing source file's behavior at all).

# Part I — The settling (pure)

`src/report/coherence-settling.ts` (a new sibling to `coherence-synchrony.ts`):

- **`SettlingState`** — a transition's settling state: `active` (at least one front crossed the floor in this step), `still` (none did).
- **`CoherenceSettlingTransition`** — one round-transition's movement read for *when* the last crossing falls: the `from_round` / `to_round` (1-based), `crossing_fronts` (how many fronts crossed), `crossed_dimensions` (the fronts that crossed, `localeCompare`-pinned), and the `state`.
- **`CoherenceSettling`** — the whole-sequence reduction: `rounds` (count), `per_transition[]` (the transitions above, in sequence order, length = rounds − 1), `total_crossings` (every front's crossing summed over the steps — equal to v24's and v25's total), `settling_round` (the `to_round` of the latest `active` step — the round the package last crossed the floor; `null` when none ever crossed), `quiet_tail` (the run of trailing `still` steps after the last crossing — the whole sequence when none crossed), `active_count` (steps where any front crossed), `unsettled` (the final transition was `active`), and `settling_hash`.
- **`computeCoherenceSettling(rounds: PostureCoherence[])`** — the pure, IO-free core. For each front, scans `floors[]` and attributes each *stated* floor crossing to the transition that reveals it (an unstated round is skipped, §3); marks each transition `active`/`still`, locates the latest active step, computes the quiet tail, and returns the reduction with a `settling_hash`. Requires ≥ 2 rounds.
- **`exposureUnsettled(settling)`** — the CI gate predicate: `settling.unsettled`. The time-of-last-movement verdict no other command exposes (v24 gates on a single front's crossing count; v25 on fronts crossing together in one step; this gates on the *last* crossing falling on the *final* step).
- **`buildCoherenceSettlingJson(settling)`** / **`renderCoherenceSettlingSummary(settling)`** — the JSON (`schema: vaulytica.posture-settling.v1`) and human-readable renderers. The summary prints the settling verdict (settled at round K with a quiet tail, or UNSETTLED at the final round, or steady-throughout when nothing crossed), the active-step count, then one line per step, then the hash.

v26 imports only the already-public `PostureCoherence` type, the `NegotiationTier` type, and the shared hashing helpers; the `below-acceptable` literal is a plain string; no existing source file changes.

# Part II — The command (headless)

`tools/cli/coherence-settling.ts` (a new sibling to `coherence-synchrony.ts`):

- **`computeCoherenceSettlingArtifacts(texts, format?)`** — the pure CLI core. Verifies all N artifacts and runs the cross-ladder guard via the shared `verifyCoherenceSequence` loader (unchanged from v18–v25); on any malformed/tampered round returns `{ ok: false, errors }` (each prefixed `round N:`); on a cross-ladder pair returns `{ ok: false }` naming the two rounds; an unpinned artifact yields `{ ok: true }` with a `ladderNote`. Then `computeCoherenceSettling(rounds)` and renders (markdown summary or JSON), returning `{ ok: true, output, unsettled, ladderNote }`.
- **`runCoherenceSettling(argv)`** — the CLI handler: reads the N files, calls the core, writes the `ladderNote` (if any) to stderr, prints the settling to stdout, and — under `--fail-on-unsettled-exposure` — exits 2 when the final transition crossed the floor. A malformed/tampered/cross-ladder input is a hard exit-1 error. Requires ≥ 2 positionals.

`tools/cli/run.ts` (the dispatcher) gains a `coherence-settling` case and a `USAGE` entry.

```
# Each round, archive its coherence (kilobytes, no clause text):
vaulytica analyze 'round1/*.docx' --playbook-file team.playbook.json --posture --emit-coherence round1.coherence.json
vaulytica analyze 'round2/*.docx' --playbook-file team.playbook.json --posture --emit-coherence round2.coherence.json
vaulytica analyze 'round3/*.docx' --playbook-file team.playbook.json --posture --emit-coherence round3.coherence.json

# Later — read/gate whether the package was still crossing the floor at the close, from the archive alone:
vaulytica coherence-settling round1.coherence.json round2.coherence.json round3.coherence.json \
  --fail-on-unsettled-exposure
```

# Part XVI — Principled deferrals

- **A `coherence-volatility --settling` or `coherence-synchrony --settling` flag.** ⬜ Not built — and deliberately not, mirroring v18–v25 Part XVI. v26 is a separate command, not a flag on a crossing-count command, so each command keeps exactly one gate and one hash.
- **A configurable "settled by round N" gate (`--settled-by N`).** ⬜ Not built. The gate fires on a crossing in the final transition (still moving at the close), the one time condition whose meaning needs no tuning. `settling_round` and `quiet_tail` are in the JSON for a consumer that wants to require a longer quiet tail.
- **A standalone settling artifact (`--emit-settling`).** ⬜ Deferred, for the same reason v14–v25 keep the derived thing derived: the settling is cheaply recomputable from the N coherence artifacts on demand, keeping the auditable inputs (each ladder-pinned, hash-verified) as the source of truth.
- **A *first-crossing* / re-exposure-latency stat (the round a front first crossed, or rounds between a front's fall and its recovery).** ⬜ Not built. v26 headlines the *last* crossing (the settling question, "had it stopped moving?"); the `per_transition` series plus the per-front `floors[]` already let a consumer locate the first crossing or measure a per-episode recovery latency. Add it if a dashboard asks to pin one.
- **A *per-front* settling (the round after which each front never crossed again).** ⬜ Not built. v26 headlines the *deal's* last crossing (the round any front last moved); a consumer wanting one front's settling reads its `floors[]` path. Add a composing command if a dashboard asks for the per-front view (the way v19 joined v17 and v18).
- **A directory/glob walker that infers round order.** ⬜ Not built. The command takes artifacts in round order on the argv (the caller's contract, mirroring the trend/exposure/persistence/breadth/recurrence/volatility/synchrony commands).
- **A browser surface for an N-round settling.** ⬜ Deferred (v16–v25 Part XVI). The browser does an in-session two-round comparison; an N-artifact settling is a CI/dashboard concern.

# Part XVII — Open questions for the maintainer

1. **Fold settling into a single "deal posture" command that reports the crossing count *and* the settling round?** Today the crossing-count commands (v24 per-front, v25 per-step) and the settling command (v26) are split. A dashboard wanting both runs them and joins on the shared `floors[]` matrix (their totals agree by construction). Recommendation: **keep them split at the command layer** — they carry categorically different gates (per-front "a front reversed", per-step "the package lurched", per-deal "still moving at the close") and different hashes; bolting one onto another would give that command two unlike gates. If a dashboard asks for the combined view, add a composing command the way v19 joined v17 and v18, not a flag.
2. **Report the *direction* of the last crossing (was the package's final move a fall or a recovery)?** The `crossed_dimensions` at the settling step plus the `floors[]` matrix already let a consumer read each crossing's direction. Recommendation: **defer** — the matrix exposes it, and a single "the last move was a fall" headline has no consumer yet; add it if a dashboard asks to pin one.

# Part XVIII — What this gives the user

- **The settling of the deal, from the archive alone.** Archive each round's kilobyte coherence artifact as you go; `coherence-settling` shows not just *how many times a front crossed the floor over the deal* (v24) or *how many fronts crossed together in each round* (v25) but *which round the package last crossed the floor, and whether it had settled by the close* — distinguishing an early cross that then held steady (settled) from a cross in the final round (unsettled), which v24 and v25 report identically as one monotone, isolated crossing, with no clause text checked out and no re-analysis run. The "had the package stopped moving by signing?" question a deal lead asks at a close, answered deterministically from N small, verifiable files.
- **A gate on the time of the last move, not just the crossing count, level, or episode count.** `--fail-on-unsettled-exposure` fires when the *final* transition crossed the floor — the package was still in motion at signing — a condition the count gates (v23/v24/v25) and the level/duration gates (v20/v21/v22) structurally cannot pose, since each sums or counts crossings without keeping their position. It catches the unsettled close even when no single front is volatile and no step is synchronized, and ignores a deal whose crossings all fell in earlier rounds.
- **The same five promises.** Deterministic, no AI, no server, citable, never drafts — every line of v26 passes the §3 gate. It reads the binding floor v12 already derives and the `below-acceptable` rung v10 already defines, through the loader v18–v25 already share; it adds one reduction over the crossings v24/v25 already count, no on-disk format, and no change to any existing source file's behavior — every existing surface is byte-for-byte unchanged.
