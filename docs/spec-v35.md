# Vaulytica v35 — Document-Free Exposure Precedence (Pairwise Lead-Lag / Who Crosses First)

> **Status:** **Shipped (9.32.0).** v32, v33, and v34 are the posture family's three **pairwise** reads, and all three are **same-step**: per unordered pair of fronts, which two crossed the acceptable floor *together* in one transition — aligned downward (v32, co-fall), aligned upward (v33, co-recovery), or opposed (v34, counter-move). They read coincidence *within* a transition and are blind to order *across* transitions. The ordering relation is unread: across the deal, when fronts A and B both eventually cross the floor, does one reliably move **first**? A step where Cap crosses in round 2 and Term crosses in round 4, every time, is a *lead-lag* pairing — Cap is an **early-warning indicator** for Term: movement on Cap reliably precedes movement on Term, so a deal lead watching Cap gets advance notice on Term. v35 supplies that read: for each unordered pair of fronts, how many round-transition comparisons saw A cross *before* B (`a_leads`), how many saw B before A (`b_leads`), how many saw the two cross in the *same* step (`co_crossings` — a timing tie), out of all `comparisons` (`a_leads + b_leads + co_crossings`); the pair's `leader` (the front that crossed first more often), the resulting `affinity` (the leader's share of all comparisons), the deal's **most-leading pairing** (`max_affinity` / `most_leading_pair` / `leading_front`), and — the one tuning-free verdict — whether any pair has a front that crossed first for a strict **majority** of the comparisons (`leading`). It continues the global step numbering after v34's Step 214, beginning at **Step 215**. One new subcommand, one pure module, **zero** change to any existing source file.
> **Scope:** one idea — the family's **first directional** pairwise read. v32/v33/v34 answer *which two fronts cross together* (and how — aligned or opposed); v35 answers *which front crosses first* — the lead-lag ordering a same-step read structurally cannot pose. It reuses v24's exact **crossing** events (a fall or a recovery; silence-skipping per §3) — so a comparison pairs one A-crossing step against one B-crossing step, and A *leads* when its step is the earlier one. By construction each pair's `co_crossings` (the same-step ties) equals v34's `joint_moves` for that pair, and summed over all pairs `total_co_crossings` equals `Σ_t C(crossing_t, 2)` (v25's per-step crossing count choose-two'd) — the same total v34 splits into aligned + opposed moves. The use case is the same archive of per-round coherence artifacts: v32 says "across the deal, **Cap and Term** _fell_ together"; v34 says "and **Cap and Fee** move _against_ each other"; v35 says "and across the deal, **Cap always crosses the floor before Term** — watch Cap to anticipate Term."
> **Posture (unchanged, non-negotiable):** deterministic (the precedence is a pure per-pair ordered comparison over the same `below-acceptable` rung v10 defines, taken over the binding floors v12 already derived and v24/v25/v34 already read; the most-leading pick is decided by integer cross-multiplication, never a float compare, so the same artifacts → identical `precedence_hash`, on any machine, forever), no AI / no probabilistic path, no server (N local files in, one report out; no socket), citable (the artifacts carry the same per-front binding floors v12 derived from each document's own clause and the team's own playbook — v35 adds no new claim), lints / references / positions — but never drafts, and never renders a legal conclusion. The command is **advisory and verifiable**: every artifact is hash-verified (a tampered round is a hard error, side-labeled by round index), and the ladders are checked for a match across the _whole_ sequence (any cross-ladder pair is refused) via the same `verifyCoherenceSequence` loader the eighteen trend/exposure/persistence/breadth/recurrence/volatility/synchrony/settling/onset/latency/concurrency/relapse/tenure/affinity/recovery-affinity/opposition commands use, unchanged. Every step passes the §3 filter or it does not ship.

# Part 0 — Intent

## §1. Why we're doing this

v32, v33, and v34 gave the posture archive its three pairwise reads, and all three are **same-step**. v32: for each unordered pair, how reliably the two _fell_ below floor **together** (the concession block). v33: how reliably the two _recovered_ **together** (the restoration block). v34: how reliably the two moved **opposite** ways at once (the see-saw). Each reads coincidence *within* a transition — which two fronts crossed in the same step, and in which directions.

But two fronts can cross the floor in a stable *order* — A before B, deal after deal — and none of v32/v33/v34 reads it. They are blind to order across transitions: a pair that always co-crosses (v32/v33/v34 see a coupling) and a pair where A crosses one round and B crosses the next (the same fronts, never the same step) can have *identical* same-step couplings, yet only the second carries a lead-lag signal. The ordering is a separate relation:

- **The leader.** Cap crosses the floor in rounds 1 and 3; Term crosses in rounds 2 and 4 — every comparison, Cap moved first. Cap _leads_ Term: movement on Cap reliably precedes movement on Term. A **precedence** linkage — an early-warning indicator.
- **The interleaved pair.** Cap and Term cross in mixed order — sometimes Cap first, sometimes Term — with no consistent first-mover. The same per-front crossing counts, but **no lead-lag signal**.

To v32/v33/v34 these are indistinguishable (all read same-step coincidence only). The difference is **pairwise on the ordering** — the missing directional axis of the same pairwise reduction. It matters because a stable lead-lag pairing is _strategic information_: it tells a deal lead which front is an **early indicator** for which other — the front to watch to anticipate the next concession (the leader moves, the follower is coming) — a relation a same-step read (v32/v33/v34) and a per-step count (v25/v29) structurally cannot pose.

This is not a gap in v32/v33/v34 — a _same-step_ affinity is supposed to read coincidence within a transition. It is the missing **directional** dimension of the same pairwise reduction. v35 supplies it. Read the same crossing events per-pair on the ordering: for each unordered pair, how many comparisons saw A cross first (the lead), how many saw B first, how many were same-step ties, the leader, the resulting affinity, the deal's most-leading such pairing, and whether any pair has a front that crossed first for a strict majority of the comparisons. The gate follows: `--fail-on-leading-front` fires when a pair has a front that _consistently_ crosses first — the one ordering boundary whose meaning ("one front reliably moves before the other") needs no tuning. All of this is a reduction over the same `floors[]` crossings v24 counts, v25 buckets per step, and v34 pairs same-step.

## §2. What v35 is and is not

**It is:**

- A **document-free precedence (lead-lag) command.** `coherence-precedence <r1.coherence.json> … <rN.coherence.json>` reads N ≥ 2 saved coherence artifacts in round order, verifies each, runs the cross-ladder guard across all of them, and prints, per **pair** of fronts with any ordered comparison, how many comparisons saw A cross first (`a_leads`), B first (`b_leads`), the two together (`co_crossings`), out of all `comparisons`; the pair's `leader` and `affinity`, and its `class` — plus the deal's **most-leading pairing** (`max_affinity` / `most_leading_pair` / `leading_front`, `null` when no front ever consistently crossed first), the `total_ordered_comparisons` and `total_co_crossings` (`= Σ_t C(crossing_t, 2)`). Markdown (default) or `--format json`. No documents are read; the engine is never run.
- A **single deal-level ordering gate.** `--fail-on-leading-front` exits 2 when at least one pair of fronts has a front that crossed the floor first for a strict **majority** of the comparisons (`max(a_leads, b_leads) × 2 > comparisons`). This is the _directional_ counterpart to v32/v33/v34's _same-step_ gates: where those fire on a coincidence, this fires on a stable order — a front the counterparty reliably moves *before* its partner. A pair that led once but crossed together twice, or split its leads evenly, is `interleaved` and clears it; a consumer wanting a different bar reads `affinity` and `max_affinity` from the JSON. It needs no tuning — a strict majority of the comparisons (ties and reverse-order working against it) _is_ "this front consistently crosses first."
- A **whole-sequence-verified input, sharing the v17–v34 front end.** Every artifact's `coherence_hash` is re-derived and checked on load (a tampered/corrupt round is a hard error, errors prefixed `round N:`). The v15/v16 cross-ladder guard runs across the sequence via the same `verifyCoherenceSequence` loader the eighteen trend/exposure/persistence/breadth/recurrence/volatility/synchrony/settling/onset/latency/concurrency/relapse/tenure/affinity/recovery-affinity/opposition commands use, with no change to it.
- A **derived report carrying its own fingerprint.** The report carries a `precedence_hash` (a stable SHA-256 over the canonical per-pair set — the integer `a_leads`/`b_leads`/`co_crossings`, the `leader`, and the class; the derived float `affinity` and the derived integer `comparisons` are omitted, so the fingerprint is integer-exact over the inputs), namespaced apart from every prior hash (`coherence_hash`, …, `affinity_hash`, `recovery_affinity_hash`, `opposition_hash`), so the same N artifacts in the same order always reproduce the same report.

**It is not:**

- **Not a new diff, classifier, predicate, or artifact format.** It reuses the `weakest_tier` binding floor v12 derives, the `below-acceptable` rung v10 defines, and the _crossing_ events v24 defines; v35 is a subcommand plus one pure module (`computeCoherencePrecedence`) whose only new logic is a per-pair ordered comparison of the crossing-step lists and a strict-majority test. `parsePostureCoherenceJson`, the coherence schema, and every trajectory/exposure/…/opposition function are unchanged.
- **Not the co-fall (v32), co-recovery (v33), or opposition (v34) command.** Those read, per pair, which two fronts cross the floor *together* in one step (aligned or opposed). v35 reads, per pair, which front crosses *first* across steps. A pair `coupled` on falls (v32) can lead, follow, or interleave on precedence (v35), and a pair with no same-step edge at all (every crossing on a different step) can be the *strongest* leader — the directional and same-step relations are independent reads of the same `floors[]` matrix. A consumer wanting "which two fronts move together?" runs v32/v33/v34; one wanting "which front moves first?" runs v35; the link is exact (each pair's `co_crossings` equals v34's `joint_moves`).
- **Not the synchrony command (v25).** v25 counts, per step, how many fronts crossed the floor together — a per-step scalar, gated on a single synchronized step. v35 reads, per pair, the *order* of crossings across the deal — a per-pair relation, gated on a pair with a consistent first-mover. Their link is the totals: summed over all pairs, v35's `total_co_crossings` equals `Σ_t C(crossing_t, 2)`, the same per-step synchrony v25 reports.
- **Not a same-step ordering (who crossed earlier *within* one transition).** A transition is atomic — two fronts crossing in the same step have no order (a `co_crossing` tie, which counts *against* a lead). v35's precedence is strictly *between* transitions: a comparison orders one front's crossing-step index against the other's, and a tie is neither a lead nor a follow.
- **Not a configurable "leads at least K times" gate.** v35 does not take a `--min-leads K` knob; the gate fires on a strict majority of the comparisons (`max(a_leads, b_leads) × 2 > comparisons`), the one ordering condition whose meaning needs no tuning. (v20–v34 made the same call.)
- **Not a minimum-comparison gate.** A pair with one ordered comparison and no ties reads `a_leads` 1 / `comparisons` 1 / `affinity` 1.0 / `leading` — honestly, the one comparison the two made, one crossed first. (Mirrors v32/v33/v34's "both-once → 100%/coupled" call.)
- **Not a per-front reduction.** Like v32/v33/v34, v35's primary object is an unordered **pair**, not a front. The `leader` names *which* front of the pair crossed first more often; it is a property of the pairing, not a per-front rank.
- **Not a precedence artifact.** v35 introduces no new on-disk format; the report is _derived_, recomputed on demand from the N auditable coherence inputs.
- **Not a sequence the command discovers.** It takes the artifacts **in round order** on the argv, exactly as the eighteen trend/exposure/persistence/… commands do.
- **Not a browser surface.** An N-round pairwise precedence of uploaded artifacts is a CI/dashboard concern (mirroring v16–v34).

## §3. The posture filter (unchanged)

1. **Deterministic** — `computeCoherencePrecedence` over N `PostureCoherence` objects is pure: it matches fronts by dimension, pins fronts by `localeCompare(_, "en")` (the same order the trajectory/exposure/concurrency/affinity functions use), enumerates unordered pairs in that pinned order (`dimension_a < dimension_b`), counts each pair's lead/follow/tie comparisons by ordering one front's crossing-step list against the other's, and picks the most-leading affinity by **integer cross-multiplication** (`leads × comparisons` of the two pairs), never a float comparison. Identical artifacts in identical order → identical `precedence_hash` on any machine.
2. **Honest about unstated data** — a front no document states in a round does not _cross_ into or out of that round: silence is neither a fall nor a recovery (the same §3 contract that keeps `below → unstated → below` zero crossings in v24/v25/v34). A crossing revealed after a silent gap is attributed to the transition into the round that reveals the new standing — never an invented crossing on the silent step. A pair the two never crossed in a *different* step (no ordered comparison — one never crossed, or they always crossed together) has no lead-lag edge and is omitted from `pairs[]`, never ranked.
3. **Advisory** — the command names which front the counterparty moved first across the team's own floor, and how reliably. It asserts no legal conclusion.
4. **No server** — N local files in, one report out. No socket, no engine run.
5. **Additive** — a brand-new subcommand and one new pure module. Every existing command (`analyze`, `diff`, `compare`, `compare-coherence`, `coherence-trend`, …, `coherence-opposition`, `verify`) is byte-for-byte unchanged in _output and goldens_; `coherence-sequence.ts` is imported and reused **without modification** (v35 needs nothing exported that was not already public — like v19–v34, it touches no existing source file's behavior at all).

# Part I — The report (pure)

`src/report/coherence-precedence.ts` (a new sibling to `coherence-opposition.ts`):

- **`PrecedenceClass`** — a pair's lead-lag coupling class: `leading` (one front crossed the floor *before* the other for a strict majority of the comparisons — `max(a_leads, b_leads) × 2 > comparisons` — a consistent first-mover, the gate-worthy class), `interleaved` (the two crossed in mixed order with no strict-majority first-mover, including an exact split or a pair whose comparisons are dominated by same-step ties). Only pairs with ≥ 1 *ordered* comparison (`a_leads + b_leads ≥ 1`) are classified; a pair the two never crossed in a different step has no lead-lag edge and is omitted.
- **`CoherencePrecedencePair`** — one unordered pair's whole-sequence lead-lag relation: the `dimension_a`/`dimension_b` (pinned, `a < b`), the `a_leads`/`b_leads` (comparisons A/B crossed first), the `co_crossings` (same-step ties), the `comparisons` (`a_leads + b_leads + co_crossings`), the `leader` (the front that crossed first more often, `null` on an exact split), the `affinity` (`max(a_leads, b_leads) / comparisons`), and its `class`.
- **`CoherencePrecedence`** — the whole-sequence reduction: `rounds` (count), `pairs[]` (the lead-lag pairs above, pinned by `(dimension_a, dimension_b)`), `class_counts` (the per-pair tally by class), `total_ordered_comparisons` (every pair's ordered comparisons summed), `total_co_crossings` (every pair's same-step ties summed — equal to `Σ_t C(crossing_t, 2)`), `max_affinity` (the most-leading coupling across the deal, `null` when no pair has a leader), `most_leading_pair` (the `[dimension_a, dimension_b]` owning `max_affinity`, earliest on a tie, `null` when none), `leading_front` (the leader of that pair, `null` when none), `leads` (any pair a strict-majority lead-lag — the gate verdict), and `precedence_hash`.
- **`computeCoherencePrecedence(rounds: PostureCoherence[])`** — the pure, IO-free core. For each front, derives its crossing-step list (the v24 events, silence-skipping, §3); for each unordered pair, orders A's crossing steps against B's into `a_leads`/`b_leads`/`co_crossings`, computes the comparisons, the affinity, and the strict-majority class; selects the most-leading affinity by integer cross-multiplication, returning the reduction with a `precedence_hash`. Requires ≥ 2 rounds.
- **`exposureLeads(precedence)`** — the CI gate predicate: `precedence.leads`. The directional pairwise verdict no other command exposes (v32/v33/v34 gate on same-step couplings; v25/v29 on per-step counts).
- **`buildCoherencePrecedenceJson(precedence)`** / **`renderCoherencePrecedenceSummary(precedence)`** — the JSON (`schema: vaulytica.posture-precedence.v1`) and human-readable renderers. The summary prints the most-leading verdict (the leader, its follower, and its lead share, or none-leading when no front ever consistently crossed first, plus the class tally), then one line per lead-lag pair (`leading` pairs first), then the hash.

v35 imports only the already-public `PostureCoherence` type, the `NegotiationTier` type, and the shared hashing helpers; the `below-acceptable` literal is a plain string; no existing source file changes.

# Part II — The command (headless)

`tools/cli/coherence-precedence.ts` (a new sibling to `coherence-opposition.ts`):

- **`computeCoherencePrecedenceArtifacts(texts, format?)`** — the pure CLI core. Verifies all N artifacts and runs the cross-ladder guard via the shared `verifyCoherenceSequence` loader (unchanged from v18–v34); on any malformed/tampered round returns `{ ok: false, errors }` (each prefixed `round N:`); on a cross-ladder pair returns `{ ok: false }` naming the two rounds; an unpinned artifact yields `{ ok: true }` with a `ladderNote`. Then `computeCoherencePrecedence(rounds)` and renders (markdown summary or JSON), returning `{ ok: true, output, leads, ladderNote }`.
- **`runCoherencePrecedence(argv)`** — the CLI handler: reads the N files, calls the core, writes the `ladderNote` (if any) to stderr, prints the report to stdout, and — under `--fail-on-leading-front` — exits 2 when any pair has a front that crossed first for a strict majority of the comparisons. A malformed/tampered/cross-ladder input is a hard exit-1 error. Requires ≥ 2 positionals.

`tools/cli/run.ts` (the dispatcher) gains a `coherence-precedence` case and a `USAGE` entry.

```
# Each round, archive its coherence (kilobytes, no clause text):
vaulytica analyze 'round1/*.docx' --playbook-file team.playbook.json --posture --emit-coherence round1.coherence.json
vaulytica analyze 'round2/*.docx' --playbook-file team.playbook.json --posture --emit-coherence round2.coherence.json
vaulytica analyze 'round3/*.docx' --playbook-file team.playbook.json --posture --emit-coherence round3.coherence.json

# Later — read/gate which front the counterparty moves first within a pair, from the archive alone:
vaulytica coherence-precedence round1.coherence.json round2.coherence.json round3.coherence.json \
  --fail-on-leading-front
```

# Part XVI — Principled deferrals

- **A `coherence-opposition --ordered` flag.** ⬜ Not built — and deliberately not, mirroring v18–v34 Part XVI. v35 is a separate command, not a flag on the counter-move command, so each command keeps exactly one gate and one hash.
- **A configurable "leads at least K times" gate (`--min-leads K`).** ⬜ Not built. The gate fires on a strict majority of the comparisons (`max(a_leads, b_leads) × 2 > comparisons`), the one ordering condition whose meaning needs no tuning. `affinity` and `max_affinity` are in the JSON for a consumer that wants a different bar.
- **A minimum-comparison floor for the gate (`--min-comparisons N`).** ⬜ Not built. A pair with one ordered comparison reads 100% / leading — honestly, the one comparison the two made, one crossed first. `comparisons` is in the JSON for a consumer wanting to discount thin evidence.
- **A direction-resolved precedence (who *falls* first vs who *recovers* first).** ⬜ Not built. v35 orders crossings of either direction (a fall or a recovery), matching v24's direction-blind crossing count. A fall-only lead-lag ("which front does the counterparty *concede* first?") and a recovery-only one ("which front does it *restore* first?") are two further reads over the same crossing-step lists, split by direction (the v29 split); deferred until a consumer asks, exactly as v34 deferred a direction-resolved opposition.
- **A mean-latency precedence (how *long* the leader leads, in rounds).** ⬜ Not built. v35 counts *whether* A's crossing index is lower, not *by how much*. A mean lead-time (the average round gap between the leader's crossing and the follower's) is a magnitude read over the same step indices; deferred (it adds a float aggregate the integer-exact hash would have to omit).
- **A transitive lead-lag chain (A leads B leads C ⇒ A leads C).** ⬜ Not built. v35 reports each unordered pair independently; composing the pairwise leaders into a deal-wide ordering (a topological rank of the fronts) is a graph reduction over `pairs[]`; a consumer wanting it joins the pairs themselves.
- **A standalone precedence artifact (`--emit-precedence`).** ⬜ Deferred, for the same reason v14–v34 keep the derived thing derived: the report is cheaply recomputable from the N coherence artifacts on demand.
- **A full pairwise matrix (every pair, including no-ordered-comparison pairs).** ⬜ Not built. `pairs[]` lists only pairs with `a_leads + b_leads ≥ 1` (the lead-lag edges); a pair the two never crossed in a different step carries no ordering signal.
- **A directory/glob walker that infers round order.** ⬜ Not built. The command takes artifacts in round order on the argv (the caller's contract, mirroring the eighteen trend/exposure/persistence/… commands).
- **A browser surface for an N-round precedence.** ⬜ Deferred (v16–v34 Part XVI). The browser does an in-session two-round comparison; an N-artifact pairwise precedence is a CI/dashboard concern.

# Part XVII — Open questions for the maintainer

1. **Report the second- and third-most-leading pairings, not just the top one?** Today `max_affinity` / `most_leading_pair` / `leading_front` headline the single most-leading coupling; the full ranked list is in `pairs[]` (sorted by pair label, not affinity). Recommendation: **keep the headline a single pair** — the list is in the JSON; add a ranked-by-affinity view only if a dashboard asks to pin it (the pair order stays label-pinned for a stable hash).
2. **Gate on a count of leading pairs, not just the verdict?** Today `leads` is a boolean and `class_counts.leading` is the count; the gate fires on `leads`. Recommendation: **defer a `--min-leading-pairs K` knob** — the count is in the JSON; add the knob only if a dashboard asks to pin it.

# Part XVIII — What this gives the user

- **The early-warning structure of the deal, from the archive alone.** Archive each round's kilobyte coherence artifact as you go; `coherence-precedence` shows not just _which two fronts the counterparty moves together_ (v32/v33/v34) but _which front it moves **first**_ — the directional pairwise read, distinguishing a leader (the same front crossing the floor ahead of its partner, deal after deal — an early indicator) from an interleaved pair (mixed order, no first-mover), which v32/v33/v34 (same-step) and v25/v29 (per-step counts) report identically, with no clause text checked out and no re-analysis run. The "which front should I watch to anticipate the next concession?" question a deal lead asks, answered deterministically from N small, verifiable files.
- **A gate on a stable lead-lag, not a single early crossing.** `--fail-on-leading-front` fires when a pair has a front that crossed first for a strict majority of the comparisons — a relation that holds _across_ the deal. It catches the precedence that a per-step count (v25/v29), a per-front count (v24), and the same-step affinities (v32/v33/v34) structurally cannot pose.
- **The same five promises.** Deterministic, no AI, no server, citable, never drafts — every line of v35 passes the §3 gate. It reads the binding floor v12 already derives, the `below-acceptable` rung v10 already defines, and the crossing events v24 already detects, through the loader v18–v34 already share; it adds one pairwise ordered comparison over the crossings v24 already counts, no on-disk format, and no change to any existing source file's behavior — every existing surface is byte-for-byte unchanged.
