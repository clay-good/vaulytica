# Vaulytica v28 — Document-Free Exposure Recovery Latency (Rounds Below Floor per Episode)

> **Status:** **Shipped (9.25.0).** v24 gave the posture archive a _per-front crossing-count_ axis (`crossings`); v25 re-bucketed those crossings _per step_ (`crossing_fronts`); v26 read the **index of the last one** (`settling_round`); v27 read the **index of the first one** (`onset_round`). All four reduce the crossings to a count or a single index — and a count and an index both throw away the **gap between a fall and the recovery that closes it**. A deal lead reviewing a close asks a question none of them can pose: per front, **once it fell below the floor, how many rounds did it stay there before it recovered — and did it ever recover at all?** A front that fell in round 2 and recovered in round 3 was exposed for one round; a front that fell in round 2 and recovered in round 6 was exposed for four — yet to v24 both have the **same** crossing count (two), to v26 the same _last_ crossing might fall on the same round, and to v27 the same _first_ crossing falls on the same round. None reports the **duration** of the exposure between the fall and its recovery. That is a missing **axis**: the _recovery-latency_ dimension of the same crossing data — the rounds between a paired fall and recovery. v28 supplies it: pair each fall crossing with the recovery crossing that closes it, and report the **rounds the front sat below floor in that episode** (`latency`), the **slowest recovery** across the deal (`max_latency`), and — the one tuning-free verdict — whether a front fell and **never recovered** (an unrecovered episode, an unbounded latency). It continues the global step numbering after v27's Step 207, beginning at **Step 208**. One new subcommand, one pure module, **zero** change to any existing source file.
> **Scope:** one idea — the _recovery-latency_ reduction the per-front crossing count (v24), the per-step crossing count (v25), the _last_-crossing index (v26), and the _first_-crossing index (v27) all leave out. v24 answers _how many times did a front cross the floor_ (a per-front sum); v26/v27 answer _which round did the package last/first cross_ (the **max**/**min** index); v28 answers _how long did a front stay below the floor between a fall and its recovery_ (the **gap** between a paired pair of crossings). `total_crossings` equals v24's grand total, v25/v26/v27's per-step sum, by construction — v28 reads the same crossings, _paired_, for the rounds _between_ each fall and its recovery, not _how many there are_ or _where they fall_. The use case is the same archive of per-round coherence artifacts: v24 says "the Cap front crossed the floor twice over the deal"; v28 says "and those two crossings were a fall in round 2 and a recovery in round 5 — it sat below floor for three rounds before it came back."
> **Posture (unchanged, non-negotiable):** deterministic (the latency is a pure reduction over the same `below-acceptable` rung v10 defines, taken over the binding floors v12 already derived and v20/v22/v24/v25/v26/v27 already read; same artifacts → identical `latency_hash`, on any machine, forever), no AI / no probabilistic path, no server (N local files in, one report out; no socket), citable (the artifacts carry the same per-front binding floors v12 derived from each document's own clause and the team's own playbook — v28 adds no new claim), lints / references / positions — but never drafts, and never renders a legal conclusion. The command is **advisory and verifiable**: every artifact is hash-verified (a tampered round is a hard error, side-labeled by round index), and the ladders are checked for a match across the _whole_ sequence (any cross-ladder pair is refused) via the same `verifyCoherenceSequence` loader the eleven trend/exposure/persistence/breadth/recurrence/volatility/synchrony/settling/onset commands use, unchanged. Every step passes the §3 filter or it does not ship.

# Part 0 — Intent

## §1. Why we're doing this

v24 gave the posture archive a per-front crossing-count axis — per front, how many times its standing crossed the acceptable floor (`crossings`), falls and recoveries alike. v25 re-bucketed those crossings by step. v26 located the **last** one (`settling_round`); v27 located the **first** one (`onset_round`). Every one of them reduces the crossings to a count or a single index. None can say _how long the gap between a fall and its recovery was_. A deal lead reviewing a close asks a question a count and an index both discard: **once a front fell below the floor, how many rounds did it sit there before it recovered — and did it ever recover?**

The crossing count cannot answer it, because a count is blind to _which_ crossings pair with which. Two fronts v24 holds as identical can be opposites on this axis:

- **The prompt recovery.** Cap falls below floor in round 2 and recovers in round 3. Two crossings — but it was exposed for a **single round**; the fall was caught and reversed at the next exchange.
- **The slow recovery.** Cap falls below floor in round 2 and recovers in round 6. Also two crossings (same `crossings`, possibly the same `onset_round` and `settling_round` depending on the other fronts) — yet it sat below floor for **four rounds** before it came back. To v24 this is the same as the prompt recovery; to v28 it is four times the exposure.

To v24 these are indistinguishable (same crossing count). To v26/v27 they may agree (same last/first crossing index). None of the existing commands reports the one fact that separates a fall that was caught at the next exchange from one that festered for rounds: the **number of rounds the front sat below floor between its fall and its recovery**, and — the limiting case — whether the front **never recovered** (an unbounded latency).

This is not a bug in v24 — a _crossing count_ is supposed to count, not measure gaps. It is a missing **axis**: the _recovery-latency_ dimension of the same crossing data. Where v24 sums crossings down the front axis, v25 sums them down the step axis, and v26/v27 read the max/min crossing index, v28 **pairs** each fall crossing with the recovery crossing that closes it and reads the **gap between them**.

v28 supplies it. Pair the same floor crossings v24/v25/v26/v27 count — a fall (at-or-above → below) with the next recovery (below → at-or-above) on the same front — and report, per front, each episode's **latency** (the rounds it sat below floor: `recovery_round − fall_round`), the deal's **slowest recovery** (`max_latency`, the longest such gap), and whether any front fell and **never recovered** (`open_count` — an episode left open at the close, an unbounded latency). The gate fires on an unrecovered episode — the one time condition whose meaning ("a front fell below the floor and never came back") needs no tuning. All of this is a reduction of the crossings derivable from the `floors[]` array v22/v24/v25/v26/v27 already carry — the sum of every front's crossings still equals v24's grand crossing total.

## §2. What v28 is and is not

**It is:**

- A **document-free recovery-latency command.** `coherence-latency <r1.coherence.json> … <rN.coherence.json>` reads N ≥ 2 saved coherence artifacts in round order, verifies each, runs the cross-ladder guard across all of them, and prints, per front, its below-floor **episodes** paired from its crossings (each a `fall_round` → `recovery_round` with its `latency` in rounds, `recovery_round` `null` when the front never recovered), the deal's **slowest recovery** (`max_latency` and the `slowest_dimension` that owns it, `null` when no front ever recovered), the **recovered-** and **unrecovered-episode** counts (`recovered_count`, `open_count`), and the **grand crossing total** (`total_crossings`, equal to v24's per-front sum and v25/v26/v27's per-step sum). Markdown (default) or `--format json`. No documents are read; the engine is never run.
- A **single deal-level latency gate.** `--fail-on-unrecovered-exposure` exits 2 when at least one front fell below the floor in-sequence and **never recovered** (`open_count > 0`) — an episode left open at the close, the limiting case of recovery latency (unbounded). This is the _recovery-latency_ counterpart to v24's _per-front instability_ gate, v26's _still-moving-at-the-close_ gate, and v27's _already-moving-at-the-open_ gate: where those fire on a count or an index, this fires on the single fact that a fall was **never closed by a recovery**. A front that fell and recovered — however slowly — clears it; a front that fell and stayed down trips it. It needs no tuning — an unrecovered fall _is_ an unbounded latency.
- A **whole-sequence-verified input, sharing the v17–v27 front end.** Every artifact's `coherence_hash` is re-derived and checked on load (a tampered/corrupt round is a hard error, errors prefixed `round N:`). The v15/v16 cross-ladder guard runs across the sequence via the same `verifyCoherenceSequence` loader the eleven trend/exposure/persistence/breadth/recurrence/volatility/synchrony/settling/onset commands use, with no change to it.
- A **derived report carrying its own fingerprint.** The latency carries a `latency_hash` (a stable SHA-256 over the canonical per-front episode set), namespaced apart from every `coherence_hash`, `movement_hash`, `trajectory_hash`, `shift_trajectory_hash`, `arc_hash`, `exposure_hash`, `persistence_hash`, `breadth_hash`, `recurrence_hash`, `volatility_hash`, `synchrony_hash`, `settling_hash`, and `onset_hash`, so the same N artifacts in the same order always reproduce the same report.

**It is not:**

- **Not a new diff, classifier, predicate, or artifact format.** It reuses the `weakest_tier` binding floor v12 derives and the `below-acceptable` rung v10 defines; v28 is a subcommand plus one pure module (`computeCoherenceLatency`) whose only new logic is pairing each fall crossing with the recovery that closes it over the same `floors[]` array v24/v25/v26/v27 scan. `parsePostureCoherenceJson`, the coherence schema, and every trajectory/exposure/persistence/breadth/recurrence/volatility/synchrony/settling/onset function are unchanged.
- **Not the persistence command.** v21 sums **how many rounds each front sat below floor over the whole deal** (`rounds_below`) and reports whether it is _currently_ below (`open`), regardless of crossings — a front stated below from round 1 has a large `rounds_below` and is `open` to v21. v28 measures the rounds below **per episode, between a paired fall and its recovery**, and counts only episodes that began with an in-sequence **fall** crossing: a front stated below from round 1 (no fall crossing in-sequence) contributes **no** episode to v28 — its descent happened before the archive begins, so v28 has no fall to pair. v21 answers _how long below in total, and is it below now_; v28 answers _how long was each fall-to-recovery gap, and did the fall ever close_.
- **Not a crossing-count command.** v24 counts a front's crossings over the deal; v25 counts the fronts crossing in each step. v28 does not headline a count — it headlines a **duration** (the rounds between a fall and its recovery). Their totals agree (`total_crossings`).
- **Not the settling or onset command.** v26 reads the **last** crossing index, v27 the **first**. v28 reads the **gap** between a fall and the recovery that closes it. They are reductions of the same crossings — `total_crossings` agrees — on orthogonal axes (index vs duration). A consumer wanting the first/last crossing runs v27/v26; one wanting the rounds-below-per-episode runs v28; one wanting all runs all and joins on the shared `floors[]` matrix.
- **Not silence-makes-an-exposure-longer.** A round no document states does **not** count as a round below floor and does **not** extend an episode: per §3 (the v21/v24/v25/v26/v27 contract), silence keeps the last known standing — it is neither a recovery nor a fall. An episode's `latency` is `recovery_round − fall_round` over the caller's round indices; a silent round inside the gap is part of that span (the front's last stated standing was below), but a crossing across a silent gap is attributed to the transition _into the round that reveals the new standing_, never to the silent step. This keeps v28 from inventing — or mislocating — a fall or a recovery at a silent round.
- **Not a configurable "recover within N rounds" gate.** v28 does not take a `--max-latency N` knob; the gate fires on an **unrecovered** episode, the one latency condition whose meaning ("the fall never closed") needs no tuning. A team wanting to flag a _slow but recovered_ episode reads `max_latency` and the per-episode `latency` from the JSON. (v20–v27 made the same call against a configurable floor/duration/breadth/episode/crossing count/simultaneity/quiet tail/lead-in.)
- **Not a trajectory-whipsaw command.** v17's `whipsaw` fires on rung jitter anywhere on the ladder, including movement entirely _above_ the floor. v28's latency is specific to the **floor boundary**: an above-floor whipsaw (`acceptable → ideal → acceptable`) crosses the floor zero times, so it pairs no fall with a recovery and contributes no episode.
- **Not a latency artifact.** v28 introduces no new on-disk format; the latency is _derived_, recomputed on demand from the N auditable coherence inputs — the same "keep the derived thing derived" discipline v14–v27 hold.
- **Not a sequence the command discovers.** It does not glob a directory or sort filenames; the caller passes the artifacts **in round order** on the argv, exactly as the eleven trend/exposure/persistence/breadth/recurrence/volatility/synchrony/settling/onset commands do.
- **Not a browser surface.** An N-round latency of uploaded artifacts is a CI/dashboard concern, which is what this command serves (mirroring v16–v27).

## §3. The posture filter (unchanged)

1. **Deterministic** — `computeCoherenceLatency` over N `PostureCoherence` objects is pure: it matches fronts by dimension, pins fronts by `localeCompare(_, "en")` (the same order the trajectory/exposure/volatility/synchrony/settling/onset functions use), and pairs each front's fall with the recovery that closes it over the caller's round order. Identical artifacts in identical order → identical `latency_hash` on any machine.
2. **Honest about unstated data** — a front no document states in a round does not cross into or out of that round. Within a front's path, an unstated round is skipped: it neither counts as a crossing nor resets the standing (silence is not a fall or a recovery — the §3 contract that keeps `unstated` never-flagged in v20 and `below → unstated → below` one episode in v23). A crossing across a silent gap is attributed to the transition into the round that _reveals_ the new standing; an episode that begins before the archive (a front stated below from round 1) has no in-sequence fall to pair, so it contributes no episode.
3. **Advisory** — the command names how many rounds the team's own floor stayed crossed between a fall and its recovery. It asserts no legal conclusion.
4. **No server** — N local files in, one report out. No socket, no engine run.
5. **Additive** — a brand-new subcommand and one new pure module. Every existing command (`analyze`, `diff`, `compare`, `compare-coherence`, `coherence-trend`, `coherence-shift-trend`, `coherence-arc`, `coherence-exposure`, `coherence-persistence`, `coherence-breadth`, `coherence-recurrence`, `coherence-volatility`, `coherence-synchrony`, `coherence-settling`, `coherence-onset`, `verify`) is byte-for-byte unchanged in _output and goldens_; `coherence-sequence.ts` is imported and reused **without modification** (v28 needs nothing exported that was not already public — like v19–v27, it touches no existing source file's behavior at all).

# Part I — The latency (pure)

`src/report/coherence-latency.ts` (a new sibling to `coherence-onset.ts`):

- **`LatencyClass`** — a front's recovery-latency class: `open` (it fell below floor in-sequence and never recovered — an unbounded latency), `recovered` (every in-sequence fall closed with a recovery — ≥ 1 closed episode, none open), `steady` (stated, but never fell below floor in-sequence — no fall crossing to pair, including a front that recovered from a pre-archive exposure), `unstated` (no document ever stated the front, §3).
- **`CoherenceLatencyEpisode`** — one fall-to-recovery span: the `fall_round` (the 1-based round the front was first seen below floor), the `recovery_round` (the 1-based round it was first seen back at-or-above floor; `null` when it never recovered), and the `latency` (`recovery_round − fall_round` — the rounds it sat below floor; `null` when open).
- **`CoherenceLatencyFront`** — one negotiation front's whole-sequence recovery structure: the `dimension`, the `floors[]` path (for context), the `crossings` (its total floor crossings — equal to v24's per-front `crossings`), the `episodes[]` (the fall-to-recovery spans above, in sequence order), the `max_latency` (its longest closed episode, `null` when none recovered), and its `latency` class.
- **`CoherenceLatency`** — the whole-sequence reduction: `rounds` (count), `fronts[]` (the fronts above, `localeCompare`-pinned), `class_counts` (the per-front tally by class), `total_crossings` (every front's crossing summed — equal to v24's, v25's, v26's, and v27's total), `recovered_count` (closed episodes across all fronts), `open_count` (unrecovered episodes across all fronts), `max_latency` (the slowest recovery across the deal — the longest closed episode, `null` when none recovered), `slowest_dimension` (the front owning `max_latency`, `null` when none recovered), `unrecovered` (any front has an open episode), and `latency_hash`.
- **`computeCoherenceLatency(rounds: PostureCoherence[])`** — the pure, IO-free core. For each front, scans `floors[]`, attributes each _stated_ floor crossing to the transition that reveals it (an unstated round is skipped, §3), pairs each fall with the next recovery into episodes, computes each closed episode's latency, locates the slowest recovery and any unrecovered episode, and returns the reduction with a `latency_hash`. Requires ≥ 2 rounds.
- **`exposureUnrecovered(latency)`** — the CI gate predicate: `latency.open_count > 0`. The recovery-latency verdict no other command exposes (v24 gates on a single front's crossing count; v26 on the last crossing falling on the final step; v27 on the first crossing falling on the opening step; this gates on a fall that **never closed with a recovery** — distinct from v21's `open`, which fires on the _current standing_ below floor regardless of whether the front ever fell in-sequence).
- **`buildCoherenceLatencyJson(latency)`** / **`renderCoherenceLatencySummary(latency)`** — the JSON (`schema: vaulytica.posture-latency.v1`) and human-readable renderers. The summary prints the slowest-recovery verdict (the front and its rounds-below, or steady-throughout when nothing fell-and-recovered, plus the unrecovered count), then one line per front with an episode, then the hash.

v28 imports only the already-public `PostureCoherence` type, the `NegotiationTier` type, and the shared hashing helpers; the `below-acceptable` literal is a plain string; no existing source file changes.

# Part II — The command (headless)

`tools/cli/coherence-latency.ts` (a new sibling to `coherence-onset.ts`):

- **`computeCoherenceLatencyArtifacts(texts, format?)`** — the pure CLI core. Verifies all N artifacts and runs the cross-ladder guard via the shared `verifyCoherenceSequence` loader (unchanged from v18–v27); on any malformed/tampered round returns `{ ok: false, errors }` (each prefixed `round N:`); on a cross-ladder pair returns `{ ok: false }` naming the two rounds; an unpinned artifact yields `{ ok: true }` with a `ladderNote`. Then `computeCoherenceLatency(rounds)` and renders (markdown summary or JSON), returning `{ ok: true, output, unrecovered, ladderNote }`.
- **`runCoherenceLatency(argv)`** — the CLI handler: reads the N files, calls the core, writes the `ladderNote` (if any) to stderr, prints the latency to stdout, and — under `--fail-on-unrecovered-exposure` — exits 2 when any front fell and never recovered. A malformed/tampered/cross-ladder input is a hard exit-1 error. Requires ≥ 2 positionals.

`tools/cli/run.ts` (the dispatcher) gains a `coherence-latency` case and a `USAGE` entry.

```
# Each round, archive its coherence (kilobytes, no clause text):
vaulytica analyze 'round1/*.docx' --playbook-file team.playbook.json --posture --emit-coherence round1.coherence.json
vaulytica analyze 'round2/*.docx' --playbook-file team.playbook.json --posture --emit-coherence round2.coherence.json
vaulytica analyze 'round3/*.docx' --playbook-file team.playbook.json --posture --emit-coherence round3.coherence.json

# Later — read/gate how long each front sat below floor between a fall and its recovery, from the archive alone:
vaulytica coherence-latency round1.coherence.json round2.coherence.json round3.coherence.json \
  --fail-on-unrecovered-exposure
```

# Part XVI — Principled deferrals

- **A `coherence-onset --latency` flag.** ⬜ Not built — and deliberately not, mirroring v18–v27 Part XVI. v28 is a separate command, not a flag on the onset command, so each command keeps exactly one gate and one hash.
- **A configurable "recover within N rounds" gate (`--max-latency N`).** ⬜ Not built. The gate fires on an unrecovered episode (a fall that never closed), the one latency condition whose meaning needs no tuning. `max_latency` and the per-episode `latency` are in the JSON for a consumer that wants to flag a slow-but-recovered episode.
- **A standalone latency artifact (`--emit-latency`).** ⬜ Deferred, for the same reason v14–v27 keep the derived thing derived: the latency is cheaply recomputable from the N coherence artifacts on demand.
- **A _leading-recovery_ latency (the rounds a front was below before the archive began).** ⬜ Not built — and not computable: the fall that opened a pre-archive exposure is not in the sequence, so its latency is unknown. v28 reports only episodes whose fall is observed in-sequence (the honest subset); a front that recovered from a pre-archive exposure is `steady` (no in-sequence fall to pair). A consumer wanting the standing-duration regardless of the fall runs v21.
- **A _mean_ recovery latency (the average gap across episodes).** ⬜ Not built. v28 headlines the slowest recovery (`max_latency`) and the per-episode `latency`; a consumer wanting the mean reduces the `episodes[]` array. Add it if a dashboard asks to pin one.
- **A directory/glob walker that infers round order.** ⬜ Not built. The command takes artifacts in round order on the argv (the caller's contract, mirroring the trend/exposure/persistence/breadth/recurrence/volatility/synchrony/settling/onset commands).
- **A browser surface for an N-round latency.** ⬜ Deferred (v16–v27 Part XVI). The browser does an in-session two-round comparison; an N-artifact latency is a CI/dashboard concern.

# Part XVII — Open questions for the maintainer

1. **Fold latency, onset, and settling into a single "deal timeline" command?** Today the recovery-latency (v28), first-crossing (v27), and last-crossing (v26) reductions are split. A dashboard wanting all runs them and joins on the shared `floors[]` matrix (their totals agree by construction). Recommendation: **keep them split at the command layer** — they carry categorically different gates (per-deal "never recovered" vs "already moving at the open" vs "still moving at the close") and different hashes; bolting one onto another would give that command unlike gates. If a dashboard asks for the combined view, add a composing command the way v19 joined v17 and v18, not a flag.
2. **Report the latency of the _deal_ (the rounds between the first deal-wide fall and the last deal-wide recovery)?** Today the latency is per-front per-episode. A deal-wide span has a different meaning (some fronts overlap) with no consumer yet. Recommendation: **defer** — the per-front `episodes[]` plus the `floors[]` matrix already let a consumer compute a deal-wide span; add it if a dashboard asks to pin one.

# Part XVIII — What this gives the user

- **The recovery latency of the deal, from the archive alone.** Archive each round's kilobyte coherence artifact as you go; `coherence-latency` shows not just _how many times a front crossed the floor over the deal_ (v24), _which round the package last/first crossed_ (v26/v27), but _how many rounds each front sat below the floor between its fall and its recovery_ — distinguishing a fall caught at the next exchange (one round below) from one that festered for rounds, which v24 reports identically (same crossing count), with no clause text checked out and no re-analysis run. The "how long was each fall exposed, and did it ever come back?" question a deal lead asks reviewing a close, answered deterministically from N small, verifiable files.
- **A gate on the latency of a fall, not just the crossing count, level, or first/last move.** `--fail-on-unrecovered-exposure` fires when a front fell below the floor and never recovered — an unbounded latency — a condition the count gates (v23/v24/v25) and the index gates (v26/v27) structurally cannot pose, and distinct from v21's current-standing gate (which flags a front below now even if it never fell in-sequence).
