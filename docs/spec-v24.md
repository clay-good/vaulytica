# Vaulytica v24 — Document-Free Exposure Volatility (Per-Front Floor Crossings)

> **Status:** **Shipped (9.21.0).** v23 gave the posture archive an *episode-count* axis: per front, how many separate times it fell below the acceptable floor (`below_runs`). But `below_runs` counts only the *entries* into below-floor — it is blind to the *recoveries*. A front whose binding floor reads `acceptable → below → acceptable` (it fell once and cleanly recovered, never relapsing) reports `below_runs = 1`, `recurrence = single`, and **does not trip** v23's `--fail-on-recurring-exposure` (one episode is not churn); v21 reports it `resolved` (its latest stated floor recovered); v20 reports it `exposed` (it touched below floor once); v17 nets it `unchanged`. None of them says the one thing that distinguishes a front that *fell and bounced back* from a front that *never moved*: it **crossed the floor twice** — once down, once up. That is a missing **axis**: not *how low* (v20), not *how long* (v21), not *how broad* (v22), not *how many descents* (v23), but **how many times the front's standing flipped across the floor** — its *instability*, counting recoveries as well as falls. v24 supplies it: read the same N artifacts per front and count the **floor crossings** (`crossings`) — every *stated* transition between a below-floor round and an at-or-above-floor round, in either direction. A front that stayed on one side of the floor the whole deal (always below, or always at-or-above) has **zero** crossings (`stable`); a front that crossed once and never came back has **one** (`monotone`); a front that crossed two or more times reversed its standing at least once (`volatile`). It names the deal's **most volatile front** and gates, with no tuning, on any front whose standing crossed the floor **two or more** times (it both fell across and rose across — it could not settle). It continues the global step numbering after v23's Step 203, beginning at **Step 204**. One new subcommand, one pure module, **zero** change to any existing source file.
> **Scope:** one idea — the *crossing-count* reduction the episode count cannot see, because the episode count drops every recovery. v17/v18/v19 answer *which way did a front move*; v20 answers *how low did a front ever get*; v21 answers *how long was a front down, and is it still down*; v22 answers *how broad was the deal each round*; v23 answers *how many separate times did a front fall below floor*; v24 answers *how many times did a front's standing flip across the floor* — a count of boundary **crossings** in both directions, not a count of below-floor runs. The use case is the same archive of per-round coherence artifacts: v23 says "the Cap front fell below floor in two separate episodes"; v24 says "and the Term front, which v23 calls a *single* clean episode, actually crossed the floor **twice** — it fell and recovered — so it is not the rock-steady front the episode count makes it look like."
> **Posture (unchanged, non-negotiable):** deterministic (the crossing count is a pure per-front scan over the same `below-acceptable` rung v10 defines, taken over the binding floors v12 already derived and v20/v21/v23 already read; same artifacts → identical `volatility_hash`, on any machine, forever), no AI / no probabilistic path, no server (N local files in, one report out; no socket), citable (the artifacts carry the same per-front binding floors v12 derived from each document's own clause and the team's own playbook — v24 adds no new claim), lints / references / positions — but never drafts, and never renders a legal conclusion. The command is **advisory and verifiable**: every artifact is hash-verified (a tampered round is a hard error, side-labeled by round index), and the ladders are checked for a match across the *whole* sequence (any cross-ladder pair is refused) via the same `verifyCoherenceSequence` loader the seven trend/exposure/persistence/breadth/recurrence commands use, unchanged. Every step passes the §3 filter or it does not ship.

# Part 0 — Intent

## §1. Why we're doing this

v23 gave the posture archive an episode-count axis — per front, the number of *separate* times it fell below the acceptable floor (`below_runs`). That is the right shape for "did we re-open this front?" But `below_runs` counts only the **falls**. It is blind to the **recoveries** between them, and it cannot see a front that fell once and came back, because that is one episode — exactly like a front that fell once and stayed. A deal lead reconciling the close asks a third question neither the episode count nor the duration can answer: **how unstable was this front — how many times did its standing flip across the floor, recoveries included?**

v23 cannot answer it, because `below_runs` is a count of below-floor **runs**, and a run that *ends in a recovery* counts the same as a run that *never ends*. Two fronts the archive holds as different deals report identically to v23:

- **The stuck front.** Cap's binding floor reads `below-acceptable → below-acceptable → below-acceptable`. It fell in round 1 and never moved. `below_runs = 1` (one episode), `recurrence = single`. It never crossed the floor — it has been on the wrong side of it the entire deal. **Zero crossings.**
- **The bounced front.** Term's binding floor reads `acceptable → below-acceptable → acceptable`. It fell in round 2 and recovered in round 3. `below_runs = 1` (one episode), `recurrence = single` — the same as the stuck front. But this front **crossed the floor twice** — once down, once back up — a fall the team won back. **Two crossings.**

To v23 these are the same: one episode each, neither `recurring`, neither tripping `--fail-on-recurring-exposure`. To v21 the first is `open` and the second is `resolved`, which captures the *current standing* but not the *movement*: the second front *moved across the floor twice* and the first did not move at all. To v20 both are `exposed` (each touched below floor). None of the existing commands reports the one fact that separates a front that *bounced* from a front that *sat still*: the count of times its standing **crossed** the floor. To learn it from v23 a consumer must pull each front's `floors[]` array and hand-count the boundary transitions the episode count collapses.

This is not a bug in v23 — an episode count is *supposed* to count entries, not crossings. It is a missing **axis**: the *crossing-count* dimension of the same below-floor data. Where v23 counts how many times a front **entered** below floor, v24 counts how many times its standing **flipped across** the floor — falls and recoveries alike.

v24 supplies it. Read the same artifacts per front and count the **floor crossings** (`crossings`): every *stated* transition between a below-floor round and an at-or-above-floor round, in either direction. A front always on one side of the floor (always below, or always at-or-above) has zero crossings; a single fall, or a single recovery, is one crossing; a fall-and-recover, or a recover-and-relapse, is two; an oscillating front is three or more. The gate follows: `--fail-on-volatile-exposure` fires when any front's standing crossed the floor **two or more** times — it both fell across and rose across, so it reversed at least once — the one instability condition whose meaning needs no tuning. All of this is cheaply derivable from the `floors[]` array v21/v23 already carry.

## §2. What v24 is and is not

**It is:**
- A **document-free volatility command.** `coherence-volatility <r1.coherence.json> … <rN.coherence.json>` reads N ≥ 2 saved coherence artifacts in round order, verifies each, runs the cross-ladder guard across all of them, and prints, *per front*, the binding-floor path (`floors`), how many rounds it was below floor (`rounds_below`, for context), the count of **floor crossings** (`crossings`), and a `volatility` class — `volatile` (≥ 2 crossings — reversed across the floor), `monotone` (exactly 1 — crossed once, never back), `stable` (0, stated — never crossed), or `unstated` (§3) — plus the deal's **most volatile front** (`most_volatile_dimension` / `max_crossings`). Markdown (default) or `--format json`. No documents are read; the engine is never run.
- A **single deal-level volatility gate.** `--fail-on-volatile-exposure` exits 2 when any front's standing crossed the floor two or more times (`volatile_count > 0`). This is the *instability* counterpart to v23's *churn* gate and v21's *current-standing* gate: where `--fail-on-recurring-exposure` fires on a front that fell below floor in two or more episodes, `--fail-on-volatile-exposure` fires on a front whose standing reversed across the floor — it both fell and recovered at least once. A front that fell once and stayed (zero or one crossing) does not trip it; a front that fell once and cleanly recovered (two crossings, `single` episode to v23) **does**. It needs no tuning — two crossings is the threshold that *means* its standing reversed.
- A **whole-sequence-verified input, sharing the v17–v23 front end.** Every artifact's `coherence_hash` is re-derived and checked on load (a tampered/corrupt round is a hard error, errors prefixed `round N:`). The v15/v16 cross-ladder guard runs across the sequence via the same `verifyCoherenceSequence` loader the seven trend/exposure/persistence/breadth/recurrence commands use, with no change to it.
- A **derived report carrying its own fingerprint.** The volatility carries a `volatility_hash` (a stable SHA-256 over the canonical per-front set), namespaced apart from every `coherence_hash`, `movement_hash`, `trajectory_hash`, `shift_trajectory_hash`, `arc_hash`, `exposure_hash`, `persistence_hash`, `breadth_hash`, and `recurrence_hash`, so the same N artifacts in the same order always reproduce the same report.

**It is not:**
- **Not a new diff, classifier, predicate, or artifact format.** It reuses the `weakest_tier` binding floor v12 derives and the `below-acceptable` rung v10 defines; v24 is a subcommand plus one pure module (`computeCoherenceVolatility`) whose only new logic is a per-front scan that counts floor crossings over the same `floors[]` array v23 builds. `parsePostureCoherenceJson`, the coherence schema, and every trajectory/exposure/persistence/breadth/recurrence function are unchanged.
- **Not an episode command.** v23 counts below-floor *runs* (`below_runs`) — entries into below floor, recoveries discarded; v24 counts *crossings* — falls **and** recoveries. A front that fell once and recovered is `below_runs = 1` (one episode) to v23 but `crossings = 2` (volatile) to v24. A front that fell once and stayed is also `below_runs = 1` to v23 but `crossings = 0` (stable) to v24. v23 conflates them; v24 separates them. v24 carries no `below_runs` — its verdict is `crossings`. (`crossings` and `below_runs` relate by `crossings = 2·below_runs − [starts below] − [ends below]`, but v24 computes the count directly, not from the episode structure.)
- **Not a level command.** v24 does not report or gate on *which side* of the floor a front sat (that is v20's worst-floor and v21's current standing). A `stable` front may be stably *below* floor for all N rounds (zero crossings, the stuck front) or stably *at-or-above* it (also zero crossings, a front that never fell). v24 reports the *count of flips*, not the side; a consumer wanting the level runs v20, and one wanting both runs v20 and v24.
- **Not silence-splits-a-side.** A round no document states does **not** count as a crossing and does **not** reset the standing: per §3 (the v21/v23 contract), silence after a fall keeps the last known standing — it is neither a recovery nor a fall. So `below → unstated → below` is **zero** crossings (silence does not flip the side), and `below → unstated → acceptable` is **one** crossing (a real recovery, just unstated in the gap round). This keeps v24 from inventing a crossing out of a missing document.
- **Not a configurable-threshold "too jittery" classifier.** v24 does not take a `--fail-over N` crossings knob; the gate fires on `crossings ≥ 2`, the one instability condition whose meaning ("the front's standing reversed across the floor") needs no tuning. A team wanting to gate on a *higher* crossing count reads `max_crossings` from the JSON. (v20/v21/v22/v23 made the same call against a configurable floor/duration/breadth/episode count.)
- **Not a trajectory-whipsaw command.** v17's `whipsaw` fires when a front had any improving rung-step *and* any regressing rung-step *anywhere on the ladder* — including movement entirely *above* the floor (`acceptable → ideal → acceptable` is whipsaw, but it never touched the floor). v24's crossing count is specific to the **floor boundary**: that same above-floor whipsaw is `stable` (zero crossings) to v24. v24 isolates the instability that matters for exposure — flips across the floor — from rung jitter that never risks it.
- **Not a per-round command.** v22 fixes a round and counts fronts; v24 fixes a front and counts crossings. They are different reductions of the same matrix; a consumer wanting one round's standing reads v22, one wanting one front's instability reads v24.
- **Not a volatility artifact.** v24 introduces no new on-disk format; the volatility is *derived*, recomputed on demand from the N auditable coherence inputs — the same "keep the derived thing derived" discipline v14–v23 hold.
- **Not a sequence the command discovers.** It does not glob a directory or sort filenames; the caller passes the artifacts **in round order** on the argv, exactly as the seven trend/exposure/persistence/breadth/recurrence commands do.
- **Not a browser surface.** An N-round volatility of uploaded artifacts is a CI/dashboard concern, which is what this command serves (mirroring v16–v23).

## §3. The posture filter (unchanged)

1. **Deterministic** — `computeCoherenceVolatility` over N `PostureCoherence` objects is pure: it matches fronts by dimension, pins them by `localeCompare(_, "en")` (the same order the trajectory/exposure/persistence/recurrence functions use), and counts each front's floor crossings over the caller's round order. Identical artifacts in identical order → identical `volatility_hash` on any machine.
2. **Honest about unstated data** — a front no document states in *any* round is `unstated`, never `volatile`/`monotone`/`stable`. Within a front's path, an unstated round is skipped: it neither counts as a crossing nor resets the standing (silence is not a fall or a recovery — the §3 contract that keeps `unstated` never-flagged in v20 and `below → unstated → below` one episode in v23). A crossing is counted only between two *stated* rounds on opposite sides of the floor.
3. **Advisory** — the command names how many times each front's standing crossed the team's own floor. It asserts no legal conclusion.
4. **No server** — N local files in, one report out. No socket, no engine run.
5. **Additive** — a brand-new subcommand and one new pure module. Every existing command (`analyze`, `diff`, `compare`, `compare-coherence`, `coherence-trend`, `coherence-shift-trend`, `coherence-arc`, `coherence-exposure`, `coherence-persistence`, `coherence-breadth`, `coherence-recurrence`, `verify`) is byte-for-byte unchanged in *output and goldens*; `coherence-sequence.ts` is imported and reused **without modification** (v24 needs nothing exported that was not already public — like v19–v23, it touches no existing source file's behavior at all).

# Part I — The volatility (pure)

`src/report/coherence-volatility.ts` (a new sibling to `coherence-recurrence.ts`):

- **`VolatilityClass`** — a front's floor-crossing class: `volatile` (≥ 2 crossings — its standing reversed across the floor), `monotone` (exactly 1 crossing — it crossed once, never back), `stable` (0 crossings, stated — it never crossed the floor), `unstated` (no document ever stated the front).
- **`CoherenceVolatilityFront`** — one front's whole-sequence crossing structure: the `dimension`, `floors` (the binding floor at each round, `null` = unstated), `rounds_below` (the count of below-floor rounds, for context), `crossings` (the count of floor-boundary crossings — the verdict), and the `volatility` class.
- **`CoherenceVolatility`** — the whole-sequence per-front series: `rounds` (count), `fronts[]` (the fronts above, pinned by `localeCompare`), `class_counts` (per-front tally by class), `volatile_count` (fronts with ≥ 2 crossings — the gate-worthy count), `most_volatile_dimension` / `max_crossings` (the front with the most crossings, earliest on a tie — `null` / `0` when no front ever crossed the floor), and `volatility_hash`.
- **`computeCoherenceVolatility(rounds: PostureCoherence[])`** — the pure, IO-free core. For each front, scans `floors[]` and counts every *stated* transition across the floor boundary (an unstated round is skipped, §3), classifies the front, finds the most volatile front, and returns the series with a `volatility_hash`. Requires ≥ 2 rounds.
- **`exposureVolatile(volatility)`** — the CI gate predicate: `volatility.volatile_count > 0`. The crossing-instability verdict no other command exposes (v20/v21 gate on level/standing; v23 on entries; this gates on flips across the floor).
- **`buildCoherenceVolatilityJson(volatility)`** / **`renderCoherenceVolatilitySummary(volatility)`** — the JSON (`schema: vaulytica.posture-volatility.v1`) and human-readable renderers. The summary prints the class tally and the volatile count, then one line per **volatile** front (its crossing count and floor path), then one line per **monotone** front, then the hash.

v24 imports only the already-public `PostureCoherence` type, the `NegotiationTier` type, and the shared hashing helpers; the `below-acceptable` literal is a plain string; no existing source file changes.

# Part II — The command (headless)

`tools/cli/coherence-volatility.ts` (a new sibling to `coherence-recurrence.ts`):

- **`computeCoherenceVolatilityArtifacts(texts, format?)`** — the pure CLI core. Verifies all N artifacts and runs the cross-ladder guard via the shared `verifyCoherenceSequence` loader (unchanged from v18–v23); on any malformed/tampered round returns `{ ok: false, errors }` (each prefixed `round N:`); on a cross-ladder pair returns `{ ok: false }` naming the two rounds; an unpinned artifact yields `{ ok: true }` with a `ladderNote`. Then `computeCoherenceVolatility(rounds)` and renders (markdown summary or JSON), returning `{ ok: true, output, volatile, ladderNote }`.
- **`runCoherenceVolatility(argv)`** — the CLI handler: reads the N files, calls the core, writes the `ladderNote` (if any) to stderr, prints the volatility to stdout, and — under `--fail-on-volatile-exposure` — exits 2 when any front's standing crossed the floor twice or more. A malformed/tampered/cross-ladder input is a hard exit-1 error. Requires ≥ 2 positionals.

`tools/cli/run.ts` (the dispatcher) gains a `coherence-volatility` case and a `USAGE` entry.

```
# Each round, archive its coherence (kilobytes, no clause text):
vaulytica analyze 'round1/*.docx' --playbook-file team.playbook.json --posture --emit-coherence round1.coherence.json
vaulytica analyze 'round2/*.docx' --playbook-file team.playbook.json --posture --emit-coherence round2.coherence.json
vaulytica analyze 'round3/*.docx' --playbook-file team.playbook.json --posture --emit-coherence round3.coherence.json

# Later — read/gate whether any front's standing flipped across the floor twice or more, from the archive alone:
vaulytica coherence-volatility round1.coherence.json round2.coherence.json round3.coherence.json \
  --fail-on-volatile-exposure
```

# Part XVI — Principled deferrals

- **A `coherence-recurrence --crossings` flag.** ⬜ Not built — and deliberately not, mirroring v18/v19/v20/v21/v22/v23 Part XVI. v24 is a separate command, not a flag on the episode command, so each command keeps exactly one gate and one hash.
- **A configurable "too jittery" gate (`--fail-over N` crossings).** ⬜ Not built. The gate fires on `crossings ≥ 2` (the standing reversed), the one instability condition whose meaning needs no tuning. `max_crossings` is in the JSON for a consumer that wants to threshold on a higher crossing count.
- **A standalone volatility artifact (`--emit-volatility`).** ⬜ Deferred, for the same reason v14–v23 keep the derived thing derived: the volatility is cheaply recomputable from the N coherence artifacts on demand, keeping the auditable inputs (each ladder-pinned, hash-verified) as the source of truth.
- **A *direction-aware* crossing model (separate down-crossings from up-crossings).** ⬜ Not built. The `floors[]` path already lets a consumer split a front's crossings into falls and recoveries; v24 headlines the total count, since the instability question ("did the standing reverse?") needs only the total. Add it if a dashboard asks to pin the split.
- **A *crossing-rate* / normalized statistic (crossings per round).** ⬜ Not built. `crossings` and `rounds` are both in the JSON; a consumer wanting a rate computes it. A single normalized headline has no consumer yet.
- **A directory/glob walker that infers round order.** ⬜ Not built. The command takes artifacts in round order on the argv (the caller's contract, mirroring the trend/exposure/persistence/breadth/recurrence commands).
- **A browser surface for an N-round volatility.** ⬜ Deferred (v16–v23 Part XVI). The browser does an in-session two-round comparison; an N-artifact volatility is a CI/dashboard concern.

# Part XVII — Open questions for the maintainer

1. **Fold volatility into a single "deal posture" command that reports episodes *and* crossings?** Today the episode command (v23) and the crossing command (v24) are split. A dashboard wanting both runs both and joins on the shared `floors[]` matrix. Recommendation: **keep them split at the command layer** — they carry categorically different gates (episodes "recovered and relapsed" vs crossings "standing reversed, recoveries included") and different hashes; bolting one onto the other would give that command two unlike gates. If a dashboard asks for the combined view, add a consumer the same way v19 joined v17 and v18 — as its own composing command, not a flag.
2. **Report a *settling round* (the round after which a front never crossed the floor again)?** The `floors[]` path already lets a consumer find the last crossing; v24 does not headline it. Recommendation: **defer** — the path exposes it, and a single "settled at round K" headline has no consumer yet; add it if a dashboard asks to pin one.

# Part XVIII — What this gives the user

- **The instability of each front, from the archive alone.** Archive each round's kilobyte coherence artifact as you go; `coherence-volatility` shows, per front, not just *how many times it fell* (v23) but *how many times its standing flipped across the floor* — distinguishing a front that fell and bounced back (two crossings, instability v23 calls a single clean episode) from a front that fell and never moved (zero crossings, stuck) and from a front that jittered entirely above the floor (zero crossings, never at risk — instability v17 calls a whipsaw), with no clause text checked out and no re-analysis run. The "which front could not settle?" question a deal lead asks at the close, answered deterministically from N small, verifiable files.
- **A gate on instability, not just standing, level, or episode count.** `--fail-on-volatile-exposure` fires when a front's standing crossed the floor twice or more — it both fell across and rose across — the reverse-across-the-floor pattern the episode gate (v23) and duration gate (v21) structurally cannot pose, since one counts only entries and the other sums rounds. It catches the front that bounced even when it ended on the right side of the floor, and ignores the front that sat stably on the wrong side.
- **The same five promises.** Deterministic, no AI, no server, citable, never drafts — every line of v24 passes the §3 gate. It reads the binding floor v12 already derives and the `below-acceptable` rung v10 already defines, through the loader v18–v23 already share; it adds one per-front crossing count, no on-disk format, and no change to any existing source file's behavior — every existing surface is byte-for-byte unchanged.
