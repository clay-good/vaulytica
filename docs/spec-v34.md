# Vaulytica v34 — Document-Free Exposure Counter-Move Affinity (Pairwise Opposition / See-Saw Coupling)

> **Status:** **Shipped (9.31.0).** v32 and v33 took the posture family's first two **pairwise** reads, on the two _aligned_ directions: per unordered pair of fronts, how reliably the two _fell_ below floor **together** (v32 — the concession block) and how reliably the two _recovered_ **together** (v33 — the restoration block). Those are two of the four cells of the 2×2 of {front A falls / recovers} × {front B falls / recovers}. The off-diagonal is unread: across the deal, **which two fronts does the counterparty move in _opposite_ directions at once?** A step where Cap _falls_ exactly as Term _recovers_ is a _counter-move_ — a trade-off, the counterparty giving ground on one front precisely as it takes ground on another. The two fronts are _substitutes_, bargaining chips swapped against each other. v34 supplies that diagonal: for each unordered pair of fronts, how many round-transitions saw the two move **opposite** ways (`opposed_moves` — one fell while the other recovered), out of the transitions in which **both** crossed (`joint_moves` = `co_falls` + `co_recoveries` + `opposed_moves`), the resulting `affinity` (`opposed_moves / joint_moves`), the deal's **most-opposed pairing** (`max_affinity` / `most_opposed_pair`), and — the one tuning-free verdict — whether any pair counter-moved for a strict **majority** of the steps both crossed (`opposed`). It continues the global step numbering after v33's Step 213, beginning at **Step 214**. One new subcommand, one pure module, **zero** change to any existing source file.
> **Scope:** one idea — the off-diagonal completion of v32's co-fall and v33's co-recovery pairwise reads. v32 answers _which two fronts fall together_; v33 answers _which two recover together_; v34 answers _which two move opposite ways at once — the counterparty trading one against the other_. It reuses v29's exact **fall** and **recovery** events (silence-skipping per §3) — so a counter-move is one front registering a v29 fall while the other registers a v29 recovery in the same transition. By construction `total_opposed_moves` equals `Σ_t falling_t × recovering_t` (v29's per-step fall count times its recovery count, summed), and `total_aligned_moves` equals `total_co_falls` (v32) `+ total_co_recoveries` (v33) `= Σ_t [C(falling_t, 2) + C(recovering_t, 2)]`; the two totals sum to `Σ_t C(crossing_t, 2)` (v25's per-step crossing count choose-two'd). The use case is the same archive of per-round coherence artifacts: v32 says "across the deal, **Cap and Term** _fell_ together — conceded as a block"; v33 says "and **Fee and Risk** _recovered_ together — restored as a block"; v34 says "and **Cap and Fee** move _against_ each other — every time one fell, the other climbed back: the counterparty trades Cap for Fee and back."
> **Posture (unchanged, non-negotiable):** deterministic (the affinity is a pure per-pair overlap count over the same `below-acceptable` rung v10 defines, taken over the binding floors v12 already derived and v24/v25/v29/v32/v33 already read; the most-opposed pick is decided by integer cross-multiplication, never a float compare, so the same artifacts → identical `opposition_hash`, on any machine, forever), no AI / no probabilistic path, no server (N local files in, one report out; no socket), citable (the artifacts carry the same per-front binding floors v12 derived from each document's own clause and the team's own playbook — v34 adds no new claim), lints / references / positions — but never drafts, and never renders a legal conclusion. The command is **advisory and verifiable**: every artifact is hash-verified (a tampered round is a hard error, side-labeled by round index), and the ladders are checked for a match across the _whole_ sequence (any cross-ladder pair is refused) via the same `verifyCoherenceSequence` loader the seventeen trend/exposure/persistence/breadth/recurrence/volatility/synchrony/settling/onset/latency/concurrency/relapse/tenure/affinity/recovery-affinity commands use, unchanged. Every step passes the §3 filter or it does not ship.

# Part 0 — Intent

## §1. Why we're doing this

v32 and v33 gave the posture archive its first two pairwise reads — the two _aligned_ directions. v32: for each unordered pair, how reliably the two _fell_ below floor **together** (the concession block; `--fail-on-coupled-fronts`). v33: how reliably the two _recovered_ **together** (the restoration block; `--fail-on-coupled-recoveries`). Both read same-_direction_ overlaps: A and B moving the **same** way at once.

But two fronts can cross the same step moving _opposite_ ways, and neither v32 nor v33 reads it. v29 resolved each step into _falls_ and _recoveries_; v32 took the pairwise overlap of two falls, v33 of two recoveries. The diagonal is unread: across the sequence, **is it the same two fronts that keep moving _against_ each other?** v32 and v33 know nothing about it — a pair can fall together (v32 `coupled`) and never oppose, or oppose every joint step and never fall or recover together. The counter-move is a separate relation:

- **The see-saw.** Cap and Fee move opposite in rounds 3 and 5 — every joint step, one fell as the other recovered. The counterparty trades them: it concedes Cap exactly as it restores Fee, and back. A **substitution** linkage.
- **The aligned pair.** Cap and Term only ever fell together (v32) or recovered together (v33), never opposed. The same per-front crossing counts, but **no counter-movement**: the two move as a bloc, not against each other.

To v32 and v33 these are indistinguishable (both read same-direction overlaps). The difference is **pairwise on the opposed direction** — the missing diagonal of the same pairwise reduction. It matters because a stable counter-move pairing is _strategic information_: it tells a deal lead which fronts the counterparty treats as _substitutes_ — the trade to anticipate (a concession on one front is bought with a recovery on the other) or to refuse (do not let them swap). An aligned pairing (v32/v33) tells them the opposite — which fronts move as a bloc.

This is not a gap in v32 or v33 — a _co-fall_ / _co-recovery_ affinity is supposed to read aligned moves. It is the missing **off-diagonal direction** of the same pairwise reduction. v34 supplies it. Read the same artifacts per-pair on the opposed direction: for each unordered pair, how many transitions saw the two move opposite ways (the numerator), how many saw both cross (the denominator), the resulting affinity, the deal's most-opposed such pairing, and whether any pair counter-moved for a strict majority of the steps both crossed. The gate follows: `--fail-on-opposed-fronts` fires when a pair counter-moved _more often than it aligned_ — the one coupling boundary whose meaning ("they move against each other more often than together") needs no tuning. All of this is a reduction over the same `floors[]` crossings v24 counts, v25 buckets per step, v29 splits by direction, and v32/v33 pair on the aligned directions.

## §2. What v34 is and is not

**It is:**

- A **document-free counter-move affinity command.** `coherence-opposition <r1.coherence.json> … <rN.coherence.json>` reads N ≥ 2 saved coherence artifacts in round order, verifies each, runs the cross-ladder guard across all of them, and prints, per **pair** of fronts that ever counter-moved, how many transitions saw the two move opposite ways (`opposed_moves`), each pair's aligned moves split (`co_falls` / `co_recoveries`), the count of transitions where both crossed (`joint_moves = co_falls + co_recoveries + opposed_moves`), the resulting `affinity` (`opposed_moves / joint_moves`), and its `class` — plus the deal's **most-opposed pairing** (`max_affinity` / `most_opposed_pair`, `null` when no two fronts ever counter-moved), the `total_opposed_moves` (`= Σ_t falling_t × recovering_t`) and `total_aligned_moves` (`= total_co_falls` (v32) `+ total_co_recoveries` (v33)). Markdown (default) or `--format json`. No documents are read; the engine is never run.
- A **single deal-level coupling gate.** `--fail-on-opposed-fronts` exits 2 when at least one pair of fronts moved opposite ways for a strict **majority** of the transitions in which both crossed (`opposed_moves × 2 > joint_moves`). This is the _off-diagonal_ counterpart to v32's and v33's _aligned_ gates: where those fire on a stable bloc, this fires on a stable see-saw — two fronts the counterparty trades one against the other, across the deal. A pair that opposed once but aligned twice is `incidental` and clears it; a consumer wanting a different bar reads `affinity` and `max_affinity` from the JSON. It needs no tuning — a strict majority of the joint moves _is_ "they counter-move more often than they align."
- A **whole-sequence-verified input, sharing the v17–v33 front end.** Every artifact's `coherence_hash` is re-derived and checked on load (a tampered/corrupt round is a hard error, errors prefixed `round N:`). The v15/v16 cross-ladder guard runs across the sequence via the same `verifyCoherenceSequence` loader the seventeen trend/exposure/persistence/breadth/recurrence/volatility/synchrony/settling/onset/latency/concurrency/relapse/tenure/affinity/recovery-affinity commands use, with no change to it.
- A **derived report carrying its own fingerprint.** The affinity carries an `opposition_hash` (a stable SHA-256 over the canonical per-pair set — the integer `co_falls`/`co_recoveries`/`opposed_moves` and the class; the derived float `affinity` and the derived integer `joint_moves` are omitted, so the fingerprint is integer-exact over the inputs), namespaced apart from every prior hash (`coherence_hash`, …, `affinity_hash`, `recovery_affinity_hash`), so the same N artifacts in the same order always reproduce the same report.

**It is not:**

- **Not a new diff, classifier, predicate, or artifact format.** It reuses the `weakest_tier` binding floor v12 derives, the `below-acceptable` rung v10 defines, and the _fall_ / _recovery_ events v29 defines; v34 is a subcommand plus one pure module (`computeCoherenceOpposition`) whose only new logic is a per-pair overlap of the opposite-direction step sets and a strict-majority test. `parsePostureCoherenceJson`, the coherence schema, and every trajectory/exposure/…/recovery-affinity function are unchanged.
- **Not the co-fall (v32) or co-recovery (v33) command.** Those read, per pair, how reliably two fronts move the **same** way together (the concession / restoration blocs). v34 reads, per pair, how reliably two fronts move **opposite** ways (the substitution). A pair `coupled` on falls (v32) can have no opposition edge (v34) and vice versa: the aligned and opposed relations are independent reads of the same `floors[]` matrix. A consumer wanting "which fronts move as a bloc?" runs v32/v33; one wanting "which fronts trade against each other?" runs v34; one wanting all three joins on the shared `floors[]` matrix. (The three reads partition the joint-move steps exactly: `co_falls + co_recoveries + opposed_moves = joint_moves`.)
- **Not the concurrency command (v29).** v29 counts, per step, how many fronts fell and how many recovered — two per-step scalars (`falling`, `recovering`), and it gates on a single step's fall count (`concerted-fall`). v34 reads, per pair, how reliably two fronts move opposite ways _across the deal_ — a per-pair relation, gated on a pair that opposed more often than it aligned. Every opposed step _is_ a v29 step with `falling ≥ 1` and `recovering ≥ 1`, so an opposition implies a `mixed`/concerted v29 step; but the reverse fails the way that matters: three different pairs each opposing once each give v29 three mixed steps yet leave every pair `incidental` to v34.
- **Not the synchrony command (v25).** v25 counts, per step, how many fronts _crossed_ the floor together — direction-blind. v34 reads only the _pairwise opposite-direction_ overlap. A step where two fronts both fell is a synchrony of two to v25 and is **not** a counter-move to v34 (same direction).
- **Not co-_standing_ opposition (one above, one below in the same round, however they got there).** v34's counter-move is a coincidence of _opposite crossing events_ (a v29 fall against a v29 recovery), not of standing: two fronts that sit on opposite sides of the floor through rounds 2–4 without _crossing_ in the same step do not counter-move (no shared opposed transition). This keeps the lineage to v29's events and the join invariant to `Σ_t falling_t × recovering_t` exact.
- **Not a configurable "opposed at least K times" gate.** v34 does not take a `--min-opposed K` knob; the gate fires on a strict majority of the joint moves (`opposed_moves × 2 > joint_moves`), the one coupling condition whose meaning needs no tuning. (v20–v33 made the same call.)
- **Not a minimum-joint gate.** A pair that opposed exactly once, with no aligned joint step, reads `opposed_moves` 1 / `joint_moves` 1 / `affinity` 1.0 / `opposed` — every joint move the two made was a counter-move. (Mirrors v32/v33's "both-once → 100%/coupled" call.)
- **Not a per-front reduction.** Like v32/v33, v34's primary object is an unordered **pair**, not a front. The per-pair aligned split (`co_falls`, `co_recoveries`) appears only as the context for the pair's opposition.
- **Not an opposition artifact.** v34 introduces no new on-disk format; the affinity is _derived_, recomputed on demand from the N auditable coherence inputs.
- **Not a sequence the command discovers.** It takes the artifacts **in round order** on the argv, exactly as the seventeen trend/exposure/persistence/… commands do.
- **Not a browser surface.** An N-round pairwise affinity of uploaded artifacts is a CI/dashboard concern (mirroring v16–v33).

## §3. The posture filter (unchanged)

1. **Deterministic** — `computeCoherenceOpposition` over N `PostureCoherence` objects is pure: it matches fronts by dimension, pins fronts by `localeCompare(_, "en")` (the same order the trajectory/exposure/concurrency/affinity functions use), enumerates unordered pairs in that pinned order (`dimension_a < dimension_b`), counts each pair's opposed moves by intersecting one front's fall-step set with the other's recovery-step set (both ways), and picks the most-opposed affinity by **integer cross-multiplication** (`opposed × joint` of the two pairs), never a float comparison. Identical artifacts in identical order → identical `opposition_hash` on any machine.
2. **Honest about unstated data** — a front no document states in a round does not _cross_ into or out of that round: silence is neither a fall nor a recovery (the same §3 contract that keeps `below → unstated → below` zero crossings in v24/v29). A crossing revealed after a silent gap is attributed to the transition into the round that reveals the new standing — never an invented crossing on the silent step. A pair that never counter-moved is omitted from `pairs[]` (no opposition edge), never ranked.
3. **Advisory** — the command names which fronts the counterparty moved opposite ways across the team's own floor together, and how reliably. It asserts no legal conclusion.
4. **No server** — N local files in, one report out. No socket, no engine run.
5. **Additive** — a brand-new subcommand and one new pure module. Every existing command (`analyze`, `diff`, `compare`, `compare-coherence`, `coherence-trend`, …, `coherence-affinity`, `coherence-recovery-affinity`, `verify`) is byte-for-byte unchanged in _output and goldens_; `coherence-sequence.ts` is imported and reused **without modification** (v34 needs nothing exported that was not already public — like v19–v33, it touches no existing source file's behavior at all).

# Part I — The affinity (pure)

`src/report/coherence-opposition.ts` (a new sibling to `coherence-affinity.ts` and `coherence-recovery-affinity.ts`):

- **`OppositionClass`** — a pair's counter-move coupling class: `opposed` (the two fronts moved opposite ways for a strict majority of the steps both crossed — `opposed_moves × 2 > joint_moves`), `incidental` (they opposed in ≥ 1 step but not a strict majority of the joint moves, including an exact split). Only pairs with `opposed_moves ≥ 1` are classified; a pair that never counter-moved has no opposition edge and is omitted.
- **`CoherenceOppositionPair`** — one unordered pair's whole-sequence counter-move relation: the `dimension_a`/`dimension_b` (pinned, `a < b`), the `co_falls`/`co_recoveries` (the aligned moves split), the `opposed_moves` (transitions the two moved opposite ways), the `joint_moves` (transitions both crossed, `co_falls + co_recoveries + opposed_moves`), the `affinity` (`opposed_moves / joint_moves`), and its `class`.
- **`CoherenceOpposition`** — the whole-sequence reduction: `rounds` (count), `pairs[]` (the counter-moving pairs above, pinned by `(dimension_a, dimension_b)`), `class_counts` (the per-pair tally by class), `total_opposed_moves` (every pair's opposed moves summed — equal to `Σ_t falling_t × recovering_t`), `total_aligned_moves` (every pair's aligned moves summed — equal to `total_co_falls` (v32) `+ total_co_recoveries` (v33)), `max_affinity` (the most-opposed coupling across the deal, `null` when no two fronts ever counter-moved), `most_opposed_pair` (the `[dimension_a, dimension_b]` owning `max_affinity`, earliest on a tie, `null` when none), `opposed` (any pair a strict-majority counter-move — the gate verdict), and `opposition_hash`.
- **`computeCoherenceOpposition(rounds: PostureCoherence[])`** — the pure, IO-free core. For each front, derives its fall-step set and recovery-step set (the v29 events, silence-skipping, §3); for each unordered pair, intersects A's falls with B's recoveries and A's recoveries with B's falls for `opposed_moves`, computes the aligned moves and the joint, the affinity, and the strict-majority class; selects the most-opposed affinity by integer cross-multiplication, returning the reduction with an `opposition_hash`. Requires ≥ 2 rounds.
- **`exposureOpposed(opposition)`** — the CI gate predicate: `opposition.opposed`. The opposed-direction pairwise verdict no other command exposes (v32/v33 gate on the aligned directions; v29 on a single per-step fall count).
- **`buildCoherenceOppositionJson(opposition)`** / **`renderCoherenceOppositionSummary(opposition)`** — the JSON (`schema: vaulytica.posture-opposition.v1`) and human-readable renderers. The summary prints the most-opposed verdict (the pair and its affinity, or none-paired when no two fronts ever counter-moved, plus the class tally), then one line per counter-moving pair (`opposed` pairs first), then the hash.

v34 imports only the already-public `PostureCoherence` type, the `NegotiationTier` type, and the shared hashing helpers; the `below-acceptable` literal is a plain string; no existing source file changes.

# Part II — The command (headless)

`tools/cli/coherence-opposition.ts` (a new sibling to `coherence-recovery-affinity.ts`):

- **`computeCoherenceOppositionArtifacts(texts, format?)`** — the pure CLI core. Verifies all N artifacts and runs the cross-ladder guard via the shared `verifyCoherenceSequence` loader (unchanged from v18–v33); on any malformed/tampered round returns `{ ok: false, errors }` (each prefixed `round N:`); on a cross-ladder pair returns `{ ok: false }` naming the two rounds; an unpinned artifact yields `{ ok: true }` with a `ladderNote`. Then `computeCoherenceOpposition(rounds)` and renders (markdown summary or JSON), returning `{ ok: true, output, opposed, ladderNote }`.
- **`runCoherenceOpposition(argv)`** — the CLI handler: reads the N files, calls the core, writes the `ladderNote` (if any) to stderr, prints the affinity to stdout, and — under `--fail-on-opposed-fronts` — exits 2 when any pair moved opposite ways for a strict majority of the steps both crossed. A malformed/tampered/cross-ladder input is a hard exit-1 error. Requires ≥ 2 positionals.

`tools/cli/run.ts` (the dispatcher) gains a `coherence-opposition` case and a `USAGE` entry.

```
# Each round, archive its coherence (kilobytes, no clause text):
vaulytica analyze 'round1/*.docx' --playbook-file team.playbook.json --posture --emit-coherence round1.coherence.json
vaulytica analyze 'round2/*.docx' --playbook-file team.playbook.json --posture --emit-coherence round2.coherence.json
vaulytica analyze 'round3/*.docx' --playbook-file team.playbook.json --posture --emit-coherence round3.coherence.json

# Later — read/gate which two fronts the counterparty trades against each other, from the archive alone:
vaulytica coherence-opposition round1.coherence.json round2.coherence.json round3.coherence.json \
  --fail-on-opposed-fronts
```

# Part XVI — Principled deferrals

- **A `coherence-affinity --opposed` flag.** ⬜ Not built — and deliberately not, mirroring v18–v33 Part XVI. v34 is a separate command, not a flag on the co-fall command, so each command keeps exactly one gate and one hash.
- **A configurable "opposed at least K times" gate (`--min-opposed K`).** ⬜ Not built. The gate fires on a strict majority of the joint moves (`opposed_moves × 2 > joint_moves`), the one coupling condition whose meaning needs no tuning. `affinity` and `max_affinity` are in the JSON for a consumer that wants a different bar.
- **A minimum-joint floor for the gate (`--min-joint N`).** ⬜ Not built. A pair that opposed once with no aligned joint step reads 100% / opposed — honestly, every joint move the two made was a counter-move. `joint_moves` is in the JSON for a consumer wanting to discount thin evidence.
- **A combined aligned/opposed view (the full directional affinity matrix).** ⬜ Not built. v32 reads co-falls, v33 co-recoveries, v34 the opposed direction; a consumer wanting all three joins the reports on `(dimension_a, dimension_b)` (the three partition each pair's joint moves exactly). A single command emitting all three would carry three gates and three hashes — against the one-command-one-gate discipline v18–v33 hold.
- **A co-_standing_ opposition (one above, one below in the same round, however they arrived).** ⬜ Not built. v34's counter-move is a coincidence of opposite crossing _events_ (a v29 fall against a v29 recovery), which keeps the join invariant to `Σ_t falling_t × recovering_t` exact. Co-standing opposition (one above, one below, no shared crossing required) is a different relation over each front's `floors[]`; a consumer wanting it intersects the standings directly.
- **A directional precedence (who crosses _first_ within a pair).** ⬜ Not built. v34 reads same-step opposition; a lead-lag read (when A and B both eventually move, does A consistently move first?) is a distinct ordering relation, deferred until a consumer asks.
- **A standalone opposition artifact (`--emit-opposition`).** ⬜ Deferred, for the same reason v14–v33 keep the derived thing derived: the affinity is cheaply recomputable from the N coherence artifacts on demand.
- **A full pairwise matrix (every pair, including zero-opposition pairs).** ⬜ Not built. `pairs[]` lists only pairs with `opposed_moves ≥ 1` (the opposition edges); a pair that never opposed has affinity 0 and carries no see-saw signal.
- **A directory/glob walker that infers round order.** ⬜ Not built. The command takes artifacts in round order on the argv (the caller's contract, mirroring the seventeen trend/exposure/persistence/… commands).
- **A browser surface for an N-round affinity.** ⬜ Deferred (v16–v33 Part XVI). The browser does an in-session two-round comparison; an N-artifact pairwise affinity is a CI/dashboard concern.

Recommendation: **keep the headline a single pair** — the list is in the JSON; add a ranked-by-affinity view only if a dashboard asks to pin it (the pair order stays label-pinned for a stable hash).

# Part XVIII — What this gives the user
