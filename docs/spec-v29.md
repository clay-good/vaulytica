# Vaulytica v29 — Document-Free Exposure Concurrency (Direction-Resolved Per-Step Crossings)

> **Status:** **Shipped (9.26.0).** v24 gave the posture archive a _per-front crossing-count_ axis (`crossings`); v25 re-bucketed those crossings _per step_ (`crossing_fronts`) — but **direction-blind**: a step where three fronts _fell_ below floor together and a step where two fell while one recovered both register as the **same** synchrony (a count of crossings, falls and recoveries indistinguishable). A deal lead reviewing a close asks a question v25 cannot pose: was there a single round the package **collapsed** — two or more fronts moving the _same_ way, _down_, below floor, in one step? A redline round that knocks several fronts below floor _at once_ is materially different from a churn where one front slips while another is won back — yet to v25 both are merely "synchronized" (two crossings). That is a missing **axis**: the _direction-resolved_ dimension of the same per-step crossing data — how many fronts **fell** vs. **recovered** in each step. v29 supplies it: split each step's crossings by direction, and report the fronts that **fell** (`falling`) and the fronts that **recovered** (`recovering`) per step, the deal's **peak fall step** (`peak_fall_transition` / `peak_fall_count`, the single step the most fronts fell at once), and — the one tuning-free verdict — whether any step was a **concerted fall** (≥2 fronts falling together). It continues the global step numbering after v28's Step 208, beginning at **Step 209**. One new subcommand, one pure module, **zero** change to any existing source file.
> **Scope:** one idea — the _direction_ split the per-step crossing count (v25) leaves out. v25 answers _how many fronts crossed the floor in each step_ (a per-step sum, falls and recoveries pooled); v29 answers _how many fell and how many recovered in each step_ (the same per-step crossings, partitioned by direction). `total_crossings` equals v24's grand total and v25/v26/v27/v28's per-step/per-front sum, by construction — and `total_falls + total_recoveries = total_crossings`: v29 reads the same crossings, _split by direction_, not _how many there are_. The use case is the same archive of per-round coherence artifacts: v25 says "two fronts crossed the floor in round 2→3"; v29 says "and both were _falls_ — the package collapsed on the same exchange," distinguishing that from "one fell while one recovered."
> **Posture (unchanged, non-negotiable):** deterministic (the concurrency is a pure reduction over the same `below-acceptable` rung v10 defines, taken over the binding floors v12 already derived and v20/v22/v24/v25/v26/v27/v28 already read; same artifacts → identical `concurrency_hash`, on any machine, forever), no AI / no probabilistic path, no server (N local files in, one report out; no socket), citable (the artifacts carry the same per-front binding floors v12 derived from each document's own clause and the team's own playbook — v29 adds no new claim), lints / references / positions — but never drafts, and never renders a legal conclusion. The command is **advisory and verifiable**: every artifact is hash-verified (a tampered round is a hard error, side-labeled by round index), and the ladders are checked for a match across the _whole_ sequence (any cross-ladder pair is refused) via the same `verifyCoherenceSequence` loader the twelve trend/exposure/persistence/breadth/recurrence/volatility/synchrony/settling/onset/latency commands use, unchanged. Every step passes the §3 filter or it does not ship.

# Part 0 — Intent

## §1. Why we're doing this

v24 gave the posture archive a per-front crossing-count axis — per front, how many times its standing crossed the acceptable floor (`crossings`), falls and recoveries alike. v25 re-bucketed those crossings by step: per round-transition, how many fronts crossed the floor at once (`crossing_fronts`), the deal's peak step, and whether any step was synchronized (≥2 fronts crossing together). But v25 is **direction-blind** — it counts crossings, not their direction. A deal lead reviewing a close asks a question a direction-blind count discards: **was there a single round the package _collapsed_ — two or more fronts moving the same way, down, below floor, together?**

The per-step crossing count cannot answer it, because a count pools falls and recoveries. Two steps v25 holds as identical can be opposites on this axis:

- **The concerted fall.** In round 2→3, the Cap front and the Term front both fall below floor. Two crossings — and both are _falls_; one redline exchange knocked two fronts below floor at once. A coordinated regression.
- **The churn.** In round 2→3, the Cap front falls below floor while the Term front recovers. Also two crossings (same `crossing_fronts`, same `synchronized` class to v25) — yet the package did not collapse; it traded one front down for another up. To v25 this is the same as the concerted fall; to v29 it is its opposite (`mixed`, not `concerted-fall`).

To v25 these are indistinguishable (same crossing count, both "synchronized"). v25 cannot report the one fact that separates a coordinated collapse from a one-down-one-up churn: the **direction** of the fronts that crossed in that step — how many **fell** and how many **recovered**.

This is not a bug in v25 — a _crossing count_ is supposed to count, not resolve direction. It is a missing **axis**: the _direction-resolved_ dimension of the same per-step crossing data. Where v25 sums crossings down the step axis pooling both directions, v29 **splits** each step's crossings into the fronts that fell and the fronts that recovered.

v29 supplies it. Split the same floor crossings v24/v25 count — a fall (at-or-above → below) from a recovery (below → at-or-above) on each front — and report, per step, the fronts that **fell** (`falling`) and the fronts that **recovered** (`recovering`), the deal's **peak fall step** (`peak_fall_transition`, the step the most fronts fell at once), and whether any step was a **concerted fall** (`concerted_fall_count` — ≥2 fronts falling together). The gate fires on a concerted fall — the one direction-resolved condition whose meaning ("two or more fronts fell below the floor in the same exchange") needs no tuning. All of this is a reduction of the crossings derivable from the `floors[]` array v22/v24/v25 already carry — the sum of every step's `falling + recovering` still equals v24's grand crossing total.

## §2. What v29 is and is not

**It is:**

- A **document-free concurrency command.** `coherence-concurrency <r1.coherence.json> … <rN.coherence.json>` reads N ≥ 2 saved coherence artifacts in round order, verifies each, runs the cross-ladder guard across all of them, and prints, per round-transition, how many fronts **fell** below the floor and how many **recovered** in that step (`falling` / `recovering`, with the `localeCompare`-pinned dimension lists), the step's direction-resolved **class** (`concerted-fall` / `concerted-recovery` / `mixed` / `isolated` / `quiet`), the deal's **peak fall step** (`peak_fall_transition` / `peak_fall_count`), the **concerted-fall**, **concerted-recovery**, and **mixed** step counts, the **grand crossing total** (`total_crossings`, equal to v24's per-front sum and v25/v26/v27/v28's totals) and its direction split (`total_falls` + `total_recoveries`). Markdown (default) or `--format json`. No documents are read; the engine is never run.
- A **single deal-level concurrency gate.** `--fail-on-concerted-fall` exits 2 when at least one step saw two or more fronts **fall** below the floor together (`concerted_fall_count > 0`) — a coordinated regression, the direction-resolved counterpart to v25's _any-direction simultaneity_ gate. Where v25 fires on any step where ≥2 fronts crossed (a fall-and-recover churn trips it just as a collapse does), this fires only on a step where ≥2 fronts moved the **same** way, down. It needs no tuning — two fronts falling at once _is_ a coordinated regression.
- A **whole-sequence-verified input, sharing the v17–v28 front end.** Every artifact's `coherence_hash` is re-derived and checked on load (a tampered/corrupt round is a hard error, errors prefixed `round N:`). The v15/v16 cross-ladder guard runs across the sequence via the same `verifyCoherenceSequence` loader the twelve trend/exposure/persistence/breadth/recurrence/volatility/synchrony/settling/onset/latency commands use, with no change to it.
- A **derived report carrying its own fingerprint.** The concurrency carries a `concurrency_hash` (a stable SHA-256 over the canonical per-transition set), namespaced apart from every `coherence_hash`, `movement_hash`, `trajectory_hash`, `shift_trajectory_hash`, `arc_hash`, `exposure_hash`, `persistence_hash`, `breadth_hash`, `recurrence_hash`, `volatility_hash`, `synchrony_hash`, `settling_hash`, `onset_hash`, and `latency_hash`, so the same N artifacts in the same order always reproduce the same report.

**It is not:**

- **Not a new diff, classifier, predicate, or artifact format.** It reuses the `weakest_tier` binding floor v12 derives and the `below-acceptable` rung v10 defines; v29 is a subcommand plus one pure module (`computeCoherenceConcurrency`) whose only new logic is splitting each step's crossings by direction over the same `floors[]` array v24/v25 scan. `parsePostureCoherenceJson`, the coherence schema, and every trajectory/exposure/persistence/breadth/recurrence/volatility/synchrony/settling/onset/latency function are unchanged.
- **Not the synchrony command.** v25 counts the fronts crossing the floor in each step, **direction-blind** (`crossing_fronts`), and gates on any step where ≥2 crossed. v29 **splits** that count by direction (`falling` / `recovering`) and gates only on ≥2 fronts _falling_ together. Their totals agree (`total_crossings`): v29 is v25's per-step crossings resolved into the two directions. A consumer wanting any-direction simultaneity runs v25; one wanting a coordinated collapse runs v29; one wanting both runs both and joins on the shared `floors[]` matrix.
- **Not the volatility command.** v24 counts a front's crossings over the deal (down the front axis). v29 does not headline a per-front sum — it headlines a per-step direction split. Their totals agree (`total_crossings`).
- **Not the latency or breadth command.** v28 measures the rounds a front sat below floor between a fall and its recovery (a duration); v22 counts the fronts below floor in each round (a per-round _level_ snapshot). v29 reads the per-step _movement_ resolved by direction, not a duration or a static level. They are orthogonal reductions of the same below-floor data.
- **Not silence-makes-a-crossing.** A round no document states does **not** count as a fall or a recovery and does **not** appear in any step's `falling` or `recovering`: per §3 (the v24/v25 contract), silence keeps the last known standing. A crossing across a silent gap is attributed to the transition _into the round that reveals the new standing_, never to the silent step.
- **Not a configurable "≥N fronts falling" gate.** v29 does not take a `--min-concerted N` knob; the gate fires on **two or more** fronts falling together, the one simultaneity whose meaning ("a coordinated collapse") needs no tuning. A team wanting a higher bar reads `peak_fall_count` from the JSON. (v20–v28 made the same call against a configurable floor/duration/breadth/episode/crossing count/simultaneity/quiet tail/lead-in/latency.)
- **Not a trajectory-whipsaw command.** v17's `whipsaw` fires on rung jitter anywhere on the ladder, including movement entirely _above_ the floor. v29's concurrency is specific to the **floor boundary**: an above-floor whipsaw (`acceptable → ideal → acceptable`) crosses the floor zero times, so it contributes no fall and no recovery to any step.
- **Not a concurrency artifact.** v29 introduces no new on-disk format; the concurrency is _derived_, recomputed on demand from the N auditable coherence inputs — the same "keep the derived thing derived" discipline v14–v28 hold.
- **Not a sequence the command discovers.** It does not glob a directory or sort filenames; the caller passes the artifacts **in round order** on the argv, exactly as the twelve trend/exposure/persistence/breadth/recurrence/volatility/synchrony/settling/onset/latency commands do.
- **Not a browser surface.** An N-round concurrency of uploaded artifacts is a CI/dashboard concern, which is what this command serves (mirroring v16–v28).

## §3. The posture filter (unchanged)

1. **Deterministic** — `computeCoherenceConcurrency` over N `PostureCoherence` objects is pure: it matches fronts by dimension, pins the per-step fell/recovered dimension lists by `localeCompare(_, "en")` (the same order the trajectory/exposure/volatility/synchrony functions use), and attributes each crossing to the transition that reveals it over the caller's round order. Identical artifacts in identical order → identical `concurrency_hash` on any machine.
2. **Honest about unstated data** — a front no document states in a round does not cross into or out of that round. Within a front's path, an unstated round is skipped: it neither counts as a crossing nor resets the standing (silence is not a fall or a recovery — the §3 contract that keeps `below → unstated → below` zero crossings in v24). A crossing across a silent gap is attributed to the transition into the round that _reveals_ the new standing, so a fall revealed after a silent round lands on the revealing step, never the silent one.
3. **Advisory** — the command names how many fronts moved each way across the team's own floor in each step. It asserts no legal conclusion.
4. **No server** — N local files in, one report out. No socket, no engine run.
5. **Additive** — a brand-new subcommand and one new pure module. Every existing command (`analyze`, `diff`, `compare`, `compare-coherence`, `coherence-trend`, `coherence-shift-trend`, `coherence-arc`, `coherence-exposure`, `coherence-persistence`, `coherence-breadth`, `coherence-recurrence`, `coherence-volatility`, `coherence-synchrony`, `coherence-settling`, `coherence-onset`, `coherence-latency`, `verify`) is byte-for-byte unchanged in _output and goldens_; `coherence-sequence.ts` is imported and reused **without modification** (v29 needs nothing exported that was not already public — like v19–v28, it touches no existing source file's behavior at all).

# Part I — The concurrency (pure)

`src/report/coherence-concurrency.ts` (a new sibling to `coherence-synchrony.ts`):

- **`ConcurrencyClass`** — a step's direction-resolved class (priority order): `concerted-fall` (≥2 fronts fell — the gate-worthy class; dominates even if a recovery also happened that step), `concerted-recovery` (≥2 recovered and <2 fell), `mixed` (fronts moved both ways but neither direction reached two — exactly one fell _and_ ≥1 recovered), `isolated` (exactly one front crossed, one direction), `quiet` (no front crossed).
- **`CoherenceConcurrencyTransition`** — one round-transition's deal-wide movement resolved by direction: the `from_round`/`to_round`, the `falling` and `recovering` counts, the `falling_dimensions`/`recovering_dimensions` lists (`localeCompare`-pinned), and the step's `concurrency` class.
- **`CoherenceConcurrency`** — the whole-sequence reduction: `rounds` (count), `per_transition[]` (the steps above, in sequence order, length = rounds − 1), `total_crossings` (every step's `falling + recovering` summed — equal to v24's, v25's, v26's, v27's, and v28's total), `total_falls`/`total_recoveries` (the direction split, summing to `total_crossings`), `peak_fall_transition` (the 1-based `from_round` of the step the most fronts fell at once, earliest on a tie, `null` when none ever fell), `peak_fall_count` (the fell count there), `concerted_fall_count`/`concerted_recovery_count`/`mixed_count` (the per-class step tallies), `concerted` (any step is a concerted fall — the gate verdict), and `concurrency_hash`.
- **`computeCoherenceConcurrency(rounds: PostureCoherence[])`** — the pure, IO-free core. For each front, scans `floors[]`, attributes each _stated_ floor crossing to the transition that reveals it (an unstated round is skipped, §3), buckets it into that step's `falling` (a fall) or `recovering` (a recovery) list, classifies each step by direction, locates the peak fall step and the concerted-fall count, and returns the reduction with a `concurrency_hash`. Requires ≥ 2 rounds.
- **`exposureConcerted(concurrency)`** — the CI gate predicate: `concurrency.concerted_fall_count > 0`. The direction-resolved verdict v25 cannot pose (v25 gates on any-direction simultaneity; this gates on a step where ≥2 fronts fell the **same** way, down).
- **`buildCoherenceConcurrencyJson(concurrency)`** / **`renderCoherenceConcurrencySummary(concurrency)`** — the JSON (`schema: vaulytica.posture-concurrency.v1`) and human-readable renderers. The summary prints the peak-fall verdict (the step and how many fell, or none-ever-fell), the concerted-fall count, then one line per step showing how many fell and recovered and which, then the hash.

v29 imports only the already-public `PostureCoherence` type, the `NegotiationTier` type, and the shared hashing helpers; the `below-acceptable` literal is a plain string; no existing source file changes.

# Part II — The command (headless)

`tools/cli/coherence-concurrency.ts` (a new sibling to `coherence-synchrony.ts`):

- **`computeCoherenceConcurrencyArtifacts(texts, format?)`** — the pure CLI core. Verifies all N artifacts and runs the cross-ladder guard via the shared `verifyCoherenceSequence` loader (unchanged from v18–v28); on any malformed/tampered round returns `{ ok: false, errors }` (each prefixed `round N:`); on a cross-ladder pair returns `{ ok: false }` naming the two rounds; an unpinned artifact yields `{ ok: true }` with a `ladderNote`. Then `computeCoherenceConcurrency(rounds)` and renders (markdown summary or JSON), returning `{ ok: true, output, concerted, ladderNote }`.
- **`runCoherenceConcurrency(argv)`** — the CLI handler: reads the N files, calls the core, writes the `ladderNote` (if any) to stderr, prints the concurrency to stdout, and — under `--fail-on-concerted-fall` — exits 2 when any step saw ≥2 fronts fall together. A malformed/tampered/cross-ladder input is a hard exit-1 error. Requires ≥ 2 positionals.

`tools/cli/run.ts` (the dispatcher) gains a `coherence-concurrency` case and a `USAGE` entry.

```
# Each round, archive its coherence (kilobytes, no clause text):
vaulytica analyze 'round1/*.docx' --playbook-file team.playbook.json --posture --emit-coherence round1.coherence.json
vaulytica analyze 'round2/*.docx' --playbook-file team.playbook.json --posture --emit-coherence round2.coherence.json
vaulytica analyze 'round3/*.docx' --playbook-file team.playbook.json --posture --emit-coherence round3.coherence.json

# Later — read/gate how many fronts fell vs recovered in each step, from the archive alone:
vaulytica coherence-concurrency round1.coherence.json round2.coherence.json round3.coherence.json \
  --fail-on-concerted-fall
```

# Part XVI — Principled deferrals

- **A `coherence-synchrony --by-direction` flag.** ⬜ Not built — and deliberately not, mirroring v18–v28 Part XVI. v29 is a separate command, not a flag on the synchrony command, so each command keeps exactly one gate and one hash.
- **A configurable "≥N fronts falling" gate (`--min-concerted N`).** ⬜ Not built. The gate fires on ≥2 fronts falling together, the one simultaneity whose meaning needs no tuning. `peak_fall_count` is in the JSON for a consumer wanting a higher bar.
- **A `--fail-on-concerted-recovery` gate.** ⬜ Not built. A coordinated rebound is good news, not a regression worth failing a build over; `concerted_recovery_count` is in the JSON for a consumer who wants to surface it.
- **A standalone concurrency artifact (`--emit-concurrency`).** ⬜ Deferred, for the same reason v14–v28 keep the derived thing derived: the concurrency is cheaply recomputable from the N coherence artifacts on demand.
- **A _net-direction_ per-step verdict (more falls than recoveries this step).** ⬜ Not built. The `falling`/`recovering` counts are in the JSON for a consumer that wants the net; v29 headlines the concerted-fall, the one tuning-free verdict.
- **A directory/glob walker that infers round order.** ⬜ Not built. The command takes artifacts in round order on the argv (the caller's contract, mirroring the trend/exposure/persistence/breadth/recurrence/volatility/synchrony/settling/onset/latency commands).
- **A browser surface for an N-round concurrency.** ⬜ Deferred (v16–v28 Part XVI). The browser does an in-session two-round comparison; an N-artifact concurrency is a CI/dashboard concern.

# Part XVII — Open questions for the maintainer

1. **Fold concurrency and synchrony into a single "deal movement" command?** Today the direction-resolved (v29) and direction-blind (v25) per-step reductions are split. A dashboard wanting both runs them and joins on the shared `floors[]` matrix (their totals agree by construction). Recommendation: **keep them split at the command layer** — they carry categorically different gates (per-deal "two fell together" vs "two crossed together") and different hashes; bolting one onto another would give that command unlike gates. If a dashboard asks for the combined view, add a composing command the way v19 joined v17 and v18, not a flag.
2. **Report a per-front concurrency (which fronts most often fell _with_ others)?** Today the concurrency is per-step. A per-front co-fall affinity has a different meaning with no consumer yet. Recommendation: **defer** — the per-step `falling_dimensions[]` lists already let a consumer compute co-fall affinity; add it if a dashboard asks to pin one.

# Part XVIII — What this gives the user

- **The direction of the deal's coordinated moves, from the archive alone.** Archive each round's kilobyte coherence artifact as you go; `coherence-concurrency` shows not just _how many fronts crossed the floor in each step_ (v25), but _how many **fell** and how many **recovered**_ — distinguishing a redline round that knocked two fronts below floor at once (a concerted fall) from a one-down-one-up churn, which v25 reports identically (both "synchronized"), with no clause text checked out and no re-analysis run. The "did the package collapse on one exchange?" question a deal lead asks reviewing a close, answered deterministically from N small, verifiable files.
- **A gate on a coordinated collapse, not just any simultaneity.** `--fail-on-concerted-fall` fires when two or more fronts fell below the floor in the same step — a coordinated regression — a condition v25's direction-blind gate structurally cannot isolate (a fall-and-recover churn trips v25's gate just as a collapse does). It catches the redline round that took several fronts down at once even when no single front is volatile across the whole deal.
- **The same five promises.** Deterministic, no AI, no server, citable, never drafts — every line of v29 passes the §3 gate. It reads the binding floor v12 already derives and the `below-acceptable` rung v10 already defines, through the loader v18–v28 already share; it adds one reduction over the crossings v24/v25 already count, no on-disk format, and no change to any existing source file's behavior — every existing surface is byte-for-byte unchanged.
