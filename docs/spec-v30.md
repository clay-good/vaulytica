# Vaulytica v30 — Document-Free Exposure Relapse Interval (Rounds Above Floor per Recovery-to-Relapse Span)

> **Status:** **Shipped (9.27.0).** v28 paired each _fall_ below the acceptable floor with the _recovery_ that closes it and read the rounds the front sat **below** floor between them (`latency`, headlined by the slowest recovery `max_latency`, gated on a fall that _never_ recovered). It answered "how long was each fall exposed, and did it ever come back?" — but it is mute on the **mirror** question a deal lead asks reviewing a close that _bounced_: once a front recovered above the floor, **how long did the fix hold before it fell again — and did it ever relapse at the very next round?** A front that recovered in round 2 and held until round 6 secured a durable fix; a front that recovered in round 2 and fell again in round 3 had a fix that did not hold — yet to v24 both have the **same** crossing count (three: fall, recovery, fall), and to v28 a relapse is not a latency at all (v28 reads the gap _below_, not the gap _above_). None reports the **duration the recovery held before being undone**. That is a missing **axis**: the _relapse-interval_ dimension of the same crossing data — the rounds between a paired recovery and the fall that undoes it, the exact mirror of v28's recovery latency. v30 supplies it: pair each recovery crossing with the next fall crossing, and report the **rounds the front held above floor in that interval** (`clean_rounds`), the deal's **quickest relapse** (`min_interval`), and — the one tuning-free verdict — whether a recovery was undone at the **very next round** (an immediate relapse, `clean_rounds === 1`). It continues the global step numbering after v29's Step 209, beginning at **Step 210**. One new subcommand, one pure module, **zero** change to any existing source file.
> **Scope:** one idea — the _relapse-interval_ reduction the per-front crossing count (v24), the per-step crossing count (v25), the last/first-crossing index (v26/v27), the recovery latency (v28), and the per-step direction split (v29) all leave out. v28 answers _how long did a front stay below the floor between a fall and its recovery_ (the gap **below**); v30 answers _how long did a front hold above the floor between a recovery and its next fall_ (the gap **above**). `total_crossings` equals v24's grand total and v25/v26/v27/v28/v29's totals, by construction — v30 reads the same crossings, _paired the other way_, for the rounds _between_ each recovery and the fall that undoes it. The use case is the same archive of per-round coherence artifacts: v28 says "the Cap front fell in round 2 and recovered in round 5 — three rounds below"; v30 says "and after that recovery it held only one round before falling again — the fix did not hold."
> **Posture (unchanged, non-negotiable):** deterministic (the relapse is a pure reduction over the same `below-acceptable` rung v10 defines, taken over the binding floors v12 already derived and v20/v22/v24/v25/v28 already read; same artifacts → identical `relapse_hash`, on any machine, forever), no AI / no probabilistic path, no server (N local files in, one report out; no socket), citable (the artifacts carry the same per-front binding floors v12 derived from each document's own clause and the team's own playbook — v30 adds no new claim), lints / references / positions — but never drafts, and never renders a legal conclusion. The command is **advisory and verifiable**: every artifact is hash-verified (a tampered round is a hard error, side-labeled by round index), and the ladders are checked for a match across the _whole_ sequence (any cross-ladder pair is refused) via the same `verifyCoherenceSequence` loader the thirteen trend/exposure/persistence/breadth/recurrence/volatility/synchrony/settling/onset/latency/concurrency commands use, unchanged. Every step passes the §3 filter or it does not ship.

# Part 0 — Intent

## §1. Why we're doing this

v28 gave the posture archive a recovery-latency axis — per front, the rounds its binding floor sat _below_ the acceptable floor between a fall and the recovery that closes it (`latency`), the deal's slowest recovery (`max_latency`), and whether a fall went unrecovered (`open_count`). It pairs each _fall_ forward with the _recovery_ that closes it. But it is silent on the **mirror**: once a front _recovered_, how long did the recovery hold before a fall undid it? A deal lead reviewing a close that bounced asks a question v28's below-gap discards: **once a front came back above the floor, how many rounds did the fix hold before it fell again — and did it ever relapse at the very next round?**

The recovery latency cannot answer it, because it measures the gap _below_, not the gap _above_. Two fronts v24 holds as identical (three crossings each) can be opposites on this axis:

- **The durable fix.** Cap recovers in round 2 and does not fall again until round 6 — the recovery held for **four rounds** before relapsing.
- **The fix that did not hold.** Cap recovers in round 2 and falls again in round 3 — the recovery held for a **single round**; it bounced straight back below the floor. To v24 this is the same as the durable fix (three crossings); to v30 it is its opposite (an _immediate_ relapse).

To v24 these are indistinguishable (same crossing count). To v28 they may agree (same below-latency on the earlier fall) — and a relapse is not a latency at all. None of the existing commands reports the one fact that separates a recovery that secured the front from one that was undone at the next exchange: the **number of rounds the front held above floor between its recovery and the fall that undoes it**, and — the limiting case — whether the recovery relapsed at the **very next round** (`clean_rounds === 1`).

This is not a bug in v28 — a _recovery latency_ is supposed to read the gap below, not the gap above. It is a missing **axis**: the _relapse-interval_ dimension of the same crossing data, the exact mirror of v28. Where v28 pairs each fall forward with the recovery that closes it and reads the rounds below, v30 **pairs** each recovery forward with the fall that undoes it and reads the rounds above.

v30 supplies it. Pair the same floor crossings v24/v28 count — a recovery (below → at-or-above) with the next fall (at-or-above → below) on the same front — and report, per front, each interval's **clean rounds** (the rounds it held above floor: `fall_round − recovery_round`), the deal's **quickest relapse** (`min_interval`, the shortest such gap), and whether any recovery was undone at the **very next round** (`immediate` — a `clean_rounds === 1` interval). The gate fires on an immediate relapse — the one time condition whose meaning ("a recovery undone the very next round — a fix that did not hold") needs no tuning. All of this is a reduction of the crossings derivable from the `floors[]` array v22/v24/v28 already carry — the sum of every front's crossings still equals v24's grand crossing total.

## §2. What v30 is and is not

**It is:**

- A **document-free relapse-interval command.** `coherence-relapse <r1.coherence.json> … <rN.coherence.json>` reads N ≥ 2 saved coherence artifacts in round order, verifies each, runs the cross-ladder guard across all of them, and prints, per front, its above-floor **intervals** paired from its crossings (each a `recovery_round` → `fall_round` with its `clean_rounds`, `fall_round` `null` when the recovery held), the deal's **quickest relapse** (`min_interval` and the `quickest_dimension` that owns it, `null` when no recovery was ever undone), the **relapse-** and **held-interval** counts (`relapse_count`, `held_count`), and the **grand crossing total** (`total_crossings`, equal to v24's per-front sum and v25/v26/v27/v28/v29's totals). Markdown (default) or `--format json`. No documents are read; the engine is never run.
- A **single deal-level relapse gate.** `--fail-on-immediate-relapse` exits 2 when at least one recovery was undone by a fall at the **very next round** (`clean_rounds === 1`) — the limiting case of relapse interval (the shortest possible above-gap), the mirror of v28's unbounded below-gap. This is the _relapse-interval_ counterpart to v28's _unrecovered_ gate: where v28 fires on a fall that never closed with a recovery (an unbounded latency below), this fires on a recovery undone the very next round (a minimal interval above). A recovery that held two or more rounds before relapsing clears it (the front is `relapsed` but not `immediate`); a recovery never undone clears it (`held`). It needs no tuning — a recovery undone the next round _is_ a fix that did not hold.
- A **whole-sequence-verified input, sharing the v17–v29 front end.** Every artifact's `coherence_hash` is re-derived and checked on load (a tampered/corrupt round is a hard error, errors prefixed `round N:`). The v15/v16 cross-ladder guard runs across the sequence via the same `verifyCoherenceSequence` loader the thirteen trend/exposure/persistence/breadth/recurrence/volatility/synchrony/settling/onset/latency/concurrency commands use, with no change to it.
- A **derived report carrying its own fingerprint.** The relapse carries a `relapse_hash` (a stable SHA-256 over the canonical per-front interval set), namespaced apart from every `coherence_hash`, `movement_hash`, `trajectory_hash`, `shift_trajectory_hash`, `arc_hash`, `exposure_hash`, `persistence_hash`, `breadth_hash`, `recurrence_hash`, `volatility_hash`, `synchrony_hash`, `settling_hash`, `onset_hash`, `latency_hash`, and `concurrency_hash`, so the same N artifacts in the same order always reproduce the same report.

**It is not:**

- **Not a new diff, classifier, predicate, or artifact format.** It reuses the `weakest_tier` binding floor v12 derives and the `below-acceptable` rung v10 defines; v30 is a subcommand plus one pure module (`computeCoherenceRelapse`) whose only new logic is pairing each recovery crossing with the fall that undoes it over the same `floors[]` array v24/v28 scan. `parsePostureCoherenceJson`, the coherence schema, and every trajectory/exposure/persistence/breadth/recurrence/volatility/synchrony/settling/onset/latency/concurrency function are unchanged.
- **Not the latency command.** v28 pairs each _fall_ forward with the recovery that closes it and reads the rounds **below** (`latency`, headlined by `max_latency`, gated on a fall that never recovered). v30 pairs each _recovery_ forward with the fall that undoes it and reads the rounds **above** (`clean_rounds`, headlined by `min_interval`, gated on an immediate relapse). They are mirror reductions of the same crossings — `total_crossings` agrees — on opposite gaps (below vs above) headlined opposite ways (the slowest recovery vs the quickest relapse). This mirror surfaces a deliberate asymmetry: a _leading recovery_ (a front stated below from round 1 that recovers) is `steady` to v28 (no in-sequence fall to pair _backward_) but pairs _forward_ in v30 (its recovery has a real future — it either held, `held`, or was undone, `relapsed`). A consumer wanting the rounds-below runs v28; one wanting the rounds-above runs v30; one wanting both runs both and joins on the shared `floors[]` matrix.
- **Not the recurrence command.** v23 counts a front's below-floor _episodes_ (≥ 2 episodes ⟺ ≥ 1 relapse), but it has **no duration** — it cannot tell a recovery undone the next round from one that held for rounds. v30 headlines the _duration_ the recovery held (`clean_rounds`) and gates on the duration being minimal (`immediate`), the dimension v23's count discards. A consumer wanting the episode count runs v23; one wanting the rounds-above-between-episodes runs v30.
- **Not the volatility command.** v24 counts a front's crossings over the deal. v30 does not headline a count — it headlines a **duration** (the rounds above floor between a recovery and the fall that undoes it). Their totals agree (`total_crossings`).
- **Not silence-makes-an-interval-shorter.** A round no document states does **not** count as a round above floor and does **not** shorten or invent an interval: per §3 (the v21/v24/v28 contract), silence keeps the last known standing — it is neither a recovery nor a fall. An interval's `clean_rounds` is `fall_round − recovery_round` over the caller's round indices; a silent round inside the gap is part of that span (the front's last stated standing was above), but a crossing across a silent gap is attributed to the transition _into the round that reveals the new standing_, never to the silent step. This keeps v30 from inventing — or mislocating — a recovery or a fall at a silent round (so a relapse revealed after a silent round is not an _immediate_ relapse — `clean_rounds` spans the silent rounds).
- **Not a configurable "relapse within N rounds" gate.** v30 does not take a `--min-interval N` knob; the gate fires on an **immediate** relapse (`clean_rounds === 1`), the one interval condition whose meaning ("the recovery was undone the very next round") needs no tuning. A team wanting to flag a _wider_ relapse reads `min_interval` and the per-interval `clean_rounds` from the JSON. (v20–v29 made the same call against a configurable floor/duration/breadth/episode/crossing count/simultaneity/quiet tail/lead-in/latency/concerted-count.)
- **Not a trajectory-whipsaw command.** v17's `whipsaw` fires on rung jitter anywhere on the ladder, including movement entirely _above_ the floor. v30's relapse is specific to the **floor boundary**: an above-floor whipsaw (`acceptable → ideal → acceptable`) crosses the floor zero times, so it pairs no recovery with a fall and contributes no interval.
- **Not a relapse artifact.** v30 introduces no new on-disk format; the relapse is _derived_, recomputed on demand from the N auditable coherence inputs — the same "keep the derived thing derived" discipline v14–v29 hold.
- **Not a sequence the command discovers.** It does not glob a directory or sort filenames; the caller passes the artifacts **in round order** on the argv, exactly as the thirteen trend/exposure/persistence/breadth/recurrence/volatility/synchrony/settling/onset/latency/concurrency commands do.
- **Not a browser surface.** An N-round relapse of uploaded artifacts is a CI/dashboard concern, which is what this command serves (mirroring v16–v29).

## §3. The posture filter (unchanged)

1. **Deterministic** — `computeCoherenceRelapse` over N `PostureCoherence` objects is pure: it matches fronts by dimension, pins fronts by `localeCompare(_, "en")` (the same order the trajectory/exposure/volatility/synchrony/settling/onset/latency functions use), and pairs each front's recovery with the fall that undoes it over the caller's round order. Identical artifacts in identical order → identical `relapse_hash` on any machine.
2. **Honest about unstated data** — a front no document states in a round does not cross into or out of that round. Within a front's path, an unstated round is skipped: it neither counts as a crossing nor resets the standing (silence is not a fall or a recovery — the §3 contract that keeps `above → unstated → above` zero crossings in v24). A crossing across a silent gap is attributed to the transition into the round that _reveals_ the new standing, so a relapse revealed after a silent round lands on the revealing step and its `clean_rounds` spans the silent rounds (never an _immediate_ relapse across a silent gap).
3. **Advisory** — the command names how many rounds the team's own floor stayed clear between a recovery and the fall that undoes it. It asserts no legal conclusion.
4. **No server** — N local files in, one report out. No socket, no engine run.
5. **Additive** — a brand-new subcommand and one new pure module. Every existing command (`analyze`, `diff`, `compare`, `compare-coherence`, `coherence-trend`, `coherence-shift-trend`, `coherence-arc`, `coherence-exposure`, `coherence-persistence`, `coherence-breadth`, `coherence-recurrence`, `coherence-volatility`, `coherence-synchrony`, `coherence-settling`, `coherence-onset`, `coherence-latency`, `coherence-concurrency`, `verify`) is byte-for-byte unchanged in _output and goldens_; `coherence-sequence.ts` is imported and reused **without modification** (v30 needs nothing exported that was not already public — like v19–v29, it touches no existing source file's behavior at all).

# Part I — The relapse (pure)

`src/report/coherence-relapse.ts` (a new sibling to `coherence-latency.ts`):

- **`RelapseClass`** — a front's relapse-interval class: `relapsed` (a recovery was undone by a later fall in-sequence — ≥ 1 closed interval, the fix did not hold), `held` (it recovered above floor in-sequence and _every_ recovery held — ≥ 1 open interval, none closed), `steady` (stated, but never _recovered_ above floor in-sequence — no recovery crossing to pair forward, including a front that never fell and one that fell and never came back), `unstated` (no document ever stated the front, §3).
- **`CoherenceRelapseInterval`** — one recovery-to-relapse span: the `recovery_round` (the 1-based round the front was first seen back at-or-above floor), the `fall_round` (the 1-based round it was next seen below floor again; `null` when the recovery held), and the `clean_rounds` (`fall_round − recovery_round` — the rounds it held above floor; `null` when held).
- **`CoherenceRelapseFront`** — one negotiation front's whole-sequence relapse structure: the `dimension`, the `floors[]` path (for context), the `crossings` (its total floor crossings — equal to v24's per-front `crossings`), the `intervals[]` (the recovery-to-relapse spans above, in sequence order), the `min_interval` (its shortest closed interval — its quickest relapse, `null` when none relapsed), and its `relapse` class.
- **`CoherenceRelapse`** — the whole-sequence reduction: `rounds` (count), `fronts[]` (the fronts above, `localeCompare`-pinned), `class_counts` (the per-front tally by class), `total_crossings` (every front's crossing summed — equal to v24's, v25's, v26's, v27's, v28's, and v29's total), `relapse_count` (closed intervals across all fronts), `held_count` (open intervals across all fronts), `min_interval` (the quickest relapse across the deal — the shortest closed interval, `null` when none relapsed), `quickest_dimension` (the front owning `min_interval`, `null` when none relapsed), `immediate` (any closed interval has `clean_rounds === 1` — the gate verdict), and `relapse_hash`.
- **`computeCoherenceRelapse(rounds: PostureCoherence[])`** — the pure, IO-free core. For each front, scans `floors[]`, attributes each _stated_ floor crossing to the transition that reveals it (an unstated round is skipped, §3), pairs each recovery with the next fall into intervals, computes each closed interval's clean rounds, locates the quickest relapse and any immediate relapse, and returns the reduction with a `relapse_hash`. Requires ≥ 2 rounds.
- **`exposureImmediateRelapse(relapse)`** — the CI gate predicate: `relapse.immediate`. The relapse-interval verdict no other command exposes (v24 gates on a single front's crossing count; v28 on a fall that never recovered; this gates on a recovery undone the **very next round** — distinct from v23's recurrence count, which fires on ≥ 2 episodes regardless of how long the recovery held between them).
- **`buildCoherenceRelapseJson(relapse)`** / **`renderCoherenceRelapseSummary(relapse)`** — the JSON (`schema: vaulytica.posture-relapse.v1`) and human-readable renderers. The summary prints the quickest-relapse verdict (the front and its rounds-above, or held-throughout when nothing recovered-and-fell-again, plus the relapse/held counts), then one line per front with an interval, then the hash.

v30 imports only the already-public `PostureCoherence` type, the `NegotiationTier` type, and the shared hashing helpers; the `below-acceptable` literal is a plain string; no existing source file changes.

# Part II — The command (headless)

`tools/cli/coherence-relapse.ts` (a new sibling to `coherence-latency.ts`):

- **`computeCoherenceRelapseArtifacts(texts, format?)`** — the pure CLI core. Verifies all N artifacts and runs the cross-ladder guard via the shared `verifyCoherenceSequence` loader (unchanged from v18–v29); on any malformed/tampered round returns `{ ok: false, errors }` (each prefixed `round N:`); on a cross-ladder pair returns `{ ok: false }` naming the two rounds; an unpinned artifact yields `{ ok: true }` with a `ladderNote`. Then `computeCoherenceRelapse(rounds)` and renders (markdown summary or JSON), returning `{ ok: true, output, immediate, ladderNote }`.
- **`runCoherenceRelapse(argv)`** — the CLI handler: reads the N files, calls the core, writes the `ladderNote` (if any) to stderr, prints the relapse to stdout, and — under `--fail-on-immediate-relapse` — exits 2 when any recovery was undone at the very next round. A malformed/tampered/cross-ladder input is a hard exit-1 error. Requires ≥ 2 positionals.

`tools/cli/run.ts` (the dispatcher) gains a `coherence-relapse` case and a `USAGE` entry.

```
# Each round, archive its coherence (kilobytes, no clause text):
vaulytica analyze 'round1/*.docx' --playbook-file team.playbook.json --posture --emit-coherence round1.coherence.json
vaulytica analyze 'round2/*.docx' --playbook-file team.playbook.json --posture --emit-coherence round2.coherence.json
vaulytica analyze 'round3/*.docx' --playbook-file team.playbook.json --posture --emit-coherence round3.coherence.json

# Later — read/gate how long each front held above floor between a recovery and the fall that undoes it, from the archive alone:
vaulytica coherence-relapse round1.coherence.json round2.coherence.json round3.coherence.json \
  --fail-on-immediate-relapse
```

# Part XVI — Principled deferrals

- **A `coherence-latency --relapse` flag.** ⬜ Not built — and deliberately not, mirroring v18–v29 Part XVI. v30 is a separate command, not a flag on the latency command, so each command keeps exactly one gate and one hash.
- **A configurable "relapse within N rounds" gate (`--min-interval N`).** ⬜ Not built. The gate fires on an immediate relapse (`clean_rounds === 1`), the one interval condition whose meaning needs no tuning. `min_interval` and the per-interval `clean_rounds` are in the JSON for a consumer that wants to flag a wider relapse.
- **A standalone relapse artifact (`--emit-relapse`).** ⬜ Deferred, for the same reason v14–v29 keep the derived thing derived: the relapse is cheaply recomputable from the N coherence artifacts on demand.
- **A _trailing-relapse_ interval (the rounds a front held above after the archive ended).** ⬜ Not built — and not computable: the fall that would close an open interval is not in the sequence, so its clean-rounds is unknown. v30 reports only intervals whose recovery is observed in-sequence; a recovery that held to the close is `held` (an open interval, no fall to pair).
- **A _mean_ relapse interval (the average gap across intervals).** ⬜ Not built. v30 headlines the quickest relapse (`min_interval`) and the per-interval `clean_rounds`; a consumer wanting the mean reduces the `intervals[]` array. Add it if a dashboard asks to pin one.
- **A directory/glob walker that infers round order.** ⬜ Not built. The command takes artifacts in round order on the argv (the caller's contract, mirroring the trend/exposure/persistence/breadth/recurrence/volatility/synchrony/settling/onset/latency/concurrency commands).
- **A browser surface for an N-round relapse.** ⬜ Deferred (v16–v29 Part XVI). The browser does an in-session two-round comparison; an N-artifact relapse is a CI/dashboard concern.

# Part XVII — Open questions for the maintainer

1. **Fold latency and relapse into a single "exposure cadence" command?** Today the recovery-latency (v28, the gap below) and the relapse-interval (v30, the gap above) reductions are split. A dashboard wanting both runs them and joins on the shared `floors[]` matrix (their totals agree by construction). Recommendation: **keep them split at the command layer** — they carry categorically different gates (per-deal "never recovered" vs "relapsed the very next round") and different hashes; bolting one onto another would give that command unlike gates. If a dashboard asks for the combined view, add a composing command the way v19 joined v17 and v18, not a flag.
2. **Report a per-front _cadence ratio_ (rounds below vs rounds above, episode by episode)?** Today the below-gap (v28) and the above-gap (v30) are separate per-front reductions. A combined ratio (how much of the deal a front spent below vs holding above) has a different meaning with no consumer yet. Recommendation: **defer** — the per-front `episodes[]` (v28) and `intervals[]` (v30) plus the `floors[]` matrix already let a consumer compute the ratio; add it if a dashboard asks to pin one.

# Part XVIII — What this gives the user
