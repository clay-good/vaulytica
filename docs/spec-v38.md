# Vaulytica v38 — Document-Free Persistent Weak Front (Concedes First _and_ Recovers Last — the Join)

> **Status:** **Shipped (9.35.0).** v36 gave the posture archive its **fall-order** (which front the counterparty *concedes* first); v37 its **recovery-order** (which front it *restores* last — the laggard left exposed longest). Each is a standalone pairwise read, and each spec named the same prize the other half could not name alone: a front that **concedes first _and_ recovers last** is the deal's **persistent weak point** — exposed coming and going — while a front that concedes first but recovers first is merely *volatile*. v38 is the **per-front join** that names it. It reuses v36 and v37 **unchanged** (calling `computeCoherenceConcession` and `computeCoherenceRecoveryOrder` as pure functions), reduces their pairwise leading edges to a per-front verdict, and gates on the one tuning-free condition the two halves make possible: a front that is a strict-majority **first-conceder** against some partner **and** a strict-majority **last-recoverer** against some partner (`--fail-on-persistent-weak-front`). It continues the global step numbering after v37's Step 217, beginning at **Step 218**. One new subcommand, one pure module, **zero** change to any existing source file.
> **Scope:** one idea — the family's **first per-front synthesis**. Every axis v32–v37 is *pairwise* (an unordered pair is its primary object); v38's primary object is a **front**. It is not a new crossing, fall, recovery, or ordering — it reads the `leading` edges v36 and v37 already classify and asks, per front, "is this front the weak side of a concession ordering **and** the weak side of a recovery ordering?" The use case closes the loop the two halves opened: v36 says "across the deal, **Cap concedes the floor before Term**"; v37 says "and **Term recovers last**"; v38 says "**Term is the persistent weak front** — the counterparty gives ground on it early relative to one partner and restores it late relative to another, deal after deal." Joining is the whole point: each half is a directional half-truth; the conjunction is the standing exposure a deal lead actually defends.
> **Posture (unchanged, non-negotiable):** deterministic (the weak-front verdict is a pure reduction over the `leading` pairs v36/v37 already decide by integer cross-multiplication — no float threshold enters; the per-front signal is a count of leading edges, the verdict a boolean conjunction, the most-exposed pick an integer comparison, so the same artifacts → identical `weak_front_hash`, on any machine, forever), no AI / no probabilistic path, no server (N local files in, one report out; no socket), citable (the artifacts carry the same per-front binding floors v12 derived from each document's own clause and the team's own playbook — v38 adds no new claim), lints / references / positions — but never drafts, and never renders a legal conclusion. The command is **advisory and verifiable**: every artifact is hash-verified (a tampered round is a hard error, side-labeled by round index), and the ladders are checked for a match across the _whole_ sequence (any cross-ladder pair is refused) via the same `verifyCoherenceSequence` loader the twenty-one trend/exposure/persistence/breadth/recurrence/volatility/synchrony/settling/onset/latency/concurrency/relapse/tenure/affinity/recovery-affinity/opposition/precedence/concession/recovery-order commands use, unchanged. Every step passes the §3 filter or it does not ship.

# Part 0 — Intent

## §1. Why we're doing this

v36 and v37 split the directional precedence relation into its two halves and each, in its own §1 and Part XVIII, pointed at a prize neither could claim alone:

- **v36 (Part XVIII):** "Joined with v37, it names the deal's persistent weak front — the one that concedes first _and_ recovers last."
- **v37 (§1):** "A front that concedes first _and_ recovers last is the persistent weak point; a front that concedes first but recovers first is merely volatile."

Both deferred the join itself (v36 Part XVI, v37 Part XVI) as "a downstream read over two independent reports, not a new command." That deferral was right while the recovery half was unbuilt — there was nothing to join. With v37 shipped, the join is the natural next axis, and Open Question #1 of v37 steers there explicitly: the next axis should be **per-front**, not another pairwise direction. v38 is that per-front synthesis.

The signal it isolates is the one a deal lead defends:

- **The persistent weak front.** Term concedes the floor before Cap (v36 names the `(Cap, Term)` pair a `leading` concession with `first_conceder` = Term — Term gives ground first) _and_ Term recovers above the floor after Mmm (v37 names the `(Mmm, Term)` pair a `leading` recovery order with `last_recoverer` = Term — Term is restored last). Term is exposed on **both** directional axes — gives ground early, is restored late. It is the standing exposure the counterparty trades against the rest of the book, round after round. v36 alone calls it a first-conceder; v37 alone calls it a laggard; only the join calls it **persistently weak**.
- **The volatile front (cleared).** Fee concedes first against Cap (v36) but recovers *first* against everyone (v37) — it gives ground early but is restored early. It is volatile, not weak; the join clears it (`recovers_last_against` is empty), where reading v36 alone would flag it.

To v36 and v37 read separately this conjunction is invisible — each is a directional half-truth, and a consumer eyeballing two JSON reports has to compute the intersection by hand (and decide what "weak" means). v38 supplies the reduction and the one verdict whose meaning needs no tuning. Read the `leading` edges of both halves over the same N artifacts: per front, the partners it concedes first against (v36) and the partners it recovers last against (v37); whether both sets are non-empty (`weak`); the partners where *both* hold against the *same* counterpart (`confirmed_against` — the sharpest evidence); the deal's single **most-exposed front**; and whether any front is persistently weak (the gate). All of it is a reduction over the same `floors[]` the whole family reads — no new event, no new ordering, no new float.

## §2. What v38 is and is not

**It is:**

- A **document-free persistent-weak-front command.** `coherence-weak-front <r1.coherence.json> … <rN.coherence.json>` reads N ≥ 2 saved coherence artifacts in round order, verifies each, runs the cross-ladder guard across all of them, computes v36's concession order and v37's recovery order internally, and prints, per **front** that is the weak side of *either* ordering, the partners it `concedes_first_against` (v36 `leading` pairs where it is the `first_conceder`), the partners it `recovers_last_against` (v37 `leading` pairs where it is the `last_recoverer`), the `confirmed_against` (the intersection — partners where it both concedes first *and* recovers last), its `class` (`persistent-weak` / `conceding` / `lagging`), plus the deal's `most_exposed_front` (the front with the most total leading edges against it, `null` when none is persistently weak), the `weak_fronts` (every `persistent-weak` front, pinned), and `has_persistent_weak_front` (the gate verdict). Markdown (default) or `--format json`. No documents are read; the engine is never run.
- A **single deal-level per-front gate, on the conjunction.** `--fail-on-persistent-weak-front` exits 2 when at least one front is **both** a strict-majority first-conceder (v36) against some partner **and** a strict-majority last-recoverer (v37) against some partner — the deal's persistent weak point. This is the conjunction of two already-tuning-free strict-majority verdicts; it inherits their lack of a knob. A front that only concedes first (`conceding`) or only recovers last (`lagging`) clears it — those are directional half-signals v36 and v37 already gate on individually; v38's gate fires only on the *standing* exposure both halves confirm. A consumer wanting the stricter same-partner bar reads `confirmed_against` from the JSON.
- A **whole-sequence-verified input, sharing the v17–v37 front end.** Every artifact's `coherence_hash` is re-derived and checked on load (a tampered/corrupt round is a hard error, errors prefixed `round N:`). The v15/v16 cross-ladder guard runs across the sequence via the same `verifyCoherenceSequence` loader the twenty-one trend/exposure/…/recovery-order commands use, with no change to it.
- A **derived report carrying its own fingerprint.** The report carries a `weak_front_hash` (a stable SHA-256 over the canonical per-front set — the front, its sorted `concedes_first_against`, its sorted `recovers_last_against`, and its `class`; the derived `confirmed_against`, the `most_exposed_front`, and `has_persistent_weak_front` are omitted, so the fingerprint is integer-exact over the inputs), namespaced apart from every prior hash (`coherence_hash`, …, `concession_hash`, `recovery_order_hash`), so the same N artifacts in the same order always reproduce the same report.

**It is not:**

- **Not a new diff, classifier, predicate, ordering, or artifact format.** It reads the `leading` pairs v36 and v37 already classify — no new fall, recovery, crossing, or ordering math. v38 is a subcommand plus one pure module (`computeCoherenceWeakFront`) whose only new logic is a per-front reduction of two existing pairwise reports into a conjunction. `computeCoherenceConcession`, `computeCoherenceRecoveryOrder`, `parsePostureCoherenceJson`, the coherence schema, and every other function are unchanged.
- **Not the concession order command (v36).** v36 is *pairwise* and *directional* (falls only); it answers "which front concedes first within this pair?" v38 is *per-front* and *bidirectional*; it answers "is this front the weak side of a concession ordering **and** a recovery ordering across the deal?" A front that is a `first_conceder` in v36 is only `conceding` in v38 unless it is also a `last_recoverer` in v37.
- **Not the recovery order command (v37).** Symmetrically: v37 names the per-pair laggard; v38 asks whether that laggard is *also* a first-conceder somewhere. A `last_recoverer` in v37 is only `lagging` in v38 unless it also concedes first in v36.
- **Not a same-partner-only verdict.** The gate fires on a front that concedes first against *some* partner and recovers last against *some* partner — they need not be the *same* partner. A front given ground on early (vs Cap) and restored late (vs Mmm) is exposed on both axes even though the counterpart differs. The sharper same-partner evidence (`confirmed_against`) is reported for a consumer that wants it, but the gate uses the looser, more sensitive conjunction (a persistent weak front is rarely weak against only one counterpart).
- **Not a configurable "weak against at least K partners" gate.** v38 does not take a `--min-edges K` knob; the gate fires on the conjunction of two strict-majority verdicts, each already tuning-free. The per-front edge counts are in the JSON for a consumer that wants a different bar.
- **Not a per-pair reduction.** Unlike v32–v37, v38's primary object is a **front**, not a pair. The `concedes_first_against` / `recovers_last_against` lists name *which* partners, but the verdict and class are per-front.
- **Not a re-derivation of v36/v37's own gates.** v36's `concedes` and v37's `lags` are deal-level "does any leading pair exist?" booleans. v38's `has_persistent_weak_front` is strictly stronger: it requires a *single front* to be the weak side of **both**. A deal can trip v36 and v37 (different fronts lead each) yet clear v38 (no one front is weak on both).
- **Not a weak-front artifact.** v38 introduces no new on-disk format; the report is _derived_, recomputed on demand from the N auditable coherence inputs.
- **Not a sequence the command discovers.** It takes the artifacts **in round order** on the argv, exactly as the twenty-one trend/exposure/… commands do.
- **Not a browser surface.** An N-round per-front weak-front synthesis of uploaded artifacts is a CI/dashboard concern (mirroring v16–v37).

## §3. The posture filter (unchanged)

1. **Deterministic** — `computeCoherenceWeakFront` over N `PostureCoherence` objects is pure: it calls `computeCoherenceConcession` and `computeCoherenceRecoveryOrder` (themselves pure and `localeCompare`-pinned), reads only their `leading` pairs, accumulates per-front `concedes_first_against` / `recovers_last_against` lists (sorted by `localeCompare(_, "en")`, the same order the family uses), enumerates fronts in that pinned order, and picks the most-exposed front by **integer comparison** of leading-edge counts (earliest label on a tie), never a float compare. Identical artifacts in identical order → identical `weak_front_hash` on any machine.
2. **Honest about unstated data** — the join inherits v36/v37's §3 contract exactly: a front no document states in a round neither falls nor recovers into or out of it; a fall or recovery across a silent gap is attributed to the transition into the revealing round. A front that is the weak side of *neither* ordering (no leading edge against it in either half) carries no signal and is omitted from `fronts[]`, never ranked. A front weak on only one axis is reported (`conceding` or `lagging`) but never gated.
3. **Advisory** — the command names which front the counterparty both gives ground on first and restores last across the team's own floor. It asserts no legal conclusion.
4. **No server** — N local files in, one report out. No socket, no engine run.
5. **Additive** — a brand-new subcommand and one new pure module. Every existing command (`analyze`, `diff`, `compare`, `compare-coherence`, `coherence-trend`, …, `coherence-recovery-order`, `verify`) is byte-for-byte unchanged in _output and goldens_; `coherence-sequence.ts`, `coherence-concession.ts`, and `coherence-recovery-order.ts` are imported and reused **without modification** (v38 needs nothing exported that was not already public — like v19–v37, it touches no existing source file's behavior at all).

# Part I — The report (pure)

`src/report/coherence-weak-front.ts` (a new sibling to `coherence-recovery-order.ts`):

- **`WeakFrontClass`** — a front's exposure class: `persistent-weak` (a strict-majority first-conceder against ≥ 1 partner _and_ a strict-majority last-recoverer against ≥ 1 partner — exposed on both directional axes, the gate-worthy class), `conceding` (concedes first against ≥ 1 partner but is never a consistent last-recoverer — gives ground early but is restored on time, a directional half-signal), `lagging` (recovers last against ≥ 1 partner but never concedes first — restored late but does not give ground early). A front weak on neither axis is omitted.
- **`CoherenceWeakFrontEntry`** — one front's whole-sequence exposure relation: the `dimension`, the `concedes_first_against` (partners it is the v36 `first_conceder` against, sorted), the `recovers_last_against` (partners it is the v37 `last_recoverer` against, sorted), the `confirmed_against` (the intersection — partners where it both concedes first *and* recovers last, the sharpest same-partner evidence, sorted), and its `class`.
- **`CoherenceWeakFront`** — the whole-sequence reduction: `rounds` (count), `fronts[]` (the entries above, pinned by `dimension`), `class_counts` (the per-front tally by class), `weak_fronts` (every `persistent-weak` front's dimension, pinned — the headline), `most_exposed_front` (the persistent-weak front with the most total leading edges against it — `concedes_first_against.length + recovers_last_against.length` — earliest label on a tie, `null` when none is persistently weak), `has_persistent_weak_front` (`weak_fronts.length ≥ 1` — the gate verdict), and `weak_front_hash`.
- **`computeCoherenceWeakFront(rounds: PostureCoherence[])`** — the pure, IO-free core. Computes v36's concession and v37's recovery order over the same rounds, reduces their `leading` pairs to per-front `concedes_first_against` / `recovers_last_against` / `confirmed_against` lists, classifies each front, selects the most-exposed front by integer leading-edge count, and returns the reduction with a `weak_front_hash`. Requires ≥ 2 rounds (delegated to v36/v37, which require it).
- **`exposurePersistentlyWeak(weakFront)`** — the CI gate predicate: `weakFront.has_persistent_weak_front`. The per-front conjunction no other command exposes (v36 gates on a concession ordering existing; v37 on a recovery ordering existing; neither requires the *same front* to be the weak side of both).
- **`buildCoherenceWeakFrontJson(weakFront)`** / **`renderCoherenceWeakFrontSummary(weakFront)`** — the JSON (`schema: vaulytica.posture-weak-front.v1`) and human-readable renderers. The summary prints the most-exposed front (its concession partners, its recovery partners, and its confirmed counterparts, or none-weak when no front is persistently weak), the class tally, then one line per front (`persistent-weak` first), then the hash.

v38 imports the already-public `PostureCoherence` type, `computeCoherenceConcession` (v36), `computeCoherenceRecoveryOrder` (v37), and the shared hashing helpers; no existing source file changes.

# Part II — The command (headless)

`tools/cli/coherence-weak-front.ts` (a new sibling to `coherence-recovery-order.ts`):

- **`computeCoherenceWeakFrontArtifacts(texts, format?)`** — the pure CLI core. Verifies all N artifacts and runs the cross-ladder guard via the shared `verifyCoherenceSequence` loader (unchanged from v18–v37); on any malformed/tampered round returns `{ ok: false, errors }` (each prefixed `round N:`); on a cross-ladder pair returns `{ ok: false }` naming the two rounds; an unpinned artifact yields `{ ok: true }` with a `ladderNote`. Then `computeCoherenceWeakFront(rounds)` and renders (markdown summary or JSON), returning `{ ok: true, output, weak, ladderNote }`.
- **`runCoherenceWeakFront(argv)`** — the CLI handler: reads the N files, calls the core, writes the `ladderNote` (if any) to stderr, prints the report to stdout, and — under `--fail-on-persistent-weak-front` — exits 2 when any front is both a strict-majority first-conceder and a strict-majority last-recoverer. A malformed/tampered/cross-ladder input is a hard exit-1 error. Requires ≥ 2 positionals.

`tools/cli/run.ts` (the dispatcher) gains a `coherence-weak-front` case and a `USAGE` entry.

```
# Each round, archive its coherence (kilobytes, no clause text):
vaulytica analyze 'round1/*.docx' --playbook-file team.playbook.json --posture --emit-coherence round1.coherence.json
vaulytica analyze 'round2/*.docx' --playbook-file team.playbook.json --posture --emit-coherence round2.coherence.json
vaulytica analyze 'round3/*.docx' --playbook-file team.playbook.json --posture --emit-coherence round3.coherence.json

# Later — name the deal's persistent weak front (concedes first AND recovers last), from the archive alone:
vaulytica coherence-weak-front round1.coherence.json round2.coherence.json round3.coherence.json \
  --fail-on-persistent-weak-front
```

# Part XVI — Principled deferrals

- **A `--same-partner` flag (gate on `confirmed_against` instead of the looser conjunction).** ⬜ Not built. The same-partner intersection is reported in the JSON for a consumer that wants the stricter bar; the gate uses the more sensitive cross-partner conjunction (a persistent weak front is rarely weak against only one counterpart, and the looser gate catches it earlier). Adding the flag would give the command two gates; v38 keeps exactly one (v18–v37 made the same call).
- **A configurable "weak against at least K partners" gate (`--min-edges K`).** ⬜ Not built. The gate fires on the conjunction of two strict-majority verdicts, each already tuning-free. The per-front edge counts are in the JSON for a consumer wanting a different bar.
- **A weighted exposure score (how *deep* below the floor, or how *long*).** ⬜ Not built. The floor is binary (`below-acceptable` is the only sub-floor rung), so depth-weighting is not viable; a duration weighting is a magnitude read (v37 Part XVI's deferred mean-lead-time), orthogonal to this conjunction.
- **A per-front transitive rank (the single deal-wide weakest front by topological order).** ⬜ Not built. v38 reports each front's leading edges independently and picks the most-exposed by edge count; composing the pairwise leaders into a deal-wide total order is a graph reduction over the two halves' `pairs[]`, deferred exactly as v37 deferred its transitive recovery chain.
- **A standalone weak-front artifact (`--emit-weak-front`).** ⬜ Deferred, for the same reason v14–v37 keep the derived thing derived: the report is cheaply recomputable from the N coherence artifacts on demand.
- **A directory/glob walker that infers round order.** ⬜ Not built. The command takes artifacts in round order on the argv (the caller's contract, mirroring the twenty-one trend/exposure/… commands).
- **A browser surface for an N-round weak-front synthesis.** ⬜ Deferred (v16–v37 Part XVI). The browser does an in-session two-round comparison; an N-artifact per-front join is a CI/dashboard concern.

# Part XVII — Open questions for the maintainer

1. **Is the precedence family now complete?** v32/v33 (same-step fall/recovery affinity), v34 (same-step opposition), v35 (direction-blind precedence), v36 (fall-order), v37 (recovery-order), and v38 (the per-front join of v36+v37) close the directional family and its first synthesis. Recommendation: **treat the pairwise-precedence-and-its-join as complete** — the natural next axes are *magnitude* (mean lead-time, how long the weak front stays exposed, in rounds) or a fresh *per-front cadence* read (below/above oscillation rate), not another pairwise direction or another conjunction over the same edges.
2. **Gate on the same-partner `confirmed_against` instead?** Today the gate fires on the cross-partner conjunction (weak on falls vs *someone*, weak on recoveries vs *someone*); `confirmed_against` (weak on both vs the *same* someone) is in the JSON. Recommendation: **keep the looser gate** — it is more sensitive and the strict evidence is one field away for a dashboard that wants to pin it.

# Part XVIII — What this gives the user

- **The deal's persistent weak point, named from the archive alone.** Archive each round's kilobyte coherence artifact as you go; `coherence-weak-front` reduces the fall-order (v36) and the recovery-order (v37) to a single per-front verdict — the front the counterparty both **gives ground on first** and **restores last**, exposed coming and going. It distinguishes the *persistently weak* front (both) from the merely *volatile* one (concedes first but recovers first) and the merely *slow* one (recovers last but never concedes first), with no clause text checked out and no re-analysis run. The "which front is our standing exposure?" question a deal lead asks, answered deterministically from N small, verifiable files — the synthesis v36 and v37 each pointed at but neither could make alone.
- **A gate on a standing exposure, not a directional half-signal.** `--fail-on-persistent-weak-front` fires only when a single front is the weak side of **both** orderings — strictly stronger than v36's or v37's own gate, which each fire on a directional ordering existing at all. It catches the exposure that a fall-order read, a recovery-order read, a same-step affinity, a direction-blind precedence, and a per-step count structurally cannot isolate, because none of them join the two directions on a single front.
- **The same five promises.** Deterministic, no AI, no server, citable, never drafts — every line of v38 passes the §3 gate. It reads the `leading` edges v36 and v37 already classify, through the loader v18–v37 already share; it adds one per-front conjunction over those edges, no on-disk format, and no change to any existing source file's behavior — every existing surface is byte-for-byte unchanged.
