# Vaulytica v25 — Document-Free Exposure Synchrony (Per-Round Floor Crossings)

> **Status:** **Shipped (9.22.0).** v24 gave the posture archive a *per-front crossing-count* axis: per front, how many times its standing flipped across the acceptable floor across the whole deal (`crossings`). v22 gave it a *per-round level* axis: per round, how many fronts sat below the floor (`exposed_fronts`). But neither reads the archive on the one remaining axis a deal lead reconciling a turbulent close needs: per round-**transition**, how many fronts *crossed* the floor in the **same step**. v24 fixes a front and sums its crossings *over the rounds*; v22 fixes a round and counts *standings* (a static below/at-or-above snapshot, not a movement). Neither can say "round 3 is where the package **lurched** — three fronts moved across the floor at once." A deal can have no single *volatile* front (v24 clears: every front crossed at most once) yet still have a round where several fronts crossed *together* — a coordinated shift v24's per-front sum structurally cannot pose, because it slices the crossings by front, not by step. That is a missing **axis**: the *per-transition transpose* of v24's crossing count. v25 supplies it: re-bucket the **same** floor crossings v24 counts — by *step* instead of by *front* — and report, per round-transition, how many fronts crossed the floor at once (`crossing_fronts`), the deal's **peak transition** (the single step the most fronts crossed together), and whether any step was **synchronized** (two or more fronts crossing the floor in one transition). It gates, with no tuning, on any synchronized step. It continues the global step numbering after v24's Step 204, beginning at **Step 205**. One new subcommand, one pure module, **zero** change to any existing source file.
> **Scope:** one idea — the *per-step co-movement* reduction the per-front crossing count and the per-round level count both leave out. v24 answers *how many times did a front cross the floor across the deal* (a per-front sum); v22 answers *how many fronts sat below floor each round* (a per-round level snapshot); v25 answers *how many fronts crossed the floor together in each step* (a per-transition movement count). It is the literal transpose of v24: the sum of every transition's `crossing_fronts` equals the sum of every front's v24 `crossings` (`total_crossings`) — v24 slices the crossings by front, v25 slices them by step. The use case is the same archive of per-round coherence artifacts: v24 says "the Cap front crossed the floor twice over the deal"; v25 says "and round 1→2 is the step where Cap *and* Term crossed at once — a coordinated lurch no single-front reading shows."
> **Posture (unchanged, non-negotiable):** deterministic (the per-step crossing count is a pure re-bucketing over the same `below-acceptable` rung v10 defines, taken over the binding floors v12 already derived and v20/v22/v24 already read; same artifacts → identical `synchrony_hash`, on any machine, forever), no AI / no probabilistic path, no server (N local files in, one report out; no socket), citable (the artifacts carry the same per-front binding floors v12 derived from each document's own clause and the team's own playbook — v25 adds no new claim), lints / references / positions — but never drafts, and never renders a legal conclusion. The command is **advisory and verifiable**: every artifact is hash-verified (a tampered round is a hard error, side-labeled by round index), and the ladders are checked for a match across the *whole* sequence (any cross-ladder pair is refused) via the same `verifyCoherenceSequence` loader the eight trend/exposure/persistence/breadth/recurrence/volatility commands use, unchanged. Every step passes the §3 filter or it does not ship.

# Part 0 — Intent

## §1. Why we're doing this

v24 gave the posture archive a per-front crossing-count axis — per front, how many times its standing flipped across the acceptable floor (`crossings`), falls and recoveries alike. That is the right shape for "which front could not settle?" But it is a *per-front* reduction: it fixes a front and sums its crossings *over the rounds*. It discards *when* each crossing happened, and so it cannot see crossings that happened *in the same step*. v22 reads the archive *per round*, but only on the *level*: it counts how many fronts sat *below* the floor each round — a static standing, not a movement. A deal lead reconciling a turbulent close asks a question neither the per-front sum nor the per-round level can answer: **was there a single round where the package lurched — where several fronts crossed the floor at once?**

v24 cannot answer it, because a per-front crossing count is sliced by *front*. Two deals v24 holds as different can report identically; and two deals it holds as the *same* can be very different:

- **The coordinated lurch.** In round 1→2, both Cap and Term fall below floor *in the same step* (a single redline round that knocked two fronts below floor at once). Each front has one crossing; *neither* is `volatile` to v24 (each crossed only once), so v24's gate clears. Yet the package took a coordinated hit in one round — exactly the event a deal lead wants flagged.
- **The staggered drift.** Cap falls in round 1→2 and Term falls in round 2→3 — two crossings, but on *different* steps. To a per-front reading this looks the same as the lurch (two single-crossing fronts). To a per-step reading it is the opposite: no single step has more than one crossing; nothing lurched.

To v24 these are indistinguishable (two monotone fronts each). To v22 the *level* differs only in the final round's count, not in *when* the package moved. None of the existing commands reports the one fact that separates a coordinated lurch from a staggered drift: the count of fronts that crossed the floor *together*, in one transition.

This is not a bug in v24 — a per-front crossing count is *supposed* to sum over rounds, not bucket by step. It is a missing **axis**: the *per-transition* dimension of the same crossing data. Where v24 counts, per front, how many times it crossed *over the deal*, v25 counts, per step, how many fronts crossed *in that step*.

v25 supplies it. Re-bucket the **same** floor crossings v24 counts — by step instead of by front — and report, per round-transition, how many fronts crossed the floor at once (`crossing_fronts`). The headline follows: the deal's **peak transition** (the single step the most fronts crossed together) and whether any step was **synchronized** (two or more fronts crossing in one transition). The gate fires on a synchronized step — the one co-movement condition whose meaning ("the package lurched in one round") needs no tuning. All of this is a re-bucketing of the crossings derivable from the `floors[]` array v22/v24 already carry — the sum of every transition's count equals v24's grand crossing total.

## §2. What v25 is and is not

**It is:**
- A **document-free synchrony command.** `coherence-synchrony <r1.coherence.json> … <rN.coherence.json>` reads N ≥ 2 saved coherence artifacts in round order, verifies each, runs the cross-ladder guard across all of them, and prints, *per round-transition*, how many fronts crossed the floor in that step (`crossing_fronts`), which fronts (`crossed_dimensions`), and a `synchrony` class — `synchronized` (≥ 2 fronts crossed at once), `isolated` (exactly 1), or `quiet` (0) — plus the deal's **peak transition** (`peak_transition` / `peak_count`), the **synchronized-step count** (`synchronized_count`), and the **grand crossing total** (`total_crossings`, equal to v24's per-front sum). Markdown (default) or `--format json`. No documents are read; the engine is never run.
- A **single deal-level synchrony gate.** `--fail-on-synchronized-exposure` exits 2 when any single step crossed the floor with two or more fronts at once (`synchronized_count > 0`). This is the *co-movement* counterpart to v24's *per-front instability* gate and v22's *deal-breadth* gate: where `--fail-on-volatile-exposure` fires on a front whose standing crossed the floor ≥ 2 times *across the whole deal*, `--fail-on-synchronized-exposure` fires on a single *step* where ≥ 2 fronts crossed *together*. A deal with no volatile front still trips it if two fronts crossed in one round; a deal whose two crossings fell on different steps does not. It needs no tuning — two fronts crossing at once is the threshold that *means* the package lurched.
- A **whole-sequence-verified input, sharing the v17–v24 front end.** Every artifact's `coherence_hash` is re-derived and checked on load (a tampered/corrupt round is a hard error, errors prefixed `round N:`). The v15/v16 cross-ladder guard runs across the sequence via the same `verifyCoherenceSequence` loader the eight trend/exposure/persistence/breadth/recurrence/volatility commands use, with no change to it.
- A **derived report carrying its own fingerprint.** The synchrony carries a `synchrony_hash` (a stable SHA-256 over the canonical per-transition set), namespaced apart from every `coherence_hash`, `movement_hash`, `trajectory_hash`, `shift_trajectory_hash`, `arc_hash`, `exposure_hash`, `persistence_hash`, `breadth_hash`, `recurrence_hash`, and `volatility_hash`, so the same N artifacts in the same order always reproduce the same report.

**It is not:**
- **Not a new diff, classifier, predicate, or artifact format.** It reuses the `weakest_tier` binding floor v12 derives and the `below-acceptable` rung v10 defines; v25 is a subcommand plus one pure module (`computeCoherenceSynchrony`) whose only new logic is a per-transition re-bucketing of the floor crossings over the same `floors[]` array v24 scans. `parsePostureCoherenceJson`, the coherence schema, and every trajectory/exposure/persistence/breadth/recurrence/volatility function are unchanged.
- **Not a per-front command.** v24 fixes a front and sums its crossings over the rounds (`crossings`); v25 fixes a *step* and counts the fronts crossing in it (`crossing_fronts`). A consumer wanting one front's instability over the deal runs v24; one wanting one round's co-movement runs v25. They are the transpose of each other over the same crossing matrix, and their totals agree (`total_crossings`).
- **Not a level command.** v22 fixes a round and counts the fronts *below floor* that round (a static standing); v25 fixes a *transition* and counts the fronts that *crossed* the floor in that step (a movement). A front stuck below floor for all N rounds counts in *every* v22 round but in *zero* v25 transitions (it never moved). v25 reports the *crossings per step*, not the *standings per round*; a consumer wanting the level runs v22, and one wanting both runs v22 and v25.
- **Not silence-splits-a-step.** A round no document states does **not** count as a crossing and does **not** reset the standing: per §3 (the v21/v24 contract), silence after a fall keeps the last known standing — it is neither a recovery nor a fall. A crossing across a silent gap is attributed to the transition *into the round that reveals the new standing*, never to the silent step. So `below → unstated → below` contributes **zero** crossings (silence does not flip the side), and `below → unstated → acceptable` contributes **one** crossing, on the step ending at the third round (the recovery, just unstated in the gap round). This keeps v25 from inventing a crossing — or mislocating one onto a silent step.
- **Not a configurable-threshold "too turbulent" gate.** v25 does not take a `--fail-over N` knob; the gate fires on `crossing_fronts ≥ 2` in any step, the one co-movement condition whose meaning ("the package lurched in one round") needs no tuning. A team wanting to gate on a *higher* simultaneity reads `peak_count` from the JSON. (v20/v21/v22/v23/v24 made the same call against a configurable floor/duration/breadth/episode/crossing count.)
- **Not a trajectory-whipsaw command.** v17's `whipsaw` fires on rung jitter anywhere on the ladder, including movement entirely *above* the floor. v25's crossing count is specific to the **floor boundary**: an above-floor whipsaw (`acceptable → ideal → acceptable`) contributes zero crossings to *every* step (all `quiet`). v25 isolates the synchronized instability that matters for exposure — fronts crossing the floor together — from rung jitter that never risks it.
- **Not a synchrony artifact.** v25 introduces no new on-disk format; the synchrony is *derived*, recomputed on demand from the N auditable coherence inputs — the same "keep the derived thing derived" discipline v14–v24 hold.
- **Not a sequence the command discovers.** It does not glob a directory or sort filenames; the caller passes the artifacts **in round order** on the argv, exactly as the eight trend/exposure/persistence/breadth/recurrence/volatility commands do.
- **Not a browser surface.** An N-round synchrony of uploaded artifacts is a CI/dashboard concern, which is what this command serves (mirroring v16–v24).

## §3. The posture filter (unchanged)

1. **Deterministic** — `computeCoherenceSynchrony` over N `PostureCoherence` objects is pure: it matches fronts by dimension, pins each step's crossed-dimension list by `localeCompare(_, "en")` (the same order the trajectory/exposure/volatility functions use), and counts each step's floor crossings over the caller's round order. Identical artifacts in identical order → identical `synchrony_hash` on any machine.
2. **Honest about unstated data** — a front no document states in a round does not cross into or out of that round. Within a front's path, an unstated round is skipped: it neither counts as a crossing nor resets the standing (silence is not a fall or a recovery — the §3 contract that keeps `unstated` never-flagged in v20 and `below → unstated → below` zero crossings in v24). A crossing across a silent gap is attributed to the transition into the round that *reveals* the new standing.
3. **Advisory** — the command names how many fronts crossed the team's own floor in each step. It asserts no legal conclusion.
4. **No server** — N local files in, one report out. No socket, no engine run.
5. **Additive** — a brand-new subcommand and one new pure module. Every existing command (`analyze`, `diff`, `compare`, `compare-coherence`, `coherence-trend`, `coherence-shift-trend`, `coherence-arc`, `coherence-exposure`, `coherence-persistence`, `coherence-breadth`, `coherence-recurrence`, `coherence-volatility`, `verify`) is byte-for-byte unchanged in *output and goldens*; `coherence-sequence.ts` is imported and reused **without modification** (v25 needs nothing exported that was not already public — like v19–v24, it touches no existing source file's behavior at all).

# Part I — The synchrony (pure)

`src/report/coherence-synchrony.ts` (a new sibling to `coherence-volatility.ts`):

- **`SynchronyClass`** — a transition's co-movement class: `synchronized` (≥ 2 fronts crossed the floor in this one step), `isolated` (exactly 1), `quiet` (0).
- **`CoherenceSynchronyTransition`** — one round-transition's deal-wide movement: the `from_round` / `to_round` (1-based), `crossing_fronts` (the count — the verdict for this step), `crossed_dimensions` (the fronts that crossed, `localeCompare`-pinned), and the `synchrony` class.
- **`CoherenceSynchrony`** — the whole-sequence per-transition series: `rounds` (count), `per_transition[]` (the transitions above, in sequence order, length = rounds − 1), `total_crossings` (every front's crossing summed over the steps — equal to v24's grand total), `peak_transition` / `peak_count` (the step the most fronts crossed at once, earliest on a tie — `null` / `0` when no front ever crossed), `synchronized_count` (steps with ≥ 2 crossings — the gate-worthy count), and `synchrony_hash`.
- **`computeCoherenceSynchrony(rounds: PostureCoherence[])`** — the pure, IO-free core. For each front, scans `floors[]` and attributes each *stated* floor crossing to the transition that reveals it (an unstated round is skipped, §3); buckets the crossings per transition, classifies each step, finds the peak, and returns the series with a `synchrony_hash`. Requires ≥ 2 rounds.
- **`exposureSynchronized(synchrony)`** — the CI gate predicate: `synchrony.synchronized_count > 0`. The co-movement verdict no other command exposes (v22 gates on the deal's breadth trend; v24 on a single front's crossing count; this gates on fronts crossing *together* in one step).
- **`buildCoherenceSynchronyJson(synchrony)`** / **`renderCoherenceSynchronySummary(synchrony)`** — the JSON (`schema: vaulytica.posture-synchrony.v1`) and human-readable renderers. The summary prints the peak step and the synchronized-step count, then one line per step (its crossing count and which fronts crossed), then the hash.

v25 imports only the already-public `PostureCoherence` type, the `NegotiationTier` type, and the shared hashing helpers; the `below-acceptable` literal is a plain string; no existing source file changes.

# Part II — The command (headless)

`tools/cli/coherence-synchrony.ts` (a new sibling to `coherence-volatility.ts`):

- **`computeCoherenceSynchronyArtifacts(texts, format?)`** — the pure CLI core. Verifies all N artifacts and runs the cross-ladder guard via the shared `verifyCoherenceSequence` loader (unchanged from v18–v24); on any malformed/tampered round returns `{ ok: false, errors }` (each prefixed `round N:`); on a cross-ladder pair returns `{ ok: false }` naming the two rounds; an unpinned artifact yields `{ ok: true }` with a `ladderNote`. Then `computeCoherenceSynchrony(rounds)` and renders (markdown summary or JSON), returning `{ ok: true, output, synchronized, ladderNote }`.
- **`runCoherenceSynchrony(argv)`** — the CLI handler: reads the N files, calls the core, writes the `ladderNote` (if any) to stderr, prints the synchrony to stdout, and — under `--fail-on-synchronized-exposure` — exits 2 when any single step crossed the floor with two or more fronts at once. A malformed/tampered/cross-ladder input is a hard exit-1 error. Requires ≥ 2 positionals.

`tools/cli/run.ts` (the dispatcher) gains a `coherence-synchrony` case and a `USAGE` entry.

```
# Each round, archive its coherence (kilobytes, no clause text):
vaulytica analyze 'round1/*.docx' --playbook-file team.playbook.json --posture --emit-coherence round1.coherence.json
vaulytica analyze 'round2/*.docx' --playbook-file team.playbook.json --posture --emit-coherence round2.coherence.json
vaulytica analyze 'round3/*.docx' --playbook-file team.playbook.json --posture --emit-coherence round3.coherence.json

# Later — read/gate whether any single round saw two or more fronts cross the floor at once, from the archive alone:
vaulytica coherence-synchrony round1.coherence.json round2.coherence.json round3.coherence.json \
  --fail-on-synchronized-exposure
```

# Part XVI — Principled deferrals

- **A `coherence-volatility --by-step` flag.** ⬜ Not built — and deliberately not, mirroring v18–v24 Part XVI. v25 is a separate command, not a flag on the per-front crossing command, so each command keeps exactly one gate and one hash.
- **A configurable "too turbulent" gate (`--fail-over N` simultaneous crossings).** ⬜ Not built. The gate fires on `crossing_fronts ≥ 2` (the package lurched), the one co-movement condition whose meaning needs no tuning. `peak_count` is in the JSON for a consumer that wants to threshold on a higher simultaneity.
- **A standalone synchrony artifact (`--emit-synchrony`).** ⬜ Deferred, for the same reason v14–v24 keep the derived thing derived: the synchrony is cheaply recomputable from the N coherence artifacts on demand, keeping the auditable inputs (each ladder-pinned, hash-verified) as the source of truth.
- **A *direction-aware* per-step model (separate down-crossings from up-crossings in a step).** ⬜ Not built. The `crossed_dimensions` list plus the per-front `floors[]` already let a consumer split a step's crossings into falls and recoveries; v25 headlines the total count, since the co-movement question ("did several fronts move at once?") needs only the total. Add it if a dashboard asks to pin the split per step.
- **A *direction-homogeneous* synchrony (gate only when the simultaneous crossings move the same way).** ⬜ Not built. A step where one front falls and another recovers is still a coordinated move; v25 counts both. A team wanting "two fronts *fell* together" reads the `floors[]` matrix at the peak step.
- **A directory/glob walker that infers round order.** ⬜ Not built. The command takes artifacts in round order on the argv (the caller's contract, mirroring the trend/exposure/persistence/breadth/recurrence/volatility commands).
- **A browser surface for an N-round synchrony.** ⬜ Deferred (v16–v24 Part XVI). The browser does an in-session two-round comparison; an N-artifact synchrony is a CI/dashboard concern.

# Part XVII — Open questions for the maintainer

1. **Fold synchrony into a single "deal posture" command that reports per-front *and* per-step crossings?** Today the per-front crossing command (v24) and the per-step crossing command (v25) are split. A dashboard wanting both runs both and joins on the shared `floors[]` matrix (their totals agree by construction). Recommendation: **keep them split at the command layer** — they carry categorically different gates (per-front "a front reversed" vs per-step "the package lurched") and different hashes; bolting one onto the other would give that command two unlike gates. If a dashboard asks for the combined view, add a composing command the way v19 joined v17 and v18, not a flag.
2. **Report the *lurch direction* of the peak step (did the package mostly fall or mostly recover)?** The `crossed_dimensions` at the peak step plus the `floors[]` matrix already let a consumer read each crossing's direction. Recommendation: **defer** — the matrix exposes it, and a single "the peak step was a net fall" headline has no consumer yet; add it if a dashboard asks to pin one.

# Part XVIII — What this gives the user

- **The co-movement of the deal, from the archive alone.** Archive each round's kilobyte coherence artifact as you go; `coherence-synchrony` shows, per step, not just *how many times a front crossed the floor over the deal* (v24) or *how many fronts sat below floor each round* (v22) but *how many fronts crossed the floor together in each transition* — distinguishing a coordinated lurch (two fronts crossing in one round) from a staggered drift (two fronts crossing in different rounds, which v24 reports identically as two monotone fronts), with no clause text checked out and no re-analysis run. The "which round did the package lurch?" question a deal lead asks at a turbulent close, answered deterministically from N small, verifiable files.
- **A gate on co-movement, not just per-front instability, level, or episode count.** `--fail-on-synchronized-exposure` fires when a single step crossed the floor with two or more fronts at once — a coordinated shift the per-front gates (v23/v24) and the level/duration gates (v20/v21/v22) structurally cannot pose, since each sums or counts along the *front* axis or a static *level*. It catches the package lurch even when no single front is volatile, and ignores a deal whose crossings were spread across separate rounds.
- **The same five promises.** Deterministic, no AI, no server, citable, never drafts — every line of v25 passes the §3 gate. It reads the binding floor v12 already derives and the `below-acceptable` rung v10 already defines, through the loader v18–v24 already share; it adds one per-transition re-bucketing of the crossings v24 already counts, no on-disk format, and no change to any existing source file's behavior — every existing surface is byte-for-byte unchanged.
