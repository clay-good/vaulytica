# Vaulytica v31 ‚Äî Document-Free Exposure Tenure (Below-Floor Occupancy Share)

> **Status:** **Shipped (9.28.0).** v21 read the posture archive on the **current-standing** axis: per front, a raw count of the rounds it sat below the acceptable floor (`rounds_below`) and whether its _latest stated_ floor is still below acceptable (`open`/`resolved`). Its gate fires on the **endpoint** ‚Äî a front below floor _now_. But the endpoint discards the one axis a deal lead asks auditing the whole package after the close: **across the rounds that actually put a front on the table, how much of that span was it an unaccepted position ‚Äî was it below floor more often than not?** A front that dipped below floor once in round 2 and recovered, and a front that sat below floor through rounds 1‚Äì4 and recovered only at round 5, are **identical** to v21 (both `resolved`, both clear `--fail-on-open-exposure`) ‚Äî yet one was a brief dip and the other was an unacceptable position for the _majority_ of the deal. None of the existing commands reports the **share of a front's stated span spent below floor**. That is a missing **axis**: the _occupancy_ dimension of the same below-floor data ‚Äî not the per-episode count (v23), the per-episode below-duration (v28), nor the endpoint standing (v21), but the **aggregate fraction** of a front's stated rounds that sat below floor. v31 supplies it: per front, the rounds it was below floor as a fraction of the rounds that **stated** it (`share`), the deal's heaviest such share (`max_share` / `heaviest_dimension`), and ‚Äî the one tuning-free verdict ‚Äî whether any front was below floor for a strict **majority** of its stated rounds (`majority`). It continues the global step numbering after v30's Step 210, beginning at **Step 211**. One new subcommand, one pure module, **zero** change to any existing source file.
> **Scope:** one idea ‚Äî the _occupancy_ reduction the whole posture family never took. v20 answers _how low did a front ever get_; v21 answers _how long was a front down, and is it still down (a raw count + the endpoint)_; v23 answers _how many below-floor episodes did a front have_; v28 answers _how long did each episode sit below floor_; v31 answers _what share of the rounds that discussed a front was it below floor_. Where v21 divides `rounds_below` by the _total_ round count and headlines the endpoint, v31 divides by the front's **stated** rounds (the ¬ß3-honest denominator ‚Äî silence neither exposes a front nor dilutes its share) and headlines the **burden**. `total_below_rounds` equals v21's `rounds_below` summed across fronts, by construction. The use case is the same archive of per-round coherence artifacts: v21 says "the Cap front is still below floor"; v31 says "and the Term front, though it recovered by the close, was below floor for 80% of the rounds it was on the table ‚Äî a chronic burden, not a fresh dip."
> **Posture (unchanged, non-negotiable):** deterministic (the tenure is a pure per-front occupancy count over the same `below-acceptable` rung v10 defines, taken over the binding floors v12 already derived and v20/v21 already read; the heaviest-share pick is decided by integer cross-multiplication, never a float compare, so the same artifacts ‚Üí identical `tenure_hash`, on any machine, forever), no AI / no probabilistic path, no server (N local files in, one report out; no socket), citable (the artifacts carry the same per-front binding floors v12 derived from each document's own clause and the team's own playbook ‚Äî v31 adds no new claim), lints / references / positions ‚Äî but never drafts, and never renders a legal conclusion. The command is **advisory and verifiable**: every artifact is hash-verified (a tampered round is a hard error, side-labeled by round index), and the ladders are checked for a match across the _whole_ sequence (any cross-ladder pair is refused) via the same `verifyCoherenceSequence` loader the fourteen trend/exposure/persistence/breadth/recurrence/volatility/synchrony/settling/onset/latency/concurrency/relapse commands use, unchanged. Every step passes the ¬ß3 filter or it does not ship.

# Part 0 ‚Äî Intent

## ¬ß1. Why we're doing this

v21 gave the posture archive a duration-and-standing axis: per front, a count of the rounds it sat below floor (`rounds_below`) and whether its latest stated floor is still below acceptable (`open`/`resolved`). The gate (`--fail-on-open-exposure`) fires on the **endpoint** ‚Äî a front below floor _now_. That is the right shape for "is this wound still open?" But a deal lead auditing the whole package has a second, categorically different question: not "is it open now?" but "across the rounds that discussed this front, **how much of that span was it an unaccepted position** ‚Äî was it below floor more often than not?"

No command answers it, because the endpoint throws the occupancy away. Two fronts v21 reports identically (both `resolved` ‚Äî recovered by the close, gate clears) can be opposites on this axis:

- **The brief dip.** Cap is stated in 5 rounds, below floor in round 2 only, then recovers and holds ‚Äî below floor **1 of 5** stated rounds (a 20% tenure). `resolved`.
- **The chronic burden.** Cap is stated in 5 rounds, below floor in rounds 1‚Äì4, and recovers only at round 5 ‚Äî below floor **4 of 5** stated rounds (an 80% tenure). Also `resolved` to v21 (it recovered by the close), yet it was an unacceptable position for the _majority_ of the deal.

To v21 these are indistinguishable (both `resolved`). The opposite asymmetry is just as real: a front below floor in only its **final** round (1 of 5) is `open` to v21 (the gate trips ‚Äî a fresh, late dip) but a clear minority to v31 (the gate clears ‚Äî it was acceptable for 80% of the deal). The endpoint and the occupancy are different reductions; neither implies the other.

This is not a bug in v21 ‚Äî a _current-standing_ reduction is supposed to read the endpoint. It is a missing **axis**: the _occupancy_ dimension of the same below-floor data. And it carries a denominator correction v21 lacks: v21's render divides `rounds_below` by the **total** round count `n` ("below floor 2 of 5 rounds"), which dilutes a front that was only ever stated in a few rounds. v31 divides by the front's **stated** rounds ‚Äî so a front on the table twice and below floor both times reads **100%** (it was an unaccepted position every time it was discussed), not 40% diluted by three rounds of silence it was never part of.

v31 supplies it. Read the same artifacts per-front: how many rounds stated the front (the denominator), how many of those sat below floor (the numerator), the resulting `share`, the deal's heaviest share, and whether any front was below floor for a strict majority of its stated rounds. The gate follows: `--fail-on-majority-below` fires when a front was below floor for more than half its stated span ‚Äî the one occupancy boundary whose meaning ("below floor more often than not") needs no tuning. All of this is a reduction over the `floors[]` matrix every artifact already carries.

## ¬ß2. What v31 is and is not

**It is:**

- A **document-free tenure command.** `coherence-tenure <r1.coherence.json> ‚Ä¶ <rN.coherence.json>` reads N ‚â• 2 saved coherence artifacts in round order, verifies each, runs the cross-ladder guard across all of them, and prints, per front, how many rounds **stated** it (`stated_rounds`), how many of those sat **below floor** (`below_rounds`), the occupancy `share` (`below_rounds / stated_rounds`), and its occupancy `tenure` class ‚Äî plus the deal's **heaviest occupancy** (`max_share` / `heaviest_dimension`, `null` when no front was ever below floor), the `total_below_rounds` (equal to v21's `rounds_below` summed) and `total_stated_rounds` (the grand denominator). Markdown (default) or `--format json`. No documents are read; the engine is never run.
- A **single deal-level occupancy gate.** `--fail-on-majority-below` exits 2 when at least one front sat below floor for a strict **majority** of its stated rounds (`below_rounds √ó 2 > stated_rounds`). This is the _occupancy_ counterpart to v21's _current-standing_ gate: where v21 fires on a front below floor at its latest stated round (including a fresh, late dip in 1 of many rounds), this fires on the _aggregate burden_ ‚Äî a front below floor for more than half the span it was discussed, **even if it recovered by the close**. A front below floor for exactly half its stated rounds (or fewer) clears it; a consumer wanting a different bar reads `share` and `max_share` from the JSON. It needs no tuning ‚Äî a strict majority _is_ "below floor more often than not."
- A **whole-sequence-verified input, sharing the v17‚Äìv30 front end.** Every artifact's `coherence_hash` is re-derived and checked on load (a tampered/corrupt round is a hard error, errors prefixed `round N:`). The v15/v16 cross-ladder guard runs across the sequence via the same `verifyCoherenceSequence` loader the fourteen trend/exposure/persistence/breadth/recurrence/volatility/synchrony/settling/onset/latency/concurrency/relapse commands use, with no change to it.
- A **derived report carrying its own fingerprint.** The tenure carries a `tenure_hash` (a stable SHA-256 over the canonical per-front occupancy set ‚Äî the integer `stated_rounds`/`below_rounds` and the class; the derived float `share` is omitted, so the fingerprint is integer-exact), namespaced apart from every `coherence_hash`, `movement_hash`, `trajectory_hash`, `shift_trajectory_hash`, `arc_hash`, `exposure_hash`, `persistence_hash`, `breadth_hash`, `recurrence_hash`, `volatility_hash`, `synchrony_hash`, `settling_hash`, `onset_hash`, `latency_hash`, `concurrency_hash`, and `relapse_hash`, so the same N artifacts in the same order always reproduce the same report.

**It is not:**

- **Not a new diff, classifier, predicate, or artifact format.** It reuses the `weakest_tier` binding floor v12 derives and the `below-acceptable` rung v10 defines; v31 is a subcommand plus one pure module (`computeCoherenceTenure`) whose only new logic is a per-front below-floor count over the front's stated rounds and a strict-majority test. `parsePostureCoherenceJson`, the coherence schema, and every trajectory/exposure/persistence/‚Ä¶/relapse function are unchanged.
- **Not the persistence command.** v21 reports a raw `rounds_below` count against the _total_ round span and gates on the _endpoint_ (`open` ‚Äî below floor now). v31 reports a `share` against the front's _stated_ span and gates on the _aggregate_ (`majority` ‚Äî below floor more often than not). They are different reductions of the same below-floor data, headlined and gated differently: a front below floor only at the close is `open` to v21 but a minority to v31; a front below floor for rounds 1‚Äì4 of 5 that recovers at round 5 is `resolved` to v21 but a majority to v31. A consumer wanting "is it open now?" runs v21; one wanting "how much of the deal was it below floor?" runs v31; one wanting both runs both and joins on the shared `floors[]` matrix (`total_below_rounds` agrees with v21's `rounds_below` sum).
- **Not the latency command.** v28 reads the per-_episode_ below-duration ‚Äî the rounds a single fall sat below floor before the recovery that closes it, headlined by the slowest recovery. v31 reads the per-_front_ occupancy ‚Äî the total share of a front's stated span below floor, regardless of how many episodes it spans or how each was distributed. Two fronts with the same `max_latency` (v28 ‚Äî same longest single episode) can have very different `share` (one episode vs three of that length), and a front below floor from round 1 (never _fell_ in-sequence, `steady` to v28) has a real, often heavy, occupancy here. A consumer wanting the per-episode gap runs v28; one wanting the whole-front share runs v31.
- **Not the recurrence or volatility command.** v23 counts a front's below-floor _episodes_; v24 counts its floor _crossings_. Both are counts of transitions; neither is an _occupancy_. A front with one long episode (high tenure) and a front with one brief episode (low tenure) have the **same** episode count (v23: 1) and the same crossing count (v24: 2) ‚Äî v31 separates them by share. A consumer wanting the episode/crossing count runs v23/v24; one wanting the share runs v31.
- **Not silence-dilutes-the-share.** A round no document states does **not** count toward a front's denominator _or_ its numerator: per ¬ß3 (the v20/v21 contract), silence is neither exposure nor a clean round. The `share` is over the rounds that _stated_ the front, so three rounds of silence do not shrink a 100% tenure to 40%. (This is the denominator correction v21's `n`-based render lacks.)
- **Not a configurable "below for more than X% of rounds" gate.** v31 does not take a `--max-share P` knob; the gate fires on a **strict majority** (`below_rounds √ó 2 > stated_rounds`), the one occupancy condition whose meaning ("below floor more often than not") needs no tuning. A team wanting a wider or tighter bar reads `share` and `max_share` from the JSON. (v20‚Äìv30 made the same call against a configurable floor/duration/breadth/episode/crossing count/simultaneity/quiet tail/lead-in/latency/concerted-count/interval.)
- **Not a minimum-stated-rounds gate.** A front stated in exactly one round and below floor in it reads `share` 1.0 / `majority` ‚Äî it was an unaccepted position every (one) time it was discussed. v31 does not discount thin evidence; a consumer wanting to require a minimum stated count reads `stated_rounds` from the JSON.
- **Not a tenure artifact.** v31 introduces no new on-disk format; the tenure is _derived_, recomputed on demand from the N auditable coherence inputs ‚Äî the same "keep the derived thing derived" discipline v14‚Äìv30 hold.
- **Not a sequence the command discovers.** It does not glob a directory or sort filenames; the caller passes the artifacts **in round order** on the argv, exactly as the fourteen trend/exposure/persistence/‚Ä¶ commands do.
- **Not a browser surface.** An N-round tenure of uploaded artifacts is a CI/dashboard concern, which is what this command serves (mirroring v16‚Äìv30).

## ¬ß3. The posture filter (unchanged)

1. **Deterministic** ‚Äî `computeCoherenceTenure` over N `PostureCoherence` objects is pure: it matches fronts by dimension, pins fronts by `localeCompare(_, "en")` (the same order the trajectory/exposure/persistence/latency functions use), counts each front's stated and below-floor rounds, and picks the heaviest share by **integer cross-multiplication** (`below √ó stated` of the two fronts), never a float comparison. Identical artifacts in identical order ‚Üí identical `tenure_hash` on any machine.
2. **Honest about unstated data** ‚Äî a front no document states in a round counts toward neither its denominator nor its numerator. A front stated in no round is `unstated` (`share` `null`), never ranked. Silence is not exposure and not a clean round.
3. **Advisory** ‚Äî the command names what share of each front's stated span sat below the team's own floor. It asserts no legal conclusion.
4. **No server** ‚Äî N local files in, one report out. No socket, no engine run.
5. **Additive** ‚Äî a brand-new subcommand and one new pure module. Every existing command (`analyze`, `diff`, `compare`, `compare-coherence`, `coherence-trend`, `coherence-shift-trend`, `coherence-arc`, `coherence-exposure`, `coherence-persistence`, `coherence-breadth`, `coherence-recurrence`, `coherence-volatility`, `coherence-synchrony`, `coherence-settling`, `coherence-onset`, `coherence-latency`, `coherence-concurrency`, `coherence-relapse`, `verify`) is byte-for-byte unchanged in _output and goldens_; `coherence-sequence.ts` is imported and reused **without modification** (v31 needs nothing exported that was not already public ‚Äî like v19‚Äìv30, it touches no existing source file's behavior at all).

# Part I ‚Äî The tenure (pure)

`src/report/coherence-tenure.ts` (a new sibling to `coherence-persistence.ts`):

- **`TenureClass`** ‚Äî a front's below-floor occupancy class: `majority` (below floor for a strict majority of its stated rounds ‚Äî `below_rounds √ó 2 > stated_rounds`), `minority` (stated and below floor in ‚â• 1 round but not a strict majority, including an exact split), `none` (stated, never below floor), `unstated` (no document ever stated the front, ¬ß3).
- **`CoherenceTenureFront`** ‚Äî one negotiation front's whole-sequence occupancy: the `dimension`, the `floors[]` path (for context), the `stated_rounds` (the ¬ß3-honest denominator), the `below_rounds` (the numerator), the `share` (`below_rounds / stated_rounds`, `null` when unstated), and its `tenure` class.
- **`CoherenceTenure`** ‚Äî the whole-sequence reduction: `rounds` (count), `fronts[]` (the fronts above, `localeCompare`-pinned), `class_counts` (the per-front tally by class), `total_below_rounds` (every front's below-floor rounds summed ‚Äî equal to v21's `rounds_below` sum), `total_stated_rounds` (every front's stated rounds summed ‚Äî the grand denominator), `max_share` (the heaviest occupancy across the deal, `null` when no front was ever below floor), `heaviest_dimension` (the front owning `max_share`, earliest on a tie, `null` when none below), `majority` (any front below floor for a strict majority ‚Äî the gate verdict), and `tenure_hash`.
- **`computeCoherenceTenure(rounds: PostureCoherence[])`** ‚Äî the pure, IO-free core. For each front, counts the rounds that stated it and the rounds it sat below floor (an unstated round counts toward neither, ¬ß3), computes the share, tests the strict majority, and selects the heaviest share by integer cross-multiplication, returning the reduction with a `tenure_hash`. Requires ‚â• 2 rounds.
- **`exposureMajorityBelow(tenure)`** ‚Äî the CI gate predicate: `tenure.majority`. The occupancy verdict no other command exposes (v21 gates on the endpoint standing; v24 on a crossing count; this gates on a front below floor for a strict majority of its stated rounds ‚Äî distinct from v21's `exposureOpen`, which fires on a front below floor _now_).
- **`buildCoherenceTenureJson(tenure)`** / **`renderCoherenceTenureSummary(tenure)`** ‚Äî the JSON (`schema: vaulytica.posture-tenure.v1`) and human-readable renderers. The summary prints the heaviest-occupancy verdict (the front and its share, or none-below when nothing was ever below floor, plus the class tally), then one line per below-floor front (`majority` fronts first), then the hash.

v31 imports only the already-public `PostureCoherence` type, the `NegotiationTier` type, and the shared hashing helpers; the `below-acceptable` literal is a plain string; no existing source file changes.

# Part II ‚Äî The command (headless)

`tools/cli/coherence-tenure.ts` (a new sibling to `coherence-persistence.ts`):

- **`computeCoherenceTenureArtifacts(texts, format?)`** ‚Äî the pure CLI core. Verifies all N artifacts and runs the cross-ladder guard via the shared `verifyCoherenceSequence` loader (unchanged from v18‚Äìv30); on any malformed/tampered round returns `{ ok: false, errors }` (each prefixed `round N:`); on a cross-ladder pair returns `{ ok: false }` naming the two rounds; an unpinned artifact yields `{ ok: true }` with a `ladderNote`. Then `computeCoherenceTenure(rounds)` and renders (markdown summary or JSON), returning `{ ok: true, output, majority, ladderNote }`.
- **`runCoherenceTenure(argv)`** ‚Äî the CLI handler: reads the N files, calls the core, writes the `ladderNote` (if any) to stderr, prints the tenure to stdout, and ‚Äî under `--fail-on-majority-below` ‚Äî exits 2 when any front was below floor for a strict majority of its stated rounds. A malformed/tampered/cross-ladder input is a hard exit-1 error. Requires ‚â• 2 positionals.

`tools/cli/run.ts` (the dispatcher) gains a `coherence-tenure` case and a `USAGE` entry.

```
# Each round, archive its coherence (kilobytes, no clause text):
vaulytica analyze 'round1/*.docx' --playbook-file team.playbook.json --posture --emit-coherence round1.coherence.json
vaulytica analyze 'round2/*.docx' --playbook-file team.playbook.json --posture --emit-coherence round2.coherence.json
vaulytica analyze 'round3/*.docx' --playbook-file team.playbook.json --posture --emit-coherence round3.coherence.json

# Later ‚Äî read/gate what share of each front's stated span sat below floor, from the archive alone:
vaulytica coherence-tenure round1.coherence.json round2.coherence.json round3.coherence.json \
  --fail-on-majority-below
```
