# Vaulytica v32 — Document-Free Exposure Co-Fall Affinity (Pairwise Concession Coupling)

> **Status:** **Shipped (9.29.0).** Every posture reduction shipped to date — v20 through v31 — is a **scalar** read of the below-floor archive: per **front** (a level, a count, a share, a latency), per **round** (a breadth, a synchrony, a concurrency), or per **episode** (a recovery latency, a relapse interval). v25 and v29 come closest to a _relational_ read — they count, per step, _how many_ fronts crossed (v25) or fell (v29) together — but they collapse the relation to a count the instant they record it: v29 says "round 3 saw two fronts fall at once," never "and it was **Cap and Term**, the same two that fell together in round 5." Across the whole sequence, **which two fronts does the counterparty keep conceding as a block?** is a question no command can answer, because every command throws the pair identity away. Two deals with byte-identical v29 concurrency profiles (same per-step fall counts) can have opposite coupling structure: one where a single stable pair falls together every time (a linked concession — the other side trades Cap _for_ Term), another where the co-falling pair is different every round (no coupling at all, the same counts). That is a missing **axis** — the first **pairwise** one: not a per-front, per-round, or per-episode scalar, but a per-**pair** relation. v32 supplies it: for each unordered pair of fronts, across the whole sequence, how many round-transitions did **both** fall below floor in the **same step** (`co_falls`), out of the steps in which **either** fell (`union_falls`), the resulting affinity (`co_falls / union_falls` — the Jaccard overlap of the two fronts' fall-step sets), the deal's **tightest pairing** (`max_affinity` / `tightest_pair`), and — the one tuning-free verdict — whether any pair fell together for a strict **majority** of the steps either of them fell (`coupled`). It continues the global step numbering after v31's Step 211, beginning at **Step 212**. One new subcommand, one pure module, **zero** change to any existing source file.
> **Scope:** one idea — the _pairwise_ reduction the whole posture family never took. v25 answers _how many fronts crossed the floor together each step_; v29 answers _how many fell vs recovered together each step_; both are per-step **counts**. v32 answers _which two fronts fall together, and how reliably, across the whole deal_ — a per-pair **relation**, aggregated over the sequence. It reuses v29's exact fall event (at-or-above → below, silence-skipping per §3) — so a co-fall is two fronts both registering a v29 fall in the same transition — but where v29 keeps a per-step count, v32 keeps the per-pair overlap. By construction `total_co_falls` equals `Σ_t C(falling_t, 2)` (the sum over v29's per-step fall counts of "two-from-k"), and `total_falls` equals v29's `total_falls` (and v24's per-front crossing-fall sum). The use case is the same archive of per-round coherence artifacts: v29 says "round 3 was a concerted fall — two fronts fell at once"; v32 says "and across the deal, **Cap and Term** fell together in 3 of the 4 rounds either fell — the counterparty concedes them as a block, while the lone concerted fall in round 7 (Fee + Risk, who never fell together again) was a coincidence."
> **Posture (unchanged, non-negotiable):** deterministic (the affinity is a pure per-pair overlap count over the same `below-acceptable` rung v10 defines, taken over the binding floors v12 already derived and v24/v25/v29 already read; the tightest-pair pick is decided by integer cross-multiplication, never a float compare, so the same artifacts → identical `affinity_hash`, on any machine, forever), no AI / no probabilistic path, no server (N local files in, one report out; no socket), citable (the artifacts carry the same per-front binding floors v12 derived from each document's own clause and the team's own playbook — v32 adds no new claim), lints / references / positions — but never drafts, and never renders a legal conclusion. The command is **advisory and verifiable**: every artifact is hash-verified (a tampered round is a hard error, side-labeled by round index), and the ladders are checked for a match across the _whole_ sequence (any cross-ladder pair is refused) via the same `verifyCoherenceSequence` loader the fifteen trend/exposure/persistence/breadth/recurrence/volatility/synchrony/settling/onset/latency/concurrency/relapse/tenure commands use, unchanged. Every step passes the §3 filter or it does not ship.

# Part 0 — Intent

## §1. Why we're doing this

v29 gave the posture archive a direction-resolved per-step count: for each round-transition, how many fronts _fell_ below floor together and how many _recovered_ (the deal's coordinated collapse, `falling ≥ 2`, a _concerted fall_). The gate (`--fail-on-concerted-fall`) fires on the **endpoint of a single step** — was there a round the package lurched, two-plus fronts down at once? That is the right shape for "did the deal ever take a coordinated hit?" But it records the event and discards the **relation**: v29 knows round 3 saw two fronts fall, and it even lists _which_ two in `falling_dimensions` — but it never asks the next question across the sequence: **is it the same two fronts, again and again?**

No command answers it, because every command to date reduces to a scalar. Two deals with **identical** v29 concurrency profiles — the same `falling` count at every transition, the same `concerted_fall_count` — can be opposites on this axis:

- **The stable coupling.** Cap and Term fall together in rounds 3, 5, and 6 — every time either of them fell, the other fell with it. The counterparty concedes them as a **block**: trade movement on Cap and Term moves too. A linked concession.
- **The coincidence.** Round 3 it was Cap and Term, round 5 it was Cap and Fee, round 6 it was Term and Risk — three concerted falls, three _different_ pairs, no pair repeating. The same `concerted_fall_count` (3), the same per-step counts, but **no coupling**: each pair fell together once, by chance, and never again.

To v29 these are indistinguishable (same counts). The difference is **pairwise**, and it is the first axis the posture family has that is _not_ a per-front, per-round, or per-episode scalar: it is a per-**pair** relation, aggregated across the whole deal. It matters because a stable concession pairing is _strategic information_ — it tells a deal lead which fronts the counterparty treats as a bundle, the linkage to exploit (or defend) in the next round; a one-off coincidental co-fall tells them nothing.

This is not a bug in v29 — a _per-step concurrency_ reduction is supposed to count the step. It is a missing **axis**: the _pairwise_ dimension of the same fall data. v32 supplies it. Read the same artifacts per-pair: for each unordered pair of fronts, how many transitions saw _both_ fall (the numerator), how many saw _either_ fall (the denominator), the resulting affinity, the deal's tightest such pairing, and whether any pair fell together for a strict majority of the steps either fell. The gate follows: `--fail-on-coupled-fronts` fires when a pair fell together _more often than apart_ — the one coupling boundary whose meaning ("they fall together more often than separately") needs no tuning. All of this is a reduction over the same `floors[]` crossings v24 counts, v25 buckets per step, and v29 splits by direction.

## §2. What v32 is and is not

**It is:**

- A **document-free affinity command.** `coherence-affinity <r1.coherence.json> … <rN.coherence.json>` reads N ≥ 2 saved coherence artifacts in round order, verifies each, runs the cross-ladder guard across all of them, and prints, per **pair** of fronts that ever fell together, how many transitions saw both fall (`co_falls`), each front's own fall count (`a_falls` / `b_falls`), the count of transitions where either fell (`union_falls = a_falls + b_falls − co_falls`), the resulting `affinity` (`co_falls / union_falls`), and its affinity `class` — plus the deal's **tightest pairing** (`max_affinity` / `tightest_pair`, `null` when no two fronts ever fell together), the `total_co_falls` (`= Σ_t C(falling_t, 2)`, v29's per-step fall counts choose-two'd) and `total_falls` (`=` v29's `total_falls`). Markdown (default) or `--format json`. No documents are read; the engine is never run.
- A **single deal-level coupling gate.** `--fail-on-coupled-fronts` exits 2 when at least one pair of fronts fell together for a strict **majority** of the transitions in which either fell (`co_falls × 2 > union_falls`). This is the _pairwise_ counterpart to v29's _per-step_ gate: where v29 fires on a single step that saw ≥2 fronts fall (a one-off lurch trips it), this fires on a _stable relation_ — two fronts that fell together more often than they fell apart, across the deal, **even if no single step ever saw three down at once**. A pair that fell together once but apart twice is `incidental` and clears it; a consumer wanting a different bar reads `affinity` and `max_affinity` from the JSON. It needs no tuning — a strict majority of the union _is_ "they fall together more often than not."
- A **whole-sequence-verified input, sharing the v17–v31 front end.** Every artifact's `coherence_hash` is re-derived and checked on load (a tampered/corrupt round is a hard error, errors prefixed `round N:`). The v15/v16 cross-ladder guard runs across the sequence via the same `verifyCoherenceSequence` loader the fifteen trend/exposure/persistence/breadth/recurrence/volatility/synchrony/settling/onset/latency/concurrency/relapse/tenure commands use, with no change to it.
- A **derived report carrying its own fingerprint.** The affinity carries an `affinity_hash` (a stable SHA-256 over the canonical per-pair set — the integer `a_falls`/`b_falls`/`co_falls` and the class; the derived float `affinity` and the derived integer `union_falls` are omitted, so the fingerprint is integer-exact over the inputs), namespaced apart from every `coherence_hash`, `movement_hash`, `trajectory_hash`, `shift_trajectory_hash`, `arc_hash`, `exposure_hash`, `persistence_hash`, `breadth_hash`, `recurrence_hash`, `volatility_hash`, `synchrony_hash`, `settling_hash`, `onset_hash`, `latency_hash`, `concurrency_hash`, `relapse_hash`, and `tenure_hash`, so the same N artifacts in the same order always reproduce the same report.

**It is not:**

- **Not a new diff, classifier, predicate, or artifact format.** It reuses the `weakest_tier` binding floor v12 derives, the `below-acceptable` rung v10 defines, and the _fall_ event v29 defines (at-or-above → below, silence-skipping); v32 is a subcommand plus one pure module (`computeCoherenceAffinity`) whose only new logic is a per-pair overlap of the fall-step sets and a strict-majority test. `parsePostureCoherenceJson`, the coherence schema, and every trajectory/exposure/…/tenure function are unchanged.
- **Not the concurrency command.** v29 counts, per step, how many fronts fell — a per-step scalar, gated on a single step with ≥2 fronts down (`concerted`). v32 reads, per pair, how reliably two fronts fall _together_ across the deal — a per-pair relation, gated on a pair that fell together more often than apart (`coupled`). Every co-fall step _is_ a v29 concerted-fall step (two fronts down at once), so `coupled ⟹ concerted` for that step; but the reverse fails the way that matters: three different pairs each falling together once trips v29 (three concerted-fall steps) yet leaves every pair `incidental` to v32 (each co-fell once, apart more). A consumer wanting "did any step lurch?" runs v29; one wanting "is there a stable concession pairing?" runs v32; one wanting both runs both and joins on the shared `floors[]` matrix (`total_falls` agrees with v29's by construction).
- **Not the synchrony command.** v25 counts, per step, how many fronts _crossed_ the floor together — direction-blind (a fall and a recovery both count). v32 reads only _falls_ (the v29 direction), and only the _pairwise_ overlap. A step where one front fell and one recovered is a synchrony of two to v25, contributes a fall to one front and a recovery to another, and is **not** a co-fall to v32 (only one front fell).
- **Not the volatility command.** v24 counts a front's floor _crossings_ (falls and recoveries). v32 counts only _falls_, and only their pairwise coincidence. Two fronts each with the same v24 crossing count can have any affinity from 0 (their falls never align) to 1 (every fall aligns).
- **Not a per-front reduction.** v20/v21/v23/v24/v28/v31 all reduce to a per-front scalar. v32 is the first per-**pair** reduction: its primary object is an unordered pair, not a front. The per-front fall counts (`a_falls`, `b_falls`) appear only as the context for the pair's overlap; the deal headline is a _pair_, not a front.
- **Not co-occupancy (both below floor in the same round, however they got there).** v32's co-fall is a coincidence of _fall events_ (the v29 transition), not of standing: two fronts that were both already below floor through rounds 2–4 without _crossing_ in the same step do not co-fall (no shared fall transition). This keeps the lineage to v29's fall and the join invariant to `Σ C(falling_t, 2)` exact. A consumer wanting co-_occupancy_ (both below floor in the same round) reads each front's `floors[]` and intersects the below-floor rounds — a different relation, deferred (Part XVI).
- **Not a configurable "co-fell at least K times" gate.** v32 does not take a `--min-co-falls K` knob; the gate fires on a strict majority of the union (`co_falls × 2 > union_falls`), the one coupling condition whose meaning ("together more often than apart") needs no tuning. A team wanting a wider or tighter bar reads `affinity` and `max_affinity` from the JSON. (v20–v31 made the same call against a configurable floor/duration/breadth/episode/crossing count/simultaneity/quiet tail/lead-in/latency/concerted-count/interval/share.)
- **Not a minimum-union gate.** A pair where both fronts fell exactly once, in the same step, reads `co_falls` 1 / `union_falls` 1 / `affinity` 1.0 / `coupled` — every fall either took was a joint fall. v32 does not discount thin evidence; a consumer wanting to require a minimum union reads `union_falls` from the JSON. (Mirrors v31's "stated once and below → 100%/majority" call.)
- **Not an affinity artifact.** v32 introduces no new on-disk format; the affinity is _derived_, recomputed on demand from the N auditable coherence inputs — the same "keep the derived thing derived" discipline v14–v31 hold.
- **Not a sequence the command discovers.** It does not glob a directory or sort filenames; the caller passes the artifacts **in round order** on the argv, exactly as the fifteen trend/exposure/persistence/… commands do.
- **Not a browser surface.** An N-round pairwise affinity of uploaded artifacts is a CI/dashboard concern, which is what this command serves (mirroring v16–v31).

## §3. The posture filter (unchanged)

1. **Deterministic** — `computeCoherenceAffinity` over N `PostureCoherence` objects is pure: it matches fronts by dimension, pins fronts by `localeCompare(_, "en")` (the same order the trajectory/exposure/concurrency functions use), enumerates unordered pairs in that pinned order (`dimension_a < dimension_b`), counts each pair's co-falls and union by intersecting the fronts' fall-step sets, and picks the tightest affinity by **integer cross-multiplication** (`co × union` of the two pairs), never a float comparison. Identical artifacts in identical order → identical `affinity_hash` on any machine.
2. **Honest about unstated data** — a front no document states in a round does not _fall_ into or out of that round: silence is neither a fall nor a recovery (the same §3 contract that keeps `below → unstated → below` zero crossings in v24 and zero falls in v29). A fall revealed after a silent gap is attributed to the transition into the round that reveals the new standing — never an invented fall on the silent step. A pair that never both-fell is omitted from `pairs[]` (no affinity edge), never ranked.
3. **Advisory** — the command names which fronts fell below the team's own floor together, and how reliably. It asserts no legal conclusion.
4. **No server** — N local files in, one report out. No socket, no engine run.
5. **Additive** — a brand-new subcommand and one new pure module. Every existing command (`analyze`, `diff`, `compare`, `compare-coherence`, `coherence-trend`, `coherence-shift-trend`, `coherence-arc`, `coherence-exposure`, `coherence-persistence`, `coherence-breadth`, `coherence-recurrence`, `coherence-volatility`, `coherence-synchrony`, `coherence-settling`, `coherence-onset`, `coherence-latency`, `coherence-concurrency`, `coherence-relapse`, `coherence-tenure`, `verify`) is byte-for-byte unchanged in _output and goldens_; `coherence-sequence.ts` is imported and reused **without modification** (v32 needs nothing exported that was not already public — like v19–v31, it touches no existing source file's behavior at all).

# Part I — The affinity (pure)

`src/report/coherence-affinity.ts` (a new sibling to `coherence-concurrency.ts`):

- **`AffinityClass`** — a pair's co-fall coupling class: `coupled` (the two fronts fell together for a strict majority of the steps either fell — `co_falls × 2 > union_falls`), `incidental` (they fell together in ≥ 1 step but not a strict majority of the union, including an exact split). Only pairs with `co_falls ≥ 1` are classified; a pair that never both-fell has no affinity edge and is omitted.
- **`CoherenceAffinityPair`** — one unordered pair's whole-sequence co-fall relation: the `dimension_a`/`dimension_b` (pinned, `a < b`), the `a_falls`/`b_falls` (each front's total falls), the `co_falls` (transitions both fell), the `union_falls` (transitions either fell, `a_falls + b_falls − co_falls`), the `affinity` (`co_falls / union_falls`), and its `class`.
- **`CoherenceAffinity`** — the whole-sequence reduction: `rounds` (count), `pairs[]` (the co-falling pairs above, pinned by `(dimension_a, dimension_b)`), `class_counts` (the per-pair tally by class), `total_co_falls` (every pair's co-falls summed — equal to `Σ_t C(falling_t, 2)`), `total_falls` (every front's falls summed — equal to v29's `total_falls`), `max_affinity` (the tightest coupling across the deal, `null` when no two fronts ever fell together), `tightest_pair` (the `[dimension_a, dimension_b]` owning `max_affinity`, earliest on a tie, `null` when none), `coupled` (any pair a strict-majority coupling — the gate verdict), and `affinity_hash`.
- **`computeCoherenceAffinity(rounds: PostureCoherence[])`** — the pure, IO-free core. For each front, derives its fall-step set (the v29 fall event, silence-skipping, §3); for each unordered pair, intersects the two sets for `co_falls`, computes the union, the affinity, and the strict-majority class; selects the tightest affinity by integer cross-multiplication, returning the reduction with an `affinity_hash`. Requires ≥ 2 rounds.
- **`exposureCoupled(affinity)`** — the CI gate predicate: `affinity.coupled`. The pairwise verdict no other command exposes (v29 gates on a single per-step fall count; this gates on a pair that fell together for a strict majority of the steps either fell — distinct from v29's `exposureConcerted`, which fires on one step's ≥2-front lurch).
- **`buildCoherenceAffinityJson(affinity)`** / **`renderCoherenceAffinitySummary(affinity)`** — the JSON (`schema: vaulytica.posture-affinity.v1`) and human-readable renderers. The summary prints the tightest-pairing verdict (the pair and its affinity, or none-paired when no two fronts ever fell together, plus the class tally), then one line per co-falling pair (`coupled` pairs first), then the hash.

v32 imports only the already-public `PostureCoherence` type, the `NegotiationTier` type, and the shared hashing helpers; the `below-acceptable` literal is a plain string; no existing source file changes.

# Part II — The command (headless)

`tools/cli/coherence-affinity.ts` (a new sibling to `coherence-concurrency.ts`):

- **`computeCoherenceAffinityArtifacts(texts, format?)`** — the pure CLI core. Verifies all N artifacts and runs the cross-ladder guard via the shared `verifyCoherenceSequence` loader (unchanged from v18–v31); on any malformed/tampered round returns `{ ok: false, errors }` (each prefixed `round N:`); on a cross-ladder pair returns `{ ok: false }` naming the two rounds; an unpinned artifact yields `{ ok: true }` with a `ladderNote`. Then `computeCoherenceAffinity(rounds)` and renders (markdown summary or JSON), returning `{ ok: true, output, coupled, ladderNote }`.
- **`runCoherenceAffinity(argv)`** — the CLI handler: reads the N files, calls the core, writes the `ladderNote` (if any) to stderr, prints the affinity to stdout, and — under `--fail-on-coupled-fronts` — exits 2 when any pair fell together for a strict majority of the steps either fell. A malformed/tampered/cross-ladder input is a hard exit-1 error. Requires ≥ 2 positionals.

`tools/cli/run.ts` (the dispatcher) gains a `coherence-affinity` case and a `USAGE` entry.

```
# Each round, archive its coherence (kilobytes, no clause text):
vaulytica analyze 'round1/*.docx' --playbook-file team.playbook.json --posture --emit-coherence round1.coherence.json
vaulytica analyze 'round2/*.docx' --playbook-file team.playbook.json --posture --emit-coherence round2.coherence.json
vaulytica analyze 'round3/*.docx' --playbook-file team.playbook.json --posture --emit-coherence round3.coherence.json

# Later — read/gate which two fronts the counterparty concedes as a block, from the archive alone:
vaulytica coherence-affinity round1.coherence.json round2.coherence.json round3.coherence.json \
  --fail-on-coupled-fronts
```

# Part XVI — Principled deferrals

- **A `coherence-concurrency --pairs` flag.** ⬜ Not built — and deliberately not, mirroring v18–v31 Part XVI. v32 is a separate command, not a flag on the concurrency command, so each command keeps exactly one gate and one hash.
- **A configurable "co-fell at least K times" gate (`--min-co-falls K`).** ⬜ Not built. The gate fires on a strict majority of the union (`co_falls × 2 > union_falls`), the one coupling condition whose meaning needs no tuning. `affinity` and `max_affinity` are in the JSON for a consumer that wants a different bar.
- **A minimum-union floor for the gate (`--min-union N`).** ⬜ Not built. A pair that both-fell once reads 100% / coupled — honestly, every fall either took was joint. `union_falls` is in the JSON for a consumer wanting to discount thin evidence.
- **A co-_recovery_ affinity (which fronts _recover_ together).** ⬜ Not built — this is the exact mirror of v32 on the recovery direction (as v30 mirrors v28, v27 mirrors v26). The recovery event is already in v29's `recovering_dimensions`; a per-pair recovery overlap is the same reduction on the other direction, with no consumer yet. Add it the way v30 mirrored v28 if a dashboard pins the co-fall view first.
- **A co-_occupancy_ affinity (both below floor in the same round, however they arrived).** ⬜ Not built. v32's co-fall is a coincidence of fall _events_ (the v29 transition), which keeps the join invariant to `Σ C(falling_t, 2)` exact. Co-occupancy (both below floor in the same round, no shared fall required) is a different relation over each front's `floors[]`; a consumer wanting it intersects the below-floor rounds directly.
- **A standalone affinity artifact (`--emit-affinity`).** ⬜ Deferred, for the same reason v14–v31 keep the derived thing derived: the affinity is cheaply recomputable from the N coherence artifacts on demand.
- **A full pairwise affinity matrix (every pair, including zero-overlap pairs).** ⬜ Not built. `pairs[]` lists only pairs with `co_falls ≥ 1` (the affinity edges); a zero-overlap pair has affinity 0 and carries no coupling signal, and listing all `C(F, 2)` pairs is O(F²) noise. A consumer wanting the dense matrix has every front's `floors[]` to compute it.
- **A directory/glob walker that infers round order.** ⬜ Not built. The command takes artifacts in round order on the argv (the caller's contract, mirroring the fifteen trend/exposure/persistence/… commands).
- **A browser surface for an N-round affinity.** ⬜ Deferred (v16–v31 Part XVI). The browser does an in-session two-round comparison; an N-artifact pairwise affinity is a CI/dashboard concern.

# Part XVII — Open questions for the maintainer

1. **Report the second- and third-tightest pairings, not just the tightest?** Today `max_affinity` / `tightest_pair` headline the single tightest coupling; the full ranked list is in `pairs[]` (sorted by pair label, not affinity). A dashboard wanting "the top-K couplings" sorts `pairs[]` by `affinity` itself. Recommendation: **keep the headline a single pair** — the list is in the JSON; add a ranked-by-affinity view only if a dashboard asks to pin it (the pair order stays label-pinned for a stable hash).
2. **Gate on a count of coupled pairs, not just the verdict?** Today `coupled` is a boolean and `class_counts.coupled` is the count; the gate fires on `coupled`. A consumer wanting "≥ K coupled pairs" reads `class_counts.coupled`.
