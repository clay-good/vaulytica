# Vaulytica v41 ‚Äî Document-Free Exposure Recovery Durability (How Long a Fix *Typically* Holds, Not Its Single Fastest Collapse ‚Äî the Above-Floor Mirror of v40's Below-Floor Mean)

> **Status:** **Shipped (9.38.0).** v30 gave the posture archive its **relapse-interval** axis: it pairs each *recovery* above the acceptable floor with the *fall* that undoes it, then reads two reductions ‚Äî the deal's **quickest** single relapse (`min_interval`, an extreme) and whether any recovery was undone the very next round (`immediate`, the gate). Both throw away the read a deal lead asks reviewing a close that bounced: not the single fastest relapse, but the **typical** one ‚Äî *when this front recovers, how many clean rounds does the fix usually hold before it falls again?* A front that holds five rounds twice and once bounces back the next round has the deal's quickest single relapse, yet its fixes almost always last; a front that holds one round every single time has the same quickest relapse, yet it is the one whose every fix is fragile. The `min_interval` extreme cannot tell them apart; the **mean** can ‚Äî and the mean is the one that names the front whose recoveries chronically fail to hold. v41 is the **central-tendency magnitude of v30's intervals**, the *above-floor mirror* of v40's below-floor mean: per front, the mean rounds its binding floor held above the acceptable floor across its *relapsed* recoveries (`mean_durability`), gated on the one tuning-free condition ‚Äî a front whose relapsed recoveries average **fewer than two clean rounds** (`--fail-on-fragile-recovery`): its fix typically does not survive even one clean round. It continues the global step numbering after v40's Step 220, beginning at **Step 221**. One new subcommand, one pure module, **zero** change to any existing source file.
> **Scope:** one idea ‚Äî the *typical length* of the clean interval between a recovery and the relapse that ends it, where v30 reads its *extreme*. It is not a new crossing, ordering, rate, or pairwise read; it reuses `computeCoherenceRelapse` **unchanged** (the join pattern v38 used for v36+v37 and v40 used for v28) and averages the per-front relapsed interval lengths v30 already computes. The use case is the chronically fragile front: v30 says "the quickest single relapse was Cap, undone the very next round"; v41 says "but **Term** averages one clean round on *every* fix ‚Äî Cap's one fast relapse was one bad fix among durable ones." Where v30 names the *fastest collapse* and the *immediate* relapse, v41 names the *typical case* ‚Äî orthogonal to one and strictly stronger evidence than the other.
> **Posture (unchanged, non-negotiable):** deterministic (the durability verdict is a pure reduction over the intervals v30 derives ‚Äî the lowest-mean pick decided by *integer cross-multiplication* (`total_rounds √ó closed_intervals`), the gate an integer comparison (`total_rounds < 2 √ó closed_intervals`); no float threshold enters the verdict, so the same artifacts ‚Üí identical `durability_hash`, on any machine, forever), no AI / no probabilistic path, no server (N local files in, one report out; no socket), citable (the artifacts carry the same per-front binding floors v12 derived from each document's own clause and the team's own playbook ‚Äî v41 adds no new claim), lints / references / positions ‚Äî but never drafts, and never renders a legal conclusion. The command is **advisory and verifiable**: every artifact is hash-verified (a tampered round is a hard error, side-labeled by round index), and the ladders are checked for a match across the _whole_ sequence (any cross-ladder pair is refused) via the same `verifyCoherenceSequence` loader the twenty-four trend/exposure/‚Ä¶/duration commands use, unchanged. Every step passes the ¬ß3 filter or it does not ship.

# Part 0 ‚Äî Intent

## ¬ß1. Why we're doing this

v40's Open Question #1 named the next axis explicitly: with the per-front *below-floor* magnitude shipped (v40's mean recovered-exposure duration), the remaining genuinely-new magnitude is the **above-floor** mirror ‚Äî the mean *clean* rounds between a recovery and its next fall. v40 deferred it in Part XVI by name ("a relapse-interval magnitude ‚Äî mean clean rounds between exposures ‚Äî the above-floor mirror of v40's below-floor mean"). v41 is that mirror read.

v30 already pairs each recovery with the fall that undoes it and measures every relapsed interval's clean length. It reduces those lengths two ways, and both are **extremes**:

- **`min_interval`** ‚Äî the single fastest relapse across the deal. It answers "what was the quickest collapse?" ‚Äî but one bad fix dominates it. A front that holds five rounds nine times and once bounces back the next round owns the deal's `min_interval` (1), yet its fixes hold 90% of the time.
- **`immediate`** ‚Äî whether any recovery was undone at the very next round. It answers "did anything bounce straight back?" ‚Äî the gate, but blind to the *length* of the relapsed intervals beyond the single shortest.

The missing read is the **mean**: the total clean rounds normalized by the number of relapsed intervals. That is the signal a deal lead reviewing a close that bounced actually wants:

- **The chronically fragile front.** Term recovers three times and relapses one clean round later *every* time ‚Äî a mean of 1. Its fixes never hold; every concession the counterparty grants is undone almost at once. Its `min_interval` (1) matches the front below, yet it is the one that *typically* relapses fast.
- **The one fast relapse (otherwise durable).** Cap recovers, holds five rounds, relapses, recovers, holds five rounds, then once bounces back the next round ‚Äî a `min_interval` of 1 (the deal's quickest), yet a mean of 11/3 ‚âà 3.7. One fix did not hold; the front's fixes usually last.

To the `min_interval` read alone this is invisible ‚Äî it sees only the fastest relapse and cannot tell the chronically fragile front (every interval short) from the front with one fast relapse (one short interval, the rest long). v41 supplies the mean and the one verdict whose meaning needs no tuning: per front, the `clean_intervals` (its relapsed interval lengths), `closed_intervals`, `open_intervals`, `total_rounds`, the `mean_durability` (`total_rounds / closed_intervals`), a `class` (`fragile` / `durable` / `held` / `steady` / `unstated`), and the deal's `most_fragile_dimension` / `min_mean` and `fragile` gate. All of it is a reduction over the same intervals v30 already pairs ‚Äî no new crossing, no new pairing, no new on-disk format.

## ¬ß2. What v41 is and is not

**It is:**

- A **document-free recovery-durability command.** `coherence-durability <r1.coherence.json> ‚Ä¶ <rN.coherence.json>` reads N ‚â• 2 saved coherence artifacts in round order, verifies each, runs the cross-ladder guard across all of them, and prints, per **front**, the relapsed-interval lengths (`clean_intervals`), the `mean_durability`, the quickest single relapse (`min_interval`, carried from v30 for contrast), the count of recoveries that held (`open_intervals`), and its `class` ‚Äî plus the deal's `most_fragile_dimension` (the lowest mean), `min_mean`, `total_relapsed_intervals` (= v30's `relapse_count`), `total_held_intervals` (= v30's `held_count`), `total_rounds`, and `fragile` (the gate verdict). Markdown (default) or `--format json`. No documents are read; the engine is never run.
- A **single deal-level per-front gate, on the typical length.** `--fail-on-fragile-recovery` exits 2 when at least one front's relapsed recoveries average fewer than two clean rounds (`total_rounds < 2 √ó closed_intervals`) ‚Äî a front that, when it recovers, typically does not survive even one clean round before relapsing. Two rounds is the first integer above the metric's structural minimum (a recovery and the immediately following fall is one clean round), so the bar inherits no knob.
- A **whole-sequence-verified input, sharing the v17‚Äìv40 front end.** Every artifact's `coherence_hash` is re-derived and checked on load (a tampered/corrupt round is a hard error, errors prefixed `round N:`). The v15/v16 cross-ladder guard runs across the sequence via the same `verifyCoherenceSequence` loader the twenty-four trend/exposure/‚Ä¶/duration commands use, with no change to it.
- A **derived report carrying its own fingerprint.** The report carries a `durability_hash` (a stable SHA-256 over the canonical per-front set ‚Äî the front, its `floors`, sorted `clean_intervals`, `open_intervals`, and `class`; the derived float `mean_durability`, `min_interval`, `min_mean`, and `fragile` are omitted, so the fingerprint is integer-exact over the inputs), namespaced apart from every prior hash (`coherence_hash`, ‚Ä¶, `duration_hash`), so the same N artifacts in the same order always reproduce the same report.

**It is not:**

- **Not a new diff, classifier, pairing, ordering, or artifact format.** It reuses the intervals `computeCoherenceRelapse` already pairs ‚Äî no new fall, recovery, crossing, or ordering math. v41 is a subcommand plus one pure module (`computeCoherenceDurability`) whose only new logic is averaging the relapsed interval lengths. `parsePostureCoherenceJson`, the coherence schema, `coherence-relapse.ts`, and every other function are unchanged.
- **Not the relapse command (v30).** v30 reads the **extreme** (`min_interval`, the single quickest relapse) and the **immediate** gate (`clean_rounds === 1`). v41 reads the **mean** (the typical clean interval). The gate is **strictly stronger evidence** than v30's: a fragile mean (`< 2`) forces at least one clean interval of one round (an all-integers mean below two cannot avoid a 1), so every `fragile` front also trips v30's `immediate` gate ‚Äî but the converse fails. A front with intervals `[1, 5]` (mean 3) trips v30's immediate gate on its single fast relapse yet is `durable` here; v41 fires only on the *chronically* fragile front, suppressing the single-fast-relapse alarm v30 raises.
- **Not the duration command (v40).** v40 reads the *below-floor* mean (how long a front sits *below* the floor across its recovered exposures). v41 reads the *above-floor* mean (how long a fix holds *above* the floor before relapsing). They are mirrors: a front that lingers long below floor (v40 `lingering`) can hold long above it once it recovers (v41 `durable`), and a front that recovers fast (v40 `brief`) can relapse fast (v41 `fragile`) ‚Äî the two means are independent, one over below-floor episodes, one over above-floor intervals.
- **Not a depth-weighted score.** The floor is binary (`below-acceptable` is the only sub-floor rung), so a *depth* weighting is not viable; v41 weights by *duration* (clean rounds above floor), the orthogonal magnitude the binary floor does admit.
- **Not a gate on the worst case.** v41 deliberately gates on the **mean**, not `min_interval`. A consumer wanting to flag a single fast relapse reads `min_interval` from the JSON (or runs v30's `--fail-on-immediate-relapse`); v41's gate fires only when the *typical* fix is fragile.
- **Not an averaging of held recoveries.** A recovery never undone is an *unbounded* clean interval with no finite length to average; it is counted (`open_intervals`) but excluded from the mean. v30 already treats it as the durable best case; v41 reads the *relapsed* recoveries' typical length, so the held recovery never enters the mean.
- **Not a durability artifact.** v41 introduces no new on-disk format; the report is _derived_, recomputed on demand from the N auditable coherence inputs.
- **Not a sequence the command discovers.** It takes the artifacts **in round order** on the argv, exactly as the twenty-four trend/exposure/‚Ä¶ commands do.
- **Not a browser surface.** An N-round per-front durability synthesis of uploaded artifacts is a CI/dashboard concern (mirroring v16‚Äìv40).

## ¬ß3. The posture filter (unchanged)

1. **Deterministic** ‚Äî `computeCoherenceDurability` over N `PostureCoherence` objects is pure: it delegates the recovery-to-relapse pairing to `computeCoherenceRelapse` (the `localeCompare`-pinned front order, the silence-skipping scan), splits each front's intervals into the relapsed (a finite clean length) and the held (excluded), sums the relapsed lengths, and picks the lowest mean by **integer cross-multiplication** (`total_rounds √ó closed_intervals`, earliest label on a tie), never a float compare. The gate is the integer test `total_rounds < 2 √ó closed_intervals`. Identical artifacts in identical order ‚Üí identical `durability_hash` on any machine.
2. **Honest about unstated data** ‚Äî a front no document states does not recover or fall (the ¬ß3 contract v30 already enforces): silence contributes no interval, so a front that never recovered in-sequence carries no mean (`steady`), and an unstated front carries none (`unstated`). A *held* recovery (one never undone) is an unbounded clean interval with no finite length to average, so it is counted (`open_intervals`) but never folded into the mean ‚Äî v30 already calls it the durable best case; v41 reads the *relapsed* recoveries' typical length.
3. **Advisory** ‚Äî the command names how long the team's own floor *typically* stays clear between a recovery and the fall that undoes it. It asserts no legal conclusion.
4. **No server** ‚Äî N local files in, one report out. No socket, no engine run.
5. **Additive** ‚Äî a brand-new subcommand and one new pure module. Every existing command (`analyze`, `diff`, `compare`, `compare-coherence`, `coherence-trend`, ‚Ä¶, `coherence-duration`, `verify`) is byte-for-byte unchanged in _output and goldens_; `coherence-relapse.ts` and `coherence-sequence.ts` are imported and reused **without modification** (v41 needs nothing exported that was not already public ‚Äî like v19‚Äìv40, it touches no existing source file's behavior at all).

# Part I ‚Äî The report (pure)

`src/report/coherence-durability.ts` (a new sibling to `coherence-duration.ts`):

- **`DurabilityClass`** ‚Äî a front's recovery-durability class: `fragile` (‚â• 1 relapsed recovery and `total_rounds < 2 √ó closed_intervals` ‚Äî a mean below two rounds, the gate-worthy class), `durable` (‚â• 1 relapsed recovery but a mean of at least two rounds), `held` (recovered in-sequence but every recovery held ‚Äî an unbounded interval, the durable best case), `steady` (stated, never recovered in-sequence), `unstated` (no document ever stated the front).
- **`CoherenceDurabilityFront`** ‚Äî one front's whole-sequence recovery durability: the `dimension`, the `floors` (binding floor at each round, `null` = unstated), the sorted `clean_intervals` (its relapsed interval lengths), `closed_intervals`, `open_intervals`, `total_rounds` (the sum of `clean_intervals`), `mean_durability` (`total_rounds / closed_intervals`, `null` when none relapsed), `min_interval` (the quickest single relapse ‚Äî v30's per-front extreme, carried for contrast), and its `class`.
- **`CoherenceDurability`** ‚Äî the whole-sequence reduction: `rounds` (count), `fronts[]` (pinned by `dimension`), `class_counts` (the per-front tally by class), `total_relapsed_intervals` (= v30's `relapse_count`), `total_held_intervals` (= v30's `held_count`), `total_rounds` (every relapsed interval's length summed), `min_mean` (the lowest mean, `null` when none relapsed), `most_fragile_dimension` (the front owning it, earliest label on a tie), `fragile` (`‚â• 1` fragile front ‚Äî the gate verdict), and `durability_hash`.
- **`computeCoherenceDurability(rounds: PostureCoherence[])`** ‚Äî the pure, IO-free core. Delegates the interval pairing to `computeCoherenceRelapse`, averages each front's relapsed interval lengths, classifies it, selects the lowest mean by integer cross-multiplication, and returns the reduction with a `durability_hash`. Requires ‚â• 2 rounds (v30 enforces this ‚Äî a relapse span is a between-round event).
- **`recoveryFragile(durability)`** ‚Äî the CI gate predicate: `durability.fragile`. The read no other command exposes (v30 gates on the single immediate relapse; v40 on the below-floor mean; neither averages the above-floor relapsed interval lengths).
- **`buildCoherenceDurabilityJson(durability)`** / **`renderCoherenceDurabilitySummary(durability)`** ‚Äî the JSON (`schema: vaulytica.posture-durability.v1`) and human-readable renderers. The summary prints the most fragile recovery (the front and its mean clean rounds, or none when nothing relapsed), the class tally, then one line per front that relapsed any recovery (`fragile` first), then the hash.

v41 imports `computeCoherenceRelapse` and the shared hashing helpers; no existing source file changes.

# Part II ‚Äî The command (headless)

`tools/cli/coherence-durability.ts` (a new sibling to `coherence-duration.ts`):

- **`computeCoherenceDurabilityArtifacts(texts, format?)`** ‚Äî the pure CLI core. Verifies all N artifacts and runs the cross-ladder guard via the shared `verifyCoherenceSequence` loader (unchanged from v18‚Äìv40); on any malformed/tampered round returns `{ ok: false, errors }` (each prefixed `round N:`); on a cross-ladder pair returns `{ ok: false }` naming the two rounds; an unpinned artifact yields `{ ok: true }` with a `ladderNote`. Then `computeCoherenceDurability(rounds)` and renders (markdown summary or JSON), returning `{ ok: true, output, fragile, ladderNote }`.
- **`runCoherenceDurability(argv)`** ‚Äî the CLI handler: reads the N files, calls the core, writes the `ladderNote` (if any) to stderr, prints the report to stdout, and ‚Äî under `--fail-on-fragile-recovery` ‚Äî exits 2 when any front's relapsed recoveries average fewer than two clean rounds. A malformed/tampered/cross-ladder input is a hard exit-1 error. Requires ‚â• 2 positionals.

`tools/cli/run.ts` (the dispatcher) gains a `coherence-durability` case and a `USAGE` entry.

# Part XVI ‚Äî Principled deferrals

- **A direction-resolved magnitude (mean fall depth vs mean recovery climb).** ‚¨ú Not built. The floor is binary, so a magnitude has only one dimension ‚Äî duration; a *depth* read needs a graded sub-floor the ladder does not have.
- **A configurable "average at least K rounds" gate (`--min-mean K`).** ‚¨ú Not built. The gate fires at a mean below two rounds ‚Äî the first integer above the metric's structural minimum ‚Äî already tuning-free. The per-front means are in the JSON for a consumer wanting a different bar.
- **A median (or other quantile) instead of the mean.** ‚¨ú Not built. The mean is the integer-exact reduction (a sum over a count, gated by cross-multiplication); a median needs a sorted-position pick that is not a single integer ratio. The sorted `clean_intervals` are in the JSON for a consumer that wants one.
- **A 2-D per-front √ó per-step read.** ‚¨ú Deferred. The per-front family (level, time, recurrence, count, settling/onset, latency/relapse, dwell, churn rate, below-floor duration, and now above-floor durability) and the pairwise family are both complete on the magnitudes the binary floor admits; the next genuinely-new direction is a *matrix* read (per-front √ó per-step), not another reduction of the same intervals.
- **A standalone durability artifact (`--emit-durability`).** ‚¨ú Deferred, for the same reason v14‚Äìv40 keep the derived thing derived: the report is cheaply recomputable from the N coherence artifacts on demand.
- **A browser surface for an N-round durability synthesis.** ‚¨ú Deferred (v16‚Äìv40 Part XVI). The browser does an in-session two-round comparison; an N-artifact per-front durability read is a CI/dashboard concern.

# Part XVII ‚Äî Open questions for the maintainer

1. **Is the magnitude family now complete, both directions included?** v40 reads the below-floor mean (how long a front lingers below); v41 reads the above-floor mean (how long a fix holds before relapsing). Together they cover both magnitude directions the binary floor admits. Recommendation: **treat the per-front magnitude family as complete.** The remaining genuinely-new read is a *2-D* one (per-front √ó per-step matrix) or a *transitive* one (lead-lag chains over the pairwise family), not another reduction of the same below/above-floor episodes.
2. **Should the gate read the mean or the worst case?** Today `--fail-on-fragile-recovery` reads the **mean** (`total_rounds < 2 √ó closed_intervals`); a consumer wanting to flag a single fast relapse runs v30's `--fail-on-immediate-relapse`. Recommendation: **keep the gate on the mean** ‚Äî it is strictly stronger evidence (every fragile front trips v30's gate, not the converse), and the mean is the read no existing gate supplies.

# Part XVIII ‚Äî What this gives the user

It catches the front whose every fix is undone almost at once, which a count, an index, a rate, or an extreme structurally cannot isolate, because none of them averages the relapsed interval lengths.
- **The same five promises.** Deterministic, no AI, no server, citable, never drafts ‚Äî every line of v41 passes the ¬ß3 gate.
