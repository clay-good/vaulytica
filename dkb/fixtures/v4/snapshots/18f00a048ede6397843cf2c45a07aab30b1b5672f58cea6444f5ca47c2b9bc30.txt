Federal Rules of Civil Procedure.

Rule 37(e) Failure to Preserve Electronically Stored Information. If electronically stored information that should have been preserved in the anticipation or conduct of litigation is lost because a party failed to take reasonable steps to preserve it, and it cannot be restored or replaced through additional discovery, the court: (1) upon finding prejudice to another party from loss of the information, may order measures no greater than necessary to cure the prejudice; or (2) only upon finding that the party acted with the intent to deprive another party of the information's use in the litigation may presume that the lost information was unfavorable to the party, instruct the jury that it may or must presume the information was unfavorable to the party, or dismiss the action. Spoliation sanctions follow. Counsel should send a litigation hold.

Rule 41 Dismissal of Actions. (a) Voluntary Dismissal: by the plaintiff, by notice or stipulation. Unless the notice or stipulation states otherwise, a voluntary dismissal is without prejudice, but if the plaintiff previously dismissed any federal- or state-court action based on or including the same claim, a notice of dismissal operates as an adjudication on the merits.
