U.C.C. Article 3 — Negotiable Instruments.

§ 3-104 Negotiable Instrument. "Negotiable instrument" means an unconditional promise or order to pay a fixed amount of money, with or without interest or other charges described in the promise or order, if it (i) is payable to bearer or to order at the time it is issued or first comes into possession of a holder; (ii) is payable on demand or at a definite time; and (iii) does not state any other undertaking or instruction by the person promising or ordering payment to do any act in addition to the payment of money. A promissory note is a negotiable instrument when these requirements are satisfied; the magic words include "pay to the order of".

§ 3-305 Defenses and Claims in Recoupment. The right to enforce the obligation of a party to pay an instrument is subject to the following: real defenses (infancy, duress, lack of legal capacity, illegality of the transaction, fraud that induced the obligor to sign the instrument with neither knowledge nor reasonable opportunity to learn of its character or its essential terms, discharge in insolvency proceedings); and ordinary contract defenses unless the person enforcing the instrument is a holder in due course.
